3.126(c)
FACILITIES, INDOOR.
The overhead lights in the rear of the building are out. This is very important that the overhead lights are replaced or
repaired to allow for ample light for the animals. Light must be provided and uniformly distributed to allow for routine
inspections and cleaning. The licensee must repair or replace the overhead lights as required.

3.131(c)
SANITATION.
Behind the armadillo enclosures around the window and the walls in the rear of the building are cob webs in various
places. It is necessary to provide adequate routine removal of cob webs to facilitate the prescribed husbandry
standards. Premises (buildings and grounds) shall be kept clean and in good order to provide a clean living
environment for the various animals housed within this building. The licensee must remove all cob webs around the
window and rear walls of this building to provide a neat and clean atmosphere for the animals.
Correct by 10-7-2017
3.131(d)
SANITATION.
There are several fly strips throughout the building that are covered with dead flies. The fly strips should be routinely
replaced when their covered with flies. A safe and effective program for the control of these flies must be
established and maintained. The licensee must remove all old fly strips and replace them with new ones on a
routine basis.
