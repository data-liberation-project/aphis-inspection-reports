3.6(a)(2)(i)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
The East Building has two enclosures with areas of broken wire fencing. The affected areas are creating several
sharp points that the dogs can come in contact with. The far-right enclosure on the south wall housing two adult
Pomeranians, has broken and rusted wire fencing along the bottom edge. The outside run on the southeast corner
has wire fencing with sharp points approximately 1/2" long. The affected area is located on the left side where an
adult Pomeranian can come in contact with it. Small clumps of dog hair have been caught on some of the wire
points.
The wire fencing that separates two enclosures in the West Building, has broken and rusted wire along the bottom
edge where dogs can come in contact with it. The enclosures are located on the south wall and contain four adult
Pomeranians.
Sharp metal points or edges could create a potential safety issue and lead to injury. The licensee must repair or
replace the broken wire material and ensure that all surfaces are maintained on a regular basis.
