2.40(a)(1) REPEAT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
The licensee does not have a program of veterinary care on hand that includes non-human primates, Arctic fox,
wallabys and an African Crested porcupine.
Each exhibitor shall employ and attending veterinarian under formal arrangements. In case of a part-time attending
veterinarian, the formal arrangements shall include a written program of veterinary care and regularly scheduled
visits to the premises of the exhibitor.
A written program of veterinary care must be on the premises so that the inspector can review it and ensure that the
guidance from the veterinarian is being followed by the licensee for the health of the animals. The program of
veterinary care must have written guidance on all species maintained by the licensee.
2.78
HEALTH CERTIFICATION AND IDENTIFICATION.
The licensee acquired two ringtailed lemurs in Georgia, transported them to Michigan and does not have a health
certificate for the transportation of the non-human primates.
No exhibitor shall transport in commerce any dog, cat, or nonhuman primate unless the dog, cat, or nonhuman
primate is accompanied by a health certificate executed and issued by a licensed veterinarian.
A health certificate must be acquired for the interstate transportation of non-human primates.
