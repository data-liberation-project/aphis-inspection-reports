2.40(a)(1)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
***The licensee uses a veterinarian under consulting arrangements to provide veterinary care for the animals but
does not have a formal agreement nor a written program of veterinary care. The licensee does not have written
guidance from the attending veterinarian to provide preventative care or treatment for routine and common
veterinary problems. In order to ensure problems of animal health are addressed adequately and timely, a written
program of veterinary care should be maintained and followed.
To be corrected from this date forward.
2.75(b)(1)
RECORDS: DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS.
***The licensee did not have records that contained the required information regarding the acquisition and
deposition of animals used or co-mingled with animals used in regulated activities. Records containing the required
information for animals acquired for use in regulated activities, including those offspring born on premise, need to be
made and maintained in order to properly track the animal numbers and use in those activities.
To be corrected from this date forward.
3.131(c)
SANITATION.
*** The premises surrounding the enclosures housing regulated animals has an accumulation of building materials,
logs, wire fencing, and other materials. These materials pose a risk of injury to these animals due to sharp edges,
uneven footing, and possible ingestion. Premises (buildings and grounds) shall be kept clean and in good repair in
order to protect the animals from injury and to facilitate the prescribed husbandry practices set forth in this subpart.
Accumulations of trash shall be placed in designated areas and cleared as necessary to protect the health of the
animals.
To be corrected by 7/4/14.
Inspection and exit briefing were conducted on-site with facility representatives.
