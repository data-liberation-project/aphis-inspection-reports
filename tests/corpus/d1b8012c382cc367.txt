2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
In the refrigerator that contains medication for the dogs there is a bottle of Penicillian that expired in 2007, a bottle of
Oxytocin that expired in 2013 and a bottle of Tresaderm that expired in 2009. Expired medications may lose their
effectiveness causing the health of the dogs to suffer. The licensee must either dispose of all expired medications
or segregate them in an appropriately labeled, physically separate location from non-expired medical materials.
Correction date: April 2, 2015
In the sheltered housing facility there is a male Maltese, USDA tag #35, that has an excessively matted hair coat.
The matting is extensive and pulled tightly to the skin over the dog'(cid:25)s back, legs, feet and head. The matting around
the feet, legs and head has become entangled with some dirt and debris. Excessively matted hair coats can be
painful to the dog. Licensee must provide the dog with hair coat maintenance and establish and maintain a hair
coat maintenance plan to ensure all dogs stay free of excessive matting.
