3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
1. The following items pertaining to primary enclosures were identified that are in need of repair.

**The tarp covering the chain link roof of the outdoor primary enclosure for 3 raccoons had been damaged by the
animals and there was a torn area approximately 18 x 18 inches in size. The damaged section of tarp had strands
of plastic (cid:28)"fringe"(cid:29) approximately 6-8 inches(cid:28) in length hanging down into the enclosure that pose a foreign body risk
should the plastic strands be ingested by the raccoons.
**The pieces of carpet covering the perches on the "cat tree" climbing structure in the outdoor primary enclosure for
2 raccoons have been damaged by the animals. Most of the carpet on the perches is very frayed and there are
fibrous threads hanging from the carpet that pose a foreign body risk should the fibers be ingested by the raccoons.
**One of the plush hammocks hanging from the top of the indoor primary enclosure for 1 kinkajou had been
damaged by the animal. The fabric covering of the hammock was torn with some of the stuffing material sticking
out that poses a foreign body risk should the stuffing material be ingested by the kinkajou.
Per this Section of the Regulations, indoor and outdoor housing facilities for animals should be maintained in good
repair to protect the animals from injury.

The licensee needs to repair or replace the items noted above for the safety and health of the animals.
Correct by 5/2/16.
