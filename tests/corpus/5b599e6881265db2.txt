3.1(f)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
Drainage and Waste Disposal.
An outdoor enclosure housing two Boston terriers had standing water at the front of the pen as the result of rain the
previous day. Improper drainage can result in wet dogs, odors, insects, and disease hazards The licensee must
ensure that enclosures are constructed so that water is rapidly eliminated so animals stay dry and disease hazards
are reduced.
TO BE CORRECTED BY APRIL 8, 2016.
3.4(b)
OUTDOOR HOUSING FACILITIES.
Shelter from the elements.
Two outdoor enclosures housing a total of 4 dogs did not provide shade in addition to the shelter structures. A lack
of shade can cause dogs to become overheated. The licensee must ensure that each outdoor enclosure provides
one or more areas of shade, large enough to contain all the animals in the enclosure at one time.
TO BE CORRECTED BY MARCH 15, 2016.
3.6(a)(2)(10) DIRECT
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
The feet and legs of 5-day-old puppies were observed to fall through the openings of the mesh flooring in their
enclosure in the whelping building. This situation can result in entrapment or injury to the puppies. The licensee
must ensure that flooring is constructed in a manner to protect dogs' feet and legs from injury.
TO BE CORRECTED ON MARCH 8, 2016.
3.6(c)(1)(3)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.An adult miniature Australian Shepherd named Sapphire was housed in the whelping building in an enclosure that
only allowed about 2 inches of space between the top of the dog's head and the top of the enclosure. Inadequate
head space does not allow dogs to make natural postural movements. The licensee must ensure that all dogs have
at least 6 inches of head space.
TO BE CORRECTED BY MARCH 10, 2016.
The inspection and the exit briefing were conducted with the licensee and with Alicia Graves, inspector for the MO
Dept. of Agriculture.
