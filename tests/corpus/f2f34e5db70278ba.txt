3.131(c)
SANITATION.
The rear fenced in farm animal pasture area behind the barn has an excessive amount of overgrown weeds along
the bottom of the fence. Although, there was no signs of rodent holes, weeds and overgrown grass is an area for
rodents to hide. This area must be kept up to prevent overgrown weeds to control rodents and to protect the health
and well-being of the animals. The inside of the barn area were the animals are housed and the feed and bedding is
kept is in disarray. Noted this inspection scattered around are empty feed bags, trash cans full without lids, latters,
old wood from previous projects, bail twine, bedding all over the floor of the barn and an old spare tire. Premises
(buildings and grounds) shall be kept clean in order to protect the animals from injury, rodents and to facilitate
prescribed husbandry standards. The licensee shall place accumulations of trash in a designated area and trim all
weeds around the pasture fence to protect the health of the animals.
Correct by 6-17-2017
(The inspection and exit interview was conducted with the licensee)
