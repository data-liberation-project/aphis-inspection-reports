3.125(a) Critical Repeat

Facilities, general.
-One of the 13 prairie dogs escaped the primary enclosure. The prairie dogs made mounds of dirt near the top of the
enclosure and one prairie dog escaped.
-The bobcat named Fred has two broken boards that make up the floor of the enclosure. One of the boards has a screw
sticking out of it. There is also a whole in the hardware cloth that leads to the bobcat's shelter.
-The goat walk is in disrepair. There are numerous slat boards missing, and exposed screw heads.
-There are two areas of the primary enclosure for the fox where the fencing is not affixed to the framing of the enclosure.
-The caracal enclosure has a board that is rotted at the bottom in the tunnel to the shelter for the caracal.
-The cavy enclosure has 1 board that the cavy have chewed which reduces the structural strength of the wall between
the cavy and muntjac.

The indoor and outdoor housing facilities shall be structurally sound and shall be maintained in good repair to protect the
animals from injury and to contain the animals.

Outdoor housing facilities must be constructed to contain the animals. The facility has added a clear plastic kick in on the

prairie dog enclosure. The prairie dog is still on the grounds, but has not been captured. The bobcat, goat, fox, caracal,
and muntjac enclosures must be maintained in good repair to protect animals from injury and to contain the animals.
3.127(b)

Facilities, outdoor.
-The shelter for the primary enclosure for 9 Herford cattle is not large enough. The day of inspection the temperature was
in the mid 40's F and it was pouring rain. Only 4 of the cattle fit inside the enclosure.
-The shelter in the primary enclosure for the 4 Kunekune pigs is wet, with wet straw in it. There is a road used by the farm
equipment behind the shelter and rain has overflowed the road into the pig shelter.

Natural or artificial shelter appropriate to the local climatic conditions for the species concerned shall be provided for all
animals kept outdoors to afford them protection and to prevent discomfort to such animals.

Additional shelter for the cattle must be provided to protect them from the weather and provide them comfort.

To be corrected by October 29, 2021
3.127(c)

Facilities, outdoor.
The primary enclosures for the Herford cattle, and zebu and alpaca have excessive mud in the area of the food and
water.

A suitable method shall be provided to rapidly eliminate excess water.


A method to rapidly eliminate excess water must be established, or the food and water moved to an area to allow dry
access to food and water.

To be corrected by October 29, 2021.
3.129(b)

Feeding.
There is excessive feces in the goat trough. The goat trough also contains a mineral block that has feces on it.

Food receptacles shall be kept clean and sanitary at all times.

Ensure that these troughs are kept clean and sanitary so that the food and supplements fed to the goats are free from
contamination by feces.

To be corrected by end of day October 26, 2021
3.130

Watering.
The water receptacles for the caracal, and the 2 bobcats have dark brown/black organic material growing on them. The
capybara is housed with geese, and the geese have made the water for the capybara brown with mud.

All water receptacles shall be kept clean and sanitary.

Clean all water receptacles as often as necessary to ensure that the animals have access to potable water.


To be corrected by end of business October 26, 2021
3.131(a) Repeat

Sanitation.
There is excessive feces in the shelter for the water buffalo, cow and goats, the primary enclosure for the ferrets, and on
the goat walk.

Excreta shall be removed from primary enclosures as often as necessary to prevent contamination of the animals
contained therein and to minimize disease hazards and to reduce odors.

Remove feces from primary enclosures and shelters as often as necessary to prevent contamination of the animals and
minimize disease hazards.
3.132

Employees.
There is only one full-time employee and several part-time employees completing daily animals care at this facility. Due
to the number of, and Repeat routine husbandry citations this number of employees has not proven to be sufficient to
maintain normal husbandry standards for a collection of nearly 200 animals.

A sufficient number of adequately trained employees shall be utilized to maintain the professionally acceptable level of
husbandry practices set forth in this subpart.

A sufficient number of employees must be utilized to maintain an acceptable level of husbandry for the animals housed at

the facility.

To be corrected by November 26, 2021.
