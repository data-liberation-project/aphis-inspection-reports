3.125(d)

Facilities, general.
Food wastes and discarded disposable food receptacles need to be removed from the enclosure housing the breeding
pair of coatimundis. Just inside the door there is a pile of several days worth of dry pelleted feed covered with yogurt. In
the back corner of the enclosure is a pile of discarded used disposable feed receptacles. Food wastes will attract pests
and can become contaminated possibly causing digestive issues if eaten. The facility shall provide for the removal of food
wastes and debris from the enclosure. Correct by 6/24/21.
3.127(d)

Facilities, outdoor.
The facility currently houses numerous exotic/wild species outdoors including coatimundi, wallaby, cavy and kangaroo.
The facility has a perimeter fence in place but it measures less than six feet in height (it is approximately four feet in
height). A perimeter fence is necessary to keep out unwanted animals and people and to act as a secondary containment
system for the animals in the facility. Perimeter fences, for the species housed at the facility, less than 6 feet high, must
be approved in writing by the Administrator. The perimeter fence must be six feet in height or must be approved in writing.
Correct by 8/22/21.
3.130

Watering.


Multiple water receptacles including in the wallaby, coatimundi, and sheep/goat enclosures are in need of cleaning. Plant
material was noted floating on the surface of the water. Green algae and or mud were present on the interior sides and
bottoms of the water receptacles. The water in receptacles that are not kept clean may become contaminated or the
animals may not wish to drink the water. All water receptacles shall be kept clean and sanitary. Correct by 6/23/21.
