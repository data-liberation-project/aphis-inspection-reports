3.127(d)
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
The licensee has some temporary animals on his property. These animals were brought to him by an unlicensed
individual. One of the animals is a 1 year old bobcat. The bobcat does not have a perimeter fence around its
primary enclosure.
Non-dangerous animals are required to have a perimeter fence a minimum of 6' tall.
A perimeter fence must be erected around the bobcat enclosure to prevent unauthorized people from coming in
contact with the bobcat and to act as a secondary containment system in case of escape from the primary
enclosure. The licensee lives approximately 100 yards from an interstate.
To be corrected by September 26, 2019.
3.130
WATERING.
The licensee has an individual that has brought 3 fox to his facility. Two of the fox are kept within the licensees
perimeter fence and have a large amount of algae growing in their water dish.
All water receptacles shall be kept clean and sanitary.
All water receptacles must be cleaned and sanitized as often as necessary to prevent an overgrowth of algae and to
provide clean potable water to the animals in the enclosure.
To be corrected from this day forward.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with licensee.
