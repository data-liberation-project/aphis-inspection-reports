2.40(b)(2) REPEAT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
An adult, male Chocolate Labrador (microchip #146563591A) has a buildup of thick tarter covering the surface of
the cheek teeth on both sides. The gums are moderately reddened and a creamy gray discharge is visualized along
the gum line above the left cheek teeth. Poor dental care can be painful, cause discomfort and lead to additional
health problems.
An adult, white and brown, female Olde Bulldog (tattoo #353) has an abnormal growth approximately one half inch
in diameter between the toes on its right front foot. The affected area is reddened and firm to the touch.
An adult, white and brown, female Olde Bulldog (tattoo #1341) has a problem with its left eye. The dog is squinting,
rubbing its eye on the front of the enclosure and pawing at it. The inside lining of the eye is swollen and has a
reddened appearance. Hair surrounding the eye is wet.
An adult, red and white, female Cavalier Spaniel (tattoo #43) has abnormal hair loss around the base of its tail and
extending the entire length of the tail. The skin on the tail was thickened and reddened. The skin around the base
of the tail also is dry and scaly and has a thickened and slightly reddened appearance.
An adult, white, female Shiba Inu (tattoo #68) has a swollen and reddened sore between its toes on its left back
foot. The affected area has moderate hair loss with a dark red spot in the center.
An adult, black and white, male Olde Bulldog (tattoo #3) has swollen sores between its toes on its right front foot,
right back foot and left back foot. The sores are moist and have a reddened appearance and each contains a dark
red spot in the center. The affected areas also have moderate hair loss.
Conditions such as these can be painful and can lead to further health complications. The animals listed above
must be examined by a licensed veterinarian in order to ensure that an accurate diagnosis is obtained and an
appropriate treatment plan is developed and followed. This information, including the diagnosis, treatment and
resolution of the condition, must be documented and made available to the inspector upon request. The licensee
must ensure that all animals receive appropriate veterinary care at all times.This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the facility representatives, an IDALS representative, Corbin
Ranslem, ACI, Kelly Maxwell, ACI and Ann Goplen, VMO.
