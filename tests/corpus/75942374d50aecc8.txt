2.40(b)(2) DIRECT REPEAT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
Adult, male Chihuahua with chip number 016 504 531 has a buildup of brown material (calculus) on his remaining
cheek teeth. The right upper cheek tooth is loose and has a creamy white material along the reddened gum line.
The left upper cheek tooth is also loose. These symptoms are consistent with dental disease. Dental disease can
cause damage to the gum tissue and tooth and mouth structures and is painful. It can impact the ability of the dog to
eat normally, affecting the overall health of the animal. The animal listed above must be examined by a licensed
veterinarian in order to ensure that an accurate diagnosis is obtained and an appropriate treatment plan is
developed and followed. This information, including the diagnosis, treatment and resolution of the condition, must
be documented and made available to the inspector upon request.
Adult, female Shih Tzu with chip number OAO2366961 has matted fur. The entire back, top of the rump, and all four
limbs have mats that are tight against the skin. Matted fur can lead to skin infections and is painful. In addition,
matted fur does not provide complete protection against the elements. The licensee must remove the matted fur of
this dog. As part of the facility's program of veterinary care, the licensee must develop and follow a program of coat
maintenance at whatever frequency necessary to ensure that matted fur is removed on a regular basis.
Adult, male Chihuahua with chip number OAO1662307 has overgrown toe nails on both front feet. The nails are
starting to curl outward as they contact the ground. Long toenails can interfere with the dog'(cid:25)s gait and can be
uncomfortable. The licensee must trim this dog(cid:25)'s toenails. As a part of the facility'(cid:25)s program of veterinary care, the
licensee must develop and follow a program of toenail maintenance at to ensure that toenails are kept at a
comfortable length.

3.9(b) REPEAT
FEEDING.
There are six plastic food receptacles in the indoor housing area with chewed edges that are deeply pitted and
trapping dirt. The chewed edges of the food receptacles show decrease in durability and are difficult to clean and
sanitize. When surfaces are not sanitized they can play a role in disease transmission. When food receptacles
become worn or chewed to the point that they cannot be sanitized in accordance with 3.11 (b), they must be
replaced. Licensee must provide durable food receptacles without chewed edges that are easily cleaned and
sanitized.
