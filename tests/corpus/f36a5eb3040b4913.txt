3.1(c)(3)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
Several primary enclosures in the small breed dog kennel had animal contact surfaces that were dirty, including
whelping boxes, kennel walls, and doggy doors. Several kennel walls and doggy doors were discolored with a
brown substance. Several whelping boxes contained carpet squares that were frayed, stained, and severely worn.
Several primary enclosures in the large breed dog kennel had animal contact surfaces that were dirty, including
kennel walls and doggy doors. Several kennel walls and doggy doors in the large breed dog kennel were
discolored with a brown substance and an accumulation of hair. There was an accumulation of dog hair and feces in
a corner of one outdoor run.
Cleaning of dog kennels is important for providing a clean environment and minimizing disease hazards. Surfaces
that cannot be cleaned (such as carpets) should be discarded when worn or dirty. Correct by cleaning kennels and
maintaining kennels in a clean manner. Correct by: September 18th, 2019.
3.11(c)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
There was evidence of pests in the large breed dog kennel. There was a large amount of rodent feces in the area
where dog food is stored. The lid on one of the dog food storage containers was not closed. There was dog food on
the floor next to this food storage container. There was rodent feces in the walkway in front of the kennel doors.
Pests, such as rodents, can contaminate dog food and spread disease. An effective program for control of pests
must be established and maintained to promote the health of the dogs and reduce contamination by pests. Correct
by: October 9th, 2019.
