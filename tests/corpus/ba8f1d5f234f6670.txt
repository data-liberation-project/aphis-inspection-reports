3.6(a)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
The buried welded-wire fencing has become uncovered in a section of one dog enclosure resulting in exposure of
the fence openings and a gap between the fencing and the ground. Dogs may become entangled in these
openings causing injury to the dogs.
A wooden panel along the outdoor portion of the whelping building has come loose resulting in a gap between it and
the wall as well three exposed nail heads. The nail heads may cause injury to the dogs.
All surfaces must be kept in good repair so dogs are protected from injury and surfaces are maintained. Correct by
5 July 2014.
3.9(b)
FEEDING.
Food is being fed in open food receptacles. Because of the overnight rain, the food in the food receptacles has
become rain-soaked. Food that is rain-soaked may become contaminated and unpalatable. Food receptacles must
be located to protect the food from rain. Correct by 12 June 2014.
3.11
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
There is a multiple-day accumulation of feces located inside the dog shelter and on the ground of one whelping
enclosure. Excessive accumulation of feces may lead to contamination of the dogs and an increase in disease
transmission. Feces must be removed from primary enclosures on a daily basis. Correct by 6 June 2014.This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the licensee.
