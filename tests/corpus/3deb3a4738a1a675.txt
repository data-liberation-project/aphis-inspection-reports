2.75(a)(2)

Records: Dealers and exhibitors.
The sheltered facility with 36 adults and 16 puppies is not using APHIS Form 7005 to record acquisitions and track animal
inventory.
The use of APHIS Form 7005 is required by the Animal Welfare Act and aids in the uniform identification of animals and
tracking inventory.
The facility needs to transfer all records of animals on hand to APHIS Form 7005.
3.1(e)

Housing facilities, general.
The facility has two palates of bedding material stored in a barn that also is used to stored hay and other materials. There
is small pile of loose hay built up around one side of one palate.
Piles of loose hay can shelter mice and other vermin that may then infest the bedding. Supplies of bedding material must
be stored in a manner that protects them from contamination and vermin infestation.
The area around the palates of bedding must be cleaned and cleared of all hay and other debris.
3.9(b)

Feeding

Three feeders for approximately 7 dogs have an excessive build up of brown colored organic material in openings where
the dogs eat that is in direct contact with their food. The owner says he cleans the feeders once a month.
Dirty feeders lead to a build up materials that can shelter bacteria, contaminate food, and cause illness. Animal Welfare
Act (AWA) regulations states self-feeders must be kept clean and sanitized at least once every two weeks.
Dirty feeders must be cleaned or replaced and all feeders sanitized using a method and frequency that meets the
requirements of the AWA.
