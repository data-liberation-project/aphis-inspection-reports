2.131(c)(1)
HANDLING OF ANIMALS.
There is no barrier around the red kangaroo enclosure.
During public exhibition, any animal must be handled so there is minimal risk of harm to the animal and to the
public, with sufficient distance and/or barriers between the animal and the general viewing public so as to assure
the safety of animals and the public.
An appropriate barrier must be used to prevent harm to the kangaroos and the general public.
To be corrected by May 12, 2016.
3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The door that allows access to the outside enclosure has small hardware cloth on it and it has sharp points and is in
disrepair. There is also an area of the outside enclosure that has sharp points and is in disrepair.
The indoor and outdoor housing facilities shall be structurally sound and shall be maintained in good repair to
protect the animals from injury and to contain the animals.
Repair or replace the two areas of fencing so that they do not cause injury to the porcupine.
To be corrected by May 12, 2016.
Inspection and exit conducted with the licensee.
