3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
3 ceiling press board panels in the zebra indoor shelter area are unstable. They are warped and sagging in the
middle and around the corners. These ceiling panels must constructed of such material and of such strength as
appropriate for the animals. They must protect the animals from injury in case these wooden press board panels
were to fall in on the zebras. The licensee must repair or replace the 3 unsafe warped wooden ceiling panels to
protect the animals from injury.
Correct by 8-6-2017
3.131(a)
SANITATION.
The inside zebra stall area has accumulations of manure and bedding that is building up. The inside stall area has
layers of impacted bedding, manure and debris. Excreta and old bedding must be removed as often as necessary to
prevent contamination of the animals contained therein. The licensee must remove all old manure, impacted
bedding and continue this process on a regular basis to protect the animals and perform adequate husbandry.
Correct by 8-6-2017
3.131(b)
SANITATION.
The metal indoor walls for the kangaroo housing need to be washed with soap or detergent. The metal walls that
cover the inside of the barn are covered in dirt, cob webs and debris. All soiled surfaces must be scrubbed down
with a detergent solution followed by a safe and effective disinfectant as directed by the attending veterinarian to
minimize diseases. The licensee shall thoroughly clean and disinfect all soiled surfaces for the kangaroo indoor
housing.
Correct by 8-6-2017
