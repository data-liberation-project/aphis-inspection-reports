3.1(c)(2) REPEAT
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
* The entrance edges to two fiberglass shelter structures are chewed exposing a jagged, roughened surface. The
shelter structures used by two dogs are located in outdoor housing. Surfaces not maintained on a regular basis
cannot be readily cleaned and sanitized. A chewed, roughened surface could harbor disease causing agents which
affects the health and well-being of the dogs. The potential exists for dogs to ingest chewed plastic which could
cause potential digestion problems. The licensee must ensure that all surfaces can be readily cleaned and
sanitized and be free of jagged edges or sharp points for the safety of the dogs.
3.10
WATERING.
* Two metal water receptacles used by two dogs have a brownish looking deposit collecting on an estimated 80 to
90 percent of the interior wall of these receptacles. The receptacles are located in shelter enclosures. It indicates
the water receptacles have not been adequately cleaned and sanitized. It could lead to water contamination
affecting the health and well-being of the dogs. The licensee must ensure all water receptacles are kept clean and
sanitized. Correct by 3/24/14.
* The entire top of one water receptacle is chewed and has a roughened, jagged edge. The receptacle used by
one dog is located on primary enclosure flooring in indoor housing. A chewed, roughened, jagged surface cannot
be easily cleaned and sanitized and could potentially cause injury to the dog. In addition, it could harbor bacteria
and other disease causing agents that can affect the health and wellbeing of the dogs. The licensee must ensure
that all receptacles can be properly cleaned and sanitized at all times. Correct by 3/24/14.
3.11(c)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
* Cobwebs and dust were observed collecting on the west interior wall and on top of three metal wire panels.
Three dogs are in contact with the affected areas which are located in sheltered housing. It indicates unsanitary
living conditions exist that could impact the health and well-being of the dogs. It indicates a potential living area
exists for other pests. The licensee must ensure all areas of the facility are kept clean to facilitate normal husbandrypractices. Correct by 3/31/14.
* The facility inspection done on 3/17/14 was conducted with facility representative.
* The exit interview done on 3/19/14 was conducted with the licensee.
