3.131(c)
SANITATION.
-Several stacks of empty wire cages were stored next to the llama enclosure. Desert brush/weeds were growing
around and in between the cages.
- Miscellaneous tools/equipment, a barrel, and several buckets were openly stored directly next to or leaning against
a bobcat enclosure.
-There were several bundles or layers of sticks and other dried, plant material, a ladder and tarp-like materials lying
on the ground along a short, wooden fence which is next to a leopard enclosure.
-Food storage/supplies room: There is a general accumulation of clutter in this room with miscellaneous animal
supplies, cleaning materials and other animal items stored in an untidy and unorganized manner. Along one wall in
this room were several bags (some opened) of dog and cat food and boxes/bags of cat litter which were disorderly,
stacked several feet high and directly on the floor. Licensee stated this dog/cat food and cat litter are collected for
donation and not used for any of the covered animals at this facility.
Premises including buildings, surrounding grounds and other animal areas must be kept clean, free of clutter and in
good repair in order to protect the health and well-being of the animals, to prevent the harborage of vermin and
pests, and to facilitate the prescribed husbandry practices. A system of maintaining effective housekeeping
practices must be in place.
Correct by 11/25/2016.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with a facility representative.
