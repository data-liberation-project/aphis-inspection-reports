2.75(b)(1)
RECORDS: DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS.
A current record of animals on hand was not available for review during this inspection. Records are required in
order to adequately verify the total number of animals housed at the facility and for evaluating the facility animal
care program. Correct by ensuring there are current records for animal totals, including births and deaths,
maintained and available for review for the guinea pigs housed at the facility. Correct by June 13, 2017.
3.25(c)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
Storage. There is a bag of feed on the floor in the lower storage room next to the wall and an open bag of feed on
top of the unopened feed. Empty food bags and clutter are lying on the floor next to the feed supplies. Supplies of
food and bedding shall be stored in facilities which adequately protect such supplies against spoilage, deterioration,
infestation or contamination by vermin. Unopened bags of feed must be stored off the floor and away from walls to
allow cleaning around and under stored supplies. Open food supplies must be stored in containers with tightly
fitting lids to protect supplies from spoilage, contamination, and pests. Correct by ensuring the feed is stacked off
the floor and open feed supplies are kept in containers with lids. Correct by June 13, 2017.
3.31(b)
SANITATION.
Housekeeping. There is a long work table in the animal room along one wall filled with miscellaneous supplies,
lumber, tools, disinfectant, oil, dust, and debris. Lumber, wood scraps, wire, dust, and debris are stored on the floor
below the work table. Animal areas inside of housing facilities must be kept neat and free of clutter, but may contain
items necessary for the cleaning and care of the guinea pigs. Animal facilities must be kept clean to ensure good
quality air for the animals to prevent unnecessary disease risks. Correct by ensuring the premises are kept clean,
including removing accumulations of dust, lumber, tools, chemicals, and clutter from the top of the work table and
items stored on the floor below the work table, and maintaining a level of cleanliness to support good animal
husbandry and health. Correct by June 27, 2017.This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the owner's wife.
