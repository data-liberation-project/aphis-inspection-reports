2.75(b)(1)
RECORDS: DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS.
Records of acquisition and disposition of animals could not be located at time of inspection.
Acquisition and disposition records are necessary to be able to accurately identify and track animals being used in
regulated activities, and aid in biosecurity surveillance to trace disease transmission. The licensee shall make, keep,
and maintain records of acquisition and disposition of all animals that fully and correctly discloses the required
information.
Correct by Dec. 5, 2019.
2.75(b)(2)
RECORDS: DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS.
Inventory could not be located at time of inspection. There were no records which documented the animals on hand.
Exhibitors must make, keep, and maintain records which fully and correctly disclose information concerning animals
purchased or otherwise acquired, owned, held, leased, or otherwise in his or her possession or under his or her
control, or which is transported, sold, euthanized, or otherwise disposed of by that dealer or exhibitor.
Correct by Dec. 5, 2019.
3.55
WATERING.
An outdoor enclosure housing 2 rabbits had a water bowl that was empty and dirty. Upon licensee filling water
bowl, the rabbits drank in a manner that indicated they were thirsty.
Sufficient potable water shall be provided rabbits daily. All watering receptacles shall be sanitized when dirty.
Correct from this time forward.3.130
WATERING.
An enclosure housing 3 Coatis had a bucket for water that was empty and hanging attached to the side of the wire
enclosure. Upon the licensee filling the water bucket all three coatis drank in a manner that displayed their thirst.
Containers need to be heavy or large enough so they are not easily tipped over and drained.
If potable water is not accessible to the animals at all times, it must be provided as often as necessary for the health
and comfort of the animal. Frequency of watering shall consider age, species, condition, size, type and number of
animals. All water receptacles shall be kept clean and sanitary.
Corrected at time of inspection.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the licensee.
