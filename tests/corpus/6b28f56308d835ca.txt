2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
An adult male Yorkie USDA tag number 052 was found during inspection to have matted and grimy hair. This male
had not be adequately groomed to promote the health and wellbeing of the animal.
An adult female Bichon, USDA tag number 188, was found to have approximately three inches of hair missing from
the base of the tail. There was a superficial lick lesion the size of a small pencil eraser on the tail near the center of
the hair loss. The licensee(cid:25)s son had indicated that he had noticed the missing hair but hadn(cid:25)t treated the animal
yet. The current program of veterinary care provides instructions to the licensee for treatment alopecia/hair loss and
hotspots/skin problems.
Proper grooming practices for these animals is needed to ensure that the animal remains in good health. The
program of veterinary care provides instructions as to the proper care of the animals and must be followed to ensure
that adequate care is given to the animals in a timely manner.
The licensee shall use appropriate methods to prevent, control, diagnose and treat diseases and injuries.
Correct by treating the Bichon according to the instructions in the program of veterinary care and by grooming the
Yorkie male by October 3, 2015.
The exit interview was conducted with the licensee, Randy Coleman ACI and Michael Neafsey, VMO.
