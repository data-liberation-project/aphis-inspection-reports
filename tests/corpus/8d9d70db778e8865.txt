3.4(c) REPEAT
OUTDOOR HOUSING FACILITIES.
The kennel facility consists of two outdoor housing areas. In the outdoor structure there are a total of twelve
primary enclosures. The interior wooden surfaces of all of the enclosures are chewed and worn. The chewed /
worn wooden surfaces need to be repaired or replaced and maintained on a regular basis. Surfaces of outdoor
housing facilities--including houses, dens, etc.--that cannot be readily cleaned and sanitized, must be replaced
when worn or soiled to facilitate husbandry pratices, prevent the spread of disease and protect the animals from
injury.
Affects Thirty-three adults.
3.11(a)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
The kennel facility consists of two outdoor housing area. There are a total of thirteen primary enclosures. The
outdoor floor surface of the enclosures are concrete. During the inspection the enclosures contained more than one
days accumulation of fresh and old dry waste material. Excreta must be removed from primary enclosures daily to
prevent an excessive accumulation of feces, to prevent soiling of the dogs contained in the primary enclosures, and
to reduce disease hazards, insects, pests and odors.
Affects thirty-three adults. Correct by May 6, 2015
