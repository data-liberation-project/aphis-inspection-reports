2.40(a)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
Section 2.40(a)(2) Veterinary care: The facility written program of veterinary care includes vaccinations for the two
opossums and one skunk, and annual fecal tests for these same animals and the four rabbits. The office for the
attending veterinarian was called during the inspection and all the animals are more than a year overdue for their
preventative care, except the skunk has had a recent fecal. The attending veterinarian must have the appropriate
authority to ensure the provisions of adequate veterinary care, which includes the facility following the written
program of veterinary care from the attending veterinarian to maintain the health of the animals. Correct by having
the attending veterinarian perform the preventative care for the animals. Correct by April 4, 2017.
3.56(a)(1)
SANITATION.
Section 3.56(a)(1) Cleaning of primary enclosures: There is an excessive amount of feces in the bedding in the
back of the shelter boxes for the three outdoor hutches, each housing one rabbit. Fecal material may harbor
parasites and bacteria, and be a health risk for the animals. The shelter boxes must be cleaned more frequently to
keep the animals free of fecal contamination and for the health of the animals. Correct by cleaning the shelter
boxes and keeping them clean. Correct by March 22, 2017.
This inspection was conducted with the facility naturalist and the exit interview were conducted with the facility
senior naturalist.
