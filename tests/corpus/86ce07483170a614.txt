2.40(b)(2) DIRECT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
There were 3 pugs and 1 boxer and 1 shiatsu that had growths on their eyes, or ulcer-like malformations. A Pug(M)
black/fawn "King" had a cloudy left eye with that appeared misshaped, and possibly the start of an issue with the
right eye. His teeth were very poor with red gums and excessive tarter, brown in color covering the molars and
canine with some gray material at the gumline. A Pug(F) fawn "#3599" had an ulcer like formation on her left eye in
the center of the eyeball, with the beginnings of another possibly in her right eye. A Pug(M) fawn "#8682" had a
growth in the center of his right eye and cloudy in appearance. Both eyes were extremely 'bug-eyed' in appearance
and appeared misshapen. A male boxer "Tommy", brown/white, had a green discharge from both eyes, with the
majority coming from his left eye that had a small white ring in the center of the eye, a possible growth or ulceration.
A shiatsu(F) brindle "#30" had a brown crusty material in the corners of both eyes tangled in the hair with increased
drainage and white cloudiness in both eyes and possible sore in the center of the left eye. The veterinarian had not
been contacted in regards to these health issues, as they had not been observed at this time.
It Is imperative that all eye drainage, cloudiness, bulging or ulcerations and growths be identified and contact made
with the veterinarian for immediate diagnosis and treatment in order to prevent a treatable condition from becoming
chronic and to decrease the amount of pain and/or discomfort of the animal.
The licensee must contact the veterinarian for proper diagnosis and treatment of any health issues with the
regulated animals.
To be corrected by: February 15, 2014
2.40(b)(3)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
There were 5 dogs with eye issues and 1 pug with bad teeth that had not been identified. A male pug "king", female
pug '#3599", a male pug " #8682", a male boxer "Tommy" and a female shiatsu female "#30" all had eye drainage
issues with various cloudy formations, ulceration like sores or growths in the centers of their eyes that were not
observed and no contact had been made to the veterinarian for diagnosis or treatment.It is important that all regulated animals are observed on a daily basis and that the attending veterinarian is
contacted when any illnesses or injuries are observed in order to diagnose and treat any health issues in a timely
manner to decrease any amount of pain or discomfort to the animals.
The licensee must communicate any health issues observed to the veterinarian for proper diagnosis and treatment,
and daily observations must be conducted on all regulated animals in order to prevent any health issues from
progressing causing pain or discomfort.
