3.75(c)(1)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The spider monkey enclosure and the Lemur enclosures had algae accumulation and the toys were soiled with dirt
and algae.The horizontal pole at the bottom of the spider monkey enclosure where the chain-link attaches was
rusted and flaky making it difficult to clean and sanitize.
The surfaces of housing facilities must be constructed in a manner and made of materials that allow them to be
readily cleaned and sanitized, or removed or replaced when worn or soiled. Any surfaces that come in contact with
nonhuman primates must be free of excessive rust that prevents the required cleaning and sanitization, or that
affects the structural strength of the enclosure.
To be corrected by 10-20-2016
3.125(a) REPEAT
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The shelter for the Puma had rotten wood on the side which needs to be replaced.
The indoor and outdoor housing facilities shall be structurally sound and shall be maintained in good repair.
3.127
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
The tiger enclosure had a tarp that was blown away by a storm leaving the animals without adequate protection
from the elements of nature (rain, sunlight, etc).
In order to prevent discomfort to the animals by elements of nature, natural or artificial shelter appropriate to the
local climatic conditions for the species concerned shall be provided.
To be corrected by 10-20-2016.
