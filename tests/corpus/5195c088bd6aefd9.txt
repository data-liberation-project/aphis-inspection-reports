2.40(b)(2) DIRECT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
A cream colored female French Bulldog, ID# U108, "Wild Thing", has a greenish/whitish mucus covering both eyes.
The mucus has also collected along the bottom of both eyelids and the hair surrounding the bottom of the eyes has
become wet. The licensee stated that she had not consulted a veterinarian about this dog's eyes.

A female Yorkshire Terrier, ID#U098, "Abbey", was observed to have severe matting on her front and hind legs.
The hair had become matted into clumps and balls that are adhered tightly to the skin. The toenails of this dog
have become excessively overgrown and are curving under. This dog also has a tan/grey colored substance
covering all the cheek teeth and the majority of the upper canine teeth. Her gums have become slightly reddened.

A male poodle, ID#U104, "Chipper", was observed to have a lower jaw that was hanging slightly to the right. Closer
inspection of this dog's mouth revealed a tan/grey colored substance covering all the cheek teeth and the majority
of the surface of both the upper and lower canine teeth. The gums were also moderately reddened. While
handling the dog's mouth, the inspectors noticed that the dog had a broken tooth on the lower, right front side of his
mouth and this tooth easily moved away from the other teeth. The licensee said that she had just recently tried to
clean this dog's teeth with her finger and finger nails.
A female Maltese puppy, DOB 12-18-15, dam "Paige", has a right eye that would not open. Closer inspection of the
puppy revealed that the eye was sealed tightly shut and a crusty, brownish substance had formed over the eye.
The licensee used warm water to open the eye during the inspection.
A male red poodle, ID#U171,"Rusty", was observed to have a sunken flank area beneath his hair coat. The dog
was removed from his enclosure and physical examination revealed that the animal(cid:25)s shoulder blades, hip bones,
ribs and vertebrae could be easily felt with little fat covering the animal. The left eye has a large clump of a dark
brown hard material in the front corner. There was a small amount of discharge from the left nasal opening. The
dog also has a buildup of a dark tan/grey substance covering the majority of the front left canine tooth and there is a
whitish cream colored substance along the gum line. The gums are moderately reddened. The licensee stated
that she had been feeding this dog dry and canned puppy food two times a day but had not consulted with a
veterinarian about the dog's physical condition.A male chocolate poodle, ID#U174, "Hershey", has a buildup of a tan/grey material on his right upper canine tooth.
There is a whitish cream colored substance along the gum line. The gums appeared moderately reddened.
A female French Bulldog, ID#U112, "Ellie", has a small reddened and swollen area between the toes of the front left
foot and the toes of the left hind foot. The licensee had been treating these areas with Vetericyn spray but had not
consulted with a licensed veterinarian about this dog's condition.
Poor body condition can be a sign of underlying health problems. Poor eye and dental health can cause pain and
discomfort and can lead to further health complications. Matted hair and overgrown toenails can also cause pain
and discomfort and further health problems.
The licensee must provide female Yorkshire Terrier, ID#U098, "Abbey" with hair coat maintenance and trim this
animal(cid:25)'s toenails. The licensee must develop a plan for hair coat maintenance and ensure that all dogs receive
adequate hair coat and toenail care at all times.
The licensee must have all the above mentioned dogs evaluated by a licensed veterinarian for an accurate
diagnosis and treatment plan. The diagnosis and treatment plan must be documented and available for the
inspectors upon request. The licensee must ensure that all dogs receive adequate veterinary care at all times.

Correction date: End of business on January 15, 2016
A female white Maltese, ID# U105, "Peaches", was observed to be placing her left hind foot on the floor in a
sensitive manner. Closer observation of the dog's foot revealed that the toenails have become excessively long
and the outer toenail was starting to curve back under the foot. The toenails on the front left foot are also
overgrown and beginning to curve under. Overgrown toenails can cause pain and discomfort to the dog as well as
affect the animal(cid:25)'s normal gait. The licensee must trim this dog's toenails and must ensure that the toenails of all
dogs are kept at an acceptable length at all times.
