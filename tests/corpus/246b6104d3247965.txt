3.25(d)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
Waste disposal. There are accumulations of blackish water and debris in the two cement gutters in the guinea pig
room. Numerous flies were present in the animal room. Provisions must be made for the removal and disposal of
animal waste, food waste, bedding and debris to minimize vermin infestation, odors and disease hazards. Correct
by ensuring the gutters are operating properly and cleaned so that stagnant liquid waste and debris are not
accumulating and blocking drainage to minimize infestations, odors, and disease hazards. Correct by September
13, 2018.
3.31(b)
SANITATION.
Housekeeping. There are accumulations of dirt, debris, dead flies, and cobwebs on the window ledges. Rodent
droppings were present on the floor by a desk and under and around an empty pallet. The cement drains had
accumulations of blackish water and debris. Numerous flies were present in the animal room. Animal facilities must
be kept clean to protect the health of the animals. Correct by ensuring the premises are kept clean, including
removing dirt, debris, dead flies, cobwebs, rodent droppings from window ledges, drains, and floor; routinely dusting
off and cleaning surfaces; and maintaining a level of general cleanliness to support good animal husbandry and
health. Correct by September 13, 2018.
3.31(c)
SANITATION.
Pest Control. There were rodent droppings on floor of the guinea pig room near desk, and under and around an
empty pallet. Numerous flies were present in the animal room. Fly strips were present and contained numerous
flies, but additional measures are needed to control the fly problem. Licensee must establish and maintain an
effective program for the control of flies and rodents to protect the health and well-being of the guinea pig colony.
Correct by September 19, 2018.
