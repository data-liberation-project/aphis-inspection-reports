2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
The elderly male fossa has a chronic eye condition in both eyes. Both eyes are very cloudy and the left eye has
what appears to be red blood vessels on the eye surface. The licensee stated that a veterinarian has not examined
the eyes in a long time. It is important to have regular veterinary eye exams when chronic conditions exist to ensure
they do not worsen . The fossa needs to be examined by a veterinarian to assess the current condition of the eyes
to determine if any treatment is needed. CORRECT BY : 5/10/16
3.130
WATERING.
The 2 African Crested Porcupines had no water at time of the inspection . Water was given at request of the
inspectors and one animal immediately began to drink. The licensee and a zoo worker stated that the animals were
watered the night before but have a tendency to knock the water container over. It is important for the health of the
animals that they have access to water at all times especially with hot weather approaching. All water containers
must be secured to ensure that the animals can not knock them over.
CORRECT BY : Corrected During Inspection .
3.131(a)
SANITATION.
The hippo pool water is dark green and there is floating material on the top of the water. Licensee stated that the
pool filter is broken but repairs will be made soon. The pool is said the be drained and filled with clean water at least
once every 2 weeks. Hippos spend a lot of time in the water and it is important to maintain a clean water
environment for the health and well being of the animal. The pool must be cleaned as often as necessary and filters
must be maintained in working order to minimize disease hazards and prevent contamination.
CORRECT BY : 5-8-16
The floors of both shelter boxes for the Asiatic Black Bears are wet from recent rains . The bedding material is wetand there is an odor present. Wet, dirty bedding may predispose the animals to skin conditions and other diseases.
The shelter boxes must be cleaned and wet bedding promptly removed to prevent contamination of the animals and
to reduce disease hazards and odors.
