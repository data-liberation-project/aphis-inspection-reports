2.40(a)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
During the inspection it was identified that the attending veterinarian conducted the annual physical exams for each
the animals on 13 May 2016. During the veterinarians exam one dog that is still at the facility was identified as
having grade 3 dental disease. The dog identified was an adult female Yorkshire Terrier (microchip# 0A01400845).
Upon examination of this dog, the teeth appeared to not have been cleaned. The upper canine teeth were both
broken, several of the cheek teeth were missing. The canines,remaining cheek teeth and molars had a moderate
buildup of tan material. The gums were swollen along the cheek teeth and were receding along the canines and
cheek teeth. According to the program of veterinary care any dog identified as having grade 3 dental disease shall
be seen by a veterinarian for dental cleaning within three months to appropriately remove the tartar buildup from the
teeth. The licensee did not contact the veterinarian for a dental cleaning. The Licensee shall assure that the
attending veterinarian has appropriate authority to ensure the provision of adequate veterinary care.
Licensee must contact the veterinarian for the examination of the animal previously identified as having dental
disease. Documentation of the examination, diagnosis and treatment must be maintained and made available for
inspection.
Correct by close of business on November 16, 2016
