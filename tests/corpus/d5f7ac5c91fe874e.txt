2.5(d) REPEAT
DURATION OF LICENSE AND TERMINATION OF LICENSE.
- The drive through area of the park is currently open for visitors. Site 002 (drive through site) is not a valid site and
must be in complete compliance with all aspects of the Animal Welfare Act prior to being used for regulated activity.
2.131(d)(2) REPEAT
HANDLING OF ANIMALS.
The drive through area of the park had public members driving through, rolling down windows, and feeding animal
feed in paper pepsi cups that had been purchased at the gift shop. There were no park employees present during
this public/ animal interaction. The facility representative stated that the public can purchase 14% creeper pellets at
the gift shop and feed to the animals in the drive through park. They dispensed feed in the paper pepsi cups which
are for sale in the gift shop. The inspectors present observed this animal feeding occuring numerous times during
the inspection without any park attendant present. A responsible, knowledgeable, and readily identifiable employee
or attendant must be present at all times during periods of public contact.
2.131 REPEAT
HANDLING OF ANIMALS.
- The information for "Handling Dangerous Animals" has not been completed and sent to the regional office. The
qualifications of the employees and addressing the other issues listed on the form are of utmost importance. The
drive through section of the park contains potentially dangerous animals, such as a rhinocerous and a tiger, which
were not on the original license. Individuals lacking knowledge and experience with dangerous animals might
negatively impact the health and welfare of the animal through improper husbandry. The licensee must provide the
regional office written documentation regarding the experience and knowledge of the individuals who handle these
animals. This information must be submitted and approved by the Western Regional Office before this site can be
approved for regulated activity.This was a focused inspection following the inspection 1 May 2014. During this inspection visitors were noted
entering the drive through area of the park.
The AV was on the property 19 June 2014. The public barrier fence to the rhinoceros enclosure has been repaired.
The section of fence just inside the cattle guard has been replaced. The trees have been removed from the
perimeter fence.The large shelter in the Serengeti area has been repaired. The tiger enclosure has been repaired
and gaps are no longer present at the kick-in. The down fence in the Prezwalski hosre enclosure has been
removed. The feed storage shed has been replaced and the area around the feed storage has been cleaned and
mowed.
