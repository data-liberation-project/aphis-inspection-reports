3.127(d)
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
The new Patagonian cavy area has two gates on the perimeter fence which are only four feet in height. The
perimeter
fence including access gates must be at least 6 feet in height so that it protects the animals in the facility by
restricting
animals and unauthorized persons from entering, and so that it can function as a secondary containment system for
the animals in the facility.The gates must be replaced or repaired so that they are at least 6 feet in height. Correct
by April 18, 2016.
3.131(a)
SANITATION.
An enclosure housing two chinchillas has accumulations of excreta on three elevated plastic shelves. Excreta must
be removed as often as necessary to prevent contamination of the animals and to minimize disease risks and
odors.
Ineffective cleaning and/or infrequent cleaning exposes the animals to disease hazards and health problems.
Correct by ensuring all primary enclosures are cleaned and sanitized often enough to prevent an accumulation of
excreta and debris.Correct by March 22, 2016.
