2.126(a)(2)
ACCESS AND INSPECTION OF RECORDS AND PROPERTY; SUBMISSION OF ITINERARIES.
* The facility does not employ a full-time veterinarian and there was no written program of veterinary care available
at the time of inspection. A written program of veterinary care is required for facilities which do not employ a
full-time veterinarian. The licensee, in coordination with the attending veterinarian, must provide more specific
information on the type of products used at the facility and the frequency of use. This is not considered to be
adequate veterinary care when information on how the licensee provides veterinary care for all the animals have not
been recorded. The licensee must ensure that the program of veterinary care is updated with current preventive
practices to ensure that all animals are provided adequate veterinary care. The program of veterinary care must be
made available for review during inspections to ensure adequate veterinary care is being provided. Correct by
12/22/16.
3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
* The entrance to one plastic shelter structure is chewed exposing a jagged, roughened surface. The shelter
structure used by one hamster is located in indoor housing. A chewed, roughened surface could harbor disease
causing agents which affects the health and well-being of the hamster. The licensee must ensure that all indoor
housing facilities shall be maintained in good repair to protect the animals from injury. Correct by 12/6/16.
* The facility inspection and exit briefing done on 11/22/16 were conducted with the licensee.
