2.50(a)(1)
TIME AND METHOD OF IDENTIFICATION.
Four Maltese dogs that were >16 weeks old did not have individual identification on them. Appropriate identification
of dogs is necessary to meet recordkeeping requirements for inventory, disposition, transport, and healthcare
documentation. The licensee must properly identify each animal that is 16 weeks of age or older with a microchip,
tatoo, or collar and tag.
TO BE CORRECTED BY DECEMBER 21, 2014.
2.50(a)(2)
TIME AND METHOD OF IDENTIFICATION.
Four weaned Maltese puppies that were from three separate litters were housed together in an enclosure. None of
the puppies had proper individual identification. Appropriate identification of dogs is necessary to meet
recordkeeping requirements for inventory, disposition, transport, and healthcare documentation. The licensee must
individually identify puppies that are from different litters and housed together in the same enclosure. Acceptable
forms of identification include a microchip, a collar and tag, or a plastic-type collar with the required information
legibly written.
TO BE CORRECTED BY DECEMBER 21, 2014.
The inspection and the exit briefing were conducted with the licensee.
