3.1(c)(1)(1)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
At time of inspection in building one pens one, three, and five pens on the south side, outside facility where Shebas
are housed the last two pens at the south end, outside facility where the Corgies and Shebas are housed pens two,
four and eight starting at the south end, and inside the big barn in one enclosure on the south side the metal base
iron used to help support the enclosure gates and wire panels are showing excessive rust. An estimated three foot
section of metal is rusted completely through on building one at the bottom of the gates. The other rusted metal
base iron is at the bottom of the wire panels. Fifteen dogs are in contact with the affected support metal base.
Excessive rust can erode and deteriorate all metal surfaces which affects the structural strength of the metal. In
addition, the ability to protect the animals from injury could be questioned. The licensee must ensure that all metal
surfaces are structurally sound, kept in good repair and must protect the animals from injury. To be corrected by
05-08-14.
3.6(a)(2)(1)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
At time of inspection in Building Four the first and fifth pens on the west side had a broken and protruding wire. The
broken wire in one pen was up closer to the top of the enclosure and the other broken wire was at the bottom of the
enclosure . Four dogs are in contact or can come in contact with the affected wiring. Sharp points or edges could
injure the dogs. The licensee must repair or replace all wire or wood surfaces to ensure the safety or health of the
dogs. To be corrected by 04-20-14.
