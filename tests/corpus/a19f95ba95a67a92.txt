3.75(c)(3)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
Excessive amounts of feces were present in the following primate enclosures: rhesus and pigtail macaque, baboon,
spider monkey, colobus, black and white ruffed lemur. Excess feces build-up can expose the primates to soiling,
disease hazards and pests. Hard surfaces with which nonhuman primates come in contact must be spot-cleaned
daily and sanitized in accordance with 3.84 of this subpart to prevent accumulation of excreta or disease hazards.
Correct from this day forward.
3.131(d)
SANITATION.
Evidence of rodents, flies, and fruit flies were evident throughout the facility. Pests can expose the animals to
disease and discomfort. A safe and effective program for the control of insects, ectoparasites, and avian and
mammalian pests shall be established and maintained. Correct from this day forward.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with facility representatives.
