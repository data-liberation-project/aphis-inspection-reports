3.75(a)

Housing facilities, general.
On the gorilla's island there were two platforms that needed repairing. One platform had a missing wooden plank and the
second platform had two broken wooden planks. One of those broken planks had three nails with the heads and threaded
shanks exposed. These broken platforms could cause injury to the animals. The gorillas platform must be constructed so
that they are structurally sound and they must be kept in good repair to protect the animals from injury. Correct by June
17, 2022.
3.75(c)(2)

Housing facilities, general.
The owl monkey enclosure had branches that were soiled. These branches can't be effectively cleaned or sanitized.
Clean and sanitized surfaces can prevent disease and possibly illness but when that can not be achieved the surfaces
shall be removed or replaced. Correct by June 10, 2022.
3.75(c)(3)

Housing facilities, general.
The owl monkey enclosure had landings that were soiled and the golden-headed tamarin's entrance way, walls and
landings were also soiled. All hard surfaces with which nonhuman primates come in contact must be spot-cleaned daily


and sanitized to prevent accumulation of excreta or disease hazards for the health and well-being of the animals. Correct
by June 10, 2022.
3.125(a) Repeat

Facilities, general.
The crossbeams at the top of the caracals outdoor enclosure supporting the ceiling has bowed. The structures strength
could be compromised and could fall inside injuring the animal or create a breach in the enclosure. The outdoor housing
facilities shall be structurally sound and shall be maintained in good repair to protect the animals from injury and to
contain the animals. Correct by September 3, 2022
