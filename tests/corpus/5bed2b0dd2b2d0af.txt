3.125(a)

Facilities, general.
There is wire fencing covering the agricultural gate that allows entrance into the enclosure housing a zebra and bactrian
camel. The wire fencing on the lower left side of the gate us pulled away from the gate and has sharp points sticking into
the enclosure.

The outdoor housing facilities shall be structurally sound and shall be maintained in good repair to protect the animals
from injury and to contain the animals.

Replace or repair the fencing attached to the gate to prevent injury to the zebra and camel contained in the enclosure.

To be corrected by November 18, 2020.
3.129(a)

Feeding.
The kale being fed to the two African Crested porcupines was wilted, and some of the kale was yellow in color.

The food shall be wholesome, palatable, and free from contamination and of sufficient quantity and nutritive value to

maintain all animals in good health.

The food used for animal consumption must be palatable, and of sufficient nutritive value to maintain the animals in good
health. Old food must be removed from primary enclosures prior to adding new food to ensure the diet is palatable and of
good quality.

To be corrected from this day forward.

This inspection and exit interview were conducted with a facility representative.
