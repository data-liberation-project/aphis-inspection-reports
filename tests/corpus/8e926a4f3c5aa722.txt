2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
2.40 (b) 2
Multiple containers of expired medications were found during inspection in the grooming/wash/treatment area.
These were intermingled on a shelf with pharmaceuticals in current use. Expired medications found included: Baytril
Injectable (Exp Jan 2007), Animax Cream (Exp April 2010), Metronidazole 250 mg (Exp April 2016), Clavamox (Exp
June 2015), Albon (Exp Nov 2016) and Vetricin (Exp August 2011).
Expired medications can have unpredictable effects and should not be used on regulated animal species. Expired
medications should be labelled as expired and segregated from non-expired medication until appropriately disposed
of. Furthermore, the licensee must ensure that all medications in current use on regulated species are not expired
and labeled properly in accordance with standard veterinary practices.
Correct this date forward

HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.

Accumulations of waste, debris, animal exudate, dirt or grime on cabinets, floors and walls in animal areas may lead
to the spread of pathogens to animals in those areas. Such accumulations may likewise attract unwanted vermin
and insect pests. Surfaces of housing areas/animal rooms must be cleaned and sanitized as often as necessary to
prevent accumulation of waste, debris, exudate, dirt or grime and to satisfy accepted husbandry standards.
