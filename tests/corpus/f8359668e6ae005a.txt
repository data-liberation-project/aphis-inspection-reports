3.131(a)
SANITATION.
1. Excessive feces and urine scale were present in the enclosure containing the Patagonian cavy, the chinchilla,
the dove, and the three finches during the time of inspection. The four species share the enclosure without any
barriers between them. Elevated shelves and planks of wood are located within the enclosure to enable the
chinchilla and birds to nest and eat without interference from the Patagonian cavy. Excessive bird feces were
present on the elevated wood shelf on the front side of the enclosure and on the shelf containing the chinchilla's
food receptacle. Excessive chinchilla feces and urine scale were present on the elevated wood shelf located in the
back of the enclosure. Excessive feces can make animals ill.
Thoroughly clean and sanitize all affected areas of the enclosure and repeat often enough to prevent build up.
To be corrected: 2-3-17
2. Excessive fly specks were present on the hedgehog enclosure during the time of inspection. The fly specks
were concentrated on the wooden walls and wall top of the enclosure. Flies carry and spread diseases that can
make animals ill.
Thoroughly clean and sanitize the hedgehog enclosure and repeat often enough to prevent build up.
To be corrected: 2-3-17
3.131(d)
SANITATION.
Excessive flies and fruit flies were present in the large outdoor building during the time of inspection. This building
houses several regulated mammals and some tortoises. The flies and fruit flies were observed on, in, and around
several of the animals and their enclosures, but were concentrated around the food in the tortoise enclosures. The
facility had at least six sticky fly bates/traps in the room, but many of them were already filled with flies. Flies carry
and spread diseases that can make animals ill.Implement and maintain an effective program to control the flies at the facility.
To be corrected: 2-7-17
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the owner.
