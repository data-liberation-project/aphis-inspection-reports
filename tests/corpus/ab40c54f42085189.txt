3.4(c) REPEAT
OUTDOOR HOUSING FACILITIES.
(c) Construction.
****
The facility has outdoor enclosures with wood floors and shelters, not impervious to moisture. The wood floors and
dog shelters had water and urine soaking into the wood. There were 7 outdoor enclosures with 12 dogs that had
wood plank floors and dog shelters that were constructed with wood on the inside in contact with the dogs. These
wood surfaces are not impervious to moisture, therefore cannot be properly cleaned and sanitized. Licensee stated
the wood is sealed about once a year. The licensee must ensure all surfaces in contact with animals are
impervious to moisture. The licensee must ensure all surfaces must be maintained on a regular basis.
3.6(a)(2)(1) REPEAT
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
(a) General requirements.(2) (i) Have no sharps
****
There were at least three enclosures affecting 6 dogs that had broken wires, tie wires and sharp edges in the
enclosures. These wires are sharp points protruding into the enclosure at dogs level. Sharp points or edges could
injury the dogs. The licensee must ensure that all enclosures are free of sharp points and edges.
3.11(a) REPEAT
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
(a) Cleaning of primary enclosures.
****
The facility had at least 4 outdoor enclosures housing at least 7 dogs, that had brown fecal like matter dried,
packed and caked on the wood flooring. This brown fecal like matter covered 60%-90% of the flooring. There were
at least 3 dog entry doors that had brown matter built-up covering 60%-80% of the door. A build-up of feces is a
disease hazard and can attract pest. The licensee must ensure all surfaces which come in contact with the dogs
are spot cleaned daily. The licensee must ensure areas with fecal waste in contact with animals are cleaned asoften as necessary to prevent accumulation of feces and food waste and to reduce disease hazards pests, insects
and odors.
The inspection and exit interview were conducted with facility representative.
