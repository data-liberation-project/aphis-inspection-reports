2.40(a)(1)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
The licensee was unable to present a written program of veterinary care at the time of the inspection. She stated
that the attending veterinarian had not been to the facility in years and when the attending veterinarian's office was
contacted, they also stated that they had not heard from the facility in years.
Each exhibitor shall employ an attending veterinarian under formal arrangements who shall make regularly
scheduled visits to the premises.
The licensee needs to work with the attending veterinarian to compile a written program of veterinary care for the
animals in their care.
To be corrected by August 31, 2018.
2.40(b)(2) DIRECT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
The male wolf - Wakan - who is gray in color, and the female wolf - Anika - who is white in color were in poor body
condition. Both animals were moderately thin and had dull, thinning hair coats. Wakan was observed to have an
arched back and a tucked-up abdomen.
Approximately one third of the tips of both ears on both wolves were red and inflamed with a crusty black matter
covering part of the ears. Bloody discharge was observed on Anika with a small amount of bloody discharge on the
hair between her ears. There was skin sloughing from Anika’s right ear and from Wakan’s right ear.
Both wolves paced continually along the front portion of the exhibit during the inspection going from one side of the
enclosure to the other and then back again.
The licensee needs to work with the attending veterinarian to establish and maintain a program of veterinary care
for the wolves that uses the appropriate methods for preventing, controlling, diagnosing, and treating diseases and
injuries.These wolves need to be evaluated by the attending veterinarian by close of business August 27, 2018.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with a facility representative.
