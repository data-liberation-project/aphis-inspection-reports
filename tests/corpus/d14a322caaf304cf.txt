2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
* "Maisey", a five year old tan and white, ShihTzu, female, (ID # 004357808) had an excessive buildup of a greenish
brown to brown tarter on the upper front, cheek and canine teeth (the large carnassial tooth) extending up to the
gum line. The tarter covered over half of the canine and front teeth. The upper back cheek teeth could not be
visualized and had a buildup of tarter. The gums were swollen and dark pink to light purple. This dog also had a
mild watery discharge from both eyes and the hair around the mouth was mildly wet.
The buildup of abnormal tarter on teeth can be painful, impact the ability of the dog to eat normally, and harm the
health of the animal. Eye discharge and drooling from the mouth can occur due to infection, allergies, injury,
irritation or other medical conditions that could be painful.
* "Cookie", a five year old chocolate and white, Cocker Spaniel, female, (ID # 025798513) had an excessive buildup
of a brown tarter on the upper front, cheek and canine teeth (the large carnassial tooth) extending up to the gum
line. The tarter covered over half of the canine and front teeth. The upper back cheek teeth could not be visualized
and had a buildup of tarter.
The buildup of abnormal tarter on teeth can be painful, impact the ability of the dog to eat normally, and harm the
health of the animal.
*An adult, fawn colored, five year old male Pug, (ID# 841008009), has a thick build-up of grayish-brown tartar
covering the upper-half of most of its teeth. The gums are reddened and moderately swollen. There is a pronounced
area of gum recession directly above the upper right canine tooth. There is also a creamy gray discharge present
along the upper gum line.
*A cream colored, adult, male Poodle (ID#604520312), has an abnormal build-up of dark brownish tartar covering
the upper-half of most of its teeth. The gums are also reddened and swollen. The upper front teeth also have a
creamy gray discharge along the gum line.
The abnormal build-up of material on teeth can cause damage to the gum tissue and tooth structures, be painful,impact the ability of the dog to eat normally and harm the health of the animal.
The dogs listed above have not yet seen a license veterinarian for tarter buildup. The licensee stated, that they were
aware they needed to be cleaned.
The licensee must have the dogs examined by a veterinarian to obtain an accurate diagnosis and appropriate
treatment plan. The outcome of this consultation must be documented and provided to the inspector upon request.
The licensee must ensure that all animals receive adequate veterinary care at all times.
