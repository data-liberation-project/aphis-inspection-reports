2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
Each breeder must provide and maintain programs of adequate veterinary care for all dogs in the kennel and use
appropriate methods to prevent, control, treat diseases, injures, and assess their health daily. A white wheaten
terrier male, identification number 00071968fFB, has matted hair. These mats cover a very big area of the dogs
coat. The mats are pulling the hair together leaving areas of skin showing. The mats are around the legs and feet,
too. Mats can lead to irritations that can cause open wounds and hots spots that can be very uncomfortable and
lead to infection that can harm dogs. The breeder must put into place a good system that addresses the hair coat of
each dog in this kennel to ensure that the best health and good care is being provided at all times. The owner must
trim this dogs hair coat and remove all mats by 05-31-2017
3.11(a)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
Excreta must be removed from enclosures daily and more often if needed to prevent an excessive accumulation of
feces. Daily cleaning of feces also helps to reduce disease hazards, insects, pests and odors. One of the
enclosures in the old kennel housing two dogs, needs to be cleaned at least once a day and more often if needed.
The owner must put into place a good cleaning program that cleans all dog enclosures at least once a day and then
sanitizes at least twice a month. Owner stated the program in place for this kennel area is cleaning feces three to
four times a week. Adequate cleaning can help dogs to stay comfortable and healthy, The owner must clean this
kennel enclosure by close of this day. 05-24-2017.
