3.126(b)
FACILITIES, INDOOR.
In the animal housing room there are multiple windows and fans however upon entry into the room there was a
strong smell of ammonia which burnt the eyes and throat. Indoor housing facilities shall be adequately ventilated by
natural or mechanical means to provide for the health and to prevent discomfort of the animals at all times. Such
facilities shall be provided with fresh air either by means of windows, doors, vents, fans, or air-conditioning and shall
be ventilated so as to minimize drafts, odors, and moisture condensation. Additional or effective ventilation must be
provided to protect the health and welfare of the animals.
Affects sixty-nine adults and three young. Correct by November 3, 2014
3.131(c)
SANITATION.
The housing facility needs to be cleaned to remove the accumulations of used food, bedding and or waste material
on furniture surfaces, the backs of the enclosures stacks and in the empty enclosures. The premises shall be kept
clean and in good repair in order to protect the animals from injury, facilitate husbandry practices, prevent the
spread of disease and protect the health and welfare of the animals.
Affects sixty-nine adults and three young. Correct by November 3, 2014
