Records: Dealers and exhibitors.
Acquisition records did not exist or could not be located for 14 sheep. Accurate recordkeeping is important for animal

2.126(c)

Access and inspection of records and property; submission of itineraries.
The licensee did not submit an itinerary for travel to North Carolina for a performance on July 4th, 2022. Submission of
itineraries is required so that animals can be inspected when they are away from their home site. Correct by 9/21/22 by
submitting itineraries at least 48 hours prior to travel to USDA Animal Care. (Itineraries can be emailed to:
animalcare@usda.gov)
3.13(a) Repeat

Veterinary care for dogs.
The Program of Veterinary Care (PVC) for dogs does not include the following required parts:
***A schedule for sampling and treatment of intestinal parasites and other pests,
***Preventive care to ensure maintenance of healthy hair coats, nails, eyes, ears, skin, and teeth
The PVC must address all of the required elements listed above to ensure the health and wellbeing of dogs. Correct by


obtaining a written program of veterinary care from the attending veterinarian which contains all of the required elements
listed in 3.13.
3.13(b) Repeat

Veterinary care for dogs.
Medical records were incomplete for 4 adult border collie dogs. The licensee stated that he administers ivermectin every
month. The records only include the date of administration, but do not include the type or amount of medication
administered. Accurate and complete medical records are required for dogs in order to keep track of when problems
arise, and when medications are given. Correct by by maintaining medical records for dogs which include all of the
required elements listed in 3.13.
3.77(f) Repeat

Sheltered housing facilities.
The outdoor area of a sheltered housing facility containing two adult white-throated capuchin monkeys is not completely
enclosed by a perimeter fence. The outdoor area of the enclosure has chain link walls and roof, beyond which there is
some additional fencing of various heights. However, there is not one continuous 6 feet tall perimeter fence. A perimeter
fence is essential for preventing contact between exotic animals and unauthorized people or animals. A perimeter fence
also acts as a secondary containment system in the event an animal escapes from its primary enclosure. Correct by
installing a perimeter fence that is at least 6 feet tall around the outdoor area of the primate enclosure, or by obtaining
approval from the Administrator for alternative measures.

This inspection was conducted with designated representatives, and exit interview was conducted with the licensee.
