3.125(a) REPEAT
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The wood doors separating the tiger enclosure from the holding area was chewed and had splinters.
Splinters may cause injury to the tigers gum, skin, paws, etc.
The facility must be constructed of such material and of such strength as appropriate for the animals involved. The
housing facilities shall be structurally sound and shall be maintained in good repair to protect the animals from
injury.
3.129(a) REPEAT
FEEDING.
The freezer containing meat was rusted around the door and the seal that surrounds the door was broken.
Pieces of rusted metal, condensate, blood, and dirt was observed inside the food storage area which may
contaminate the food.
The food shall be wholesome, palatable, and free from contamination and of good nutritive value to maintain all
animals in good health.
3.131(c) REPEAT
SANITATION.
The area has construction work been done and there is construction material and equipment scattered around the
area.
Premises (buildings and grounds) shall be kept clean and in good repair in order to protect the animals and to
facilitate the prescribed husbandry practices set forth in this subpart.
The inspection and exit interview were conducted with the facility representative.
