3.1(c)(2)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
Four plastic dog houses in the outdoor enclosures were severely chewed and worn. Surfaces that are chewed or
worn cannot be readily cleaned and sanitized and may be contaminated with grime, feces, or harmful bacteria.
Correct by repairing or replacing chewed or worn dog houses by March 1, 2019.
3.1(c)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
There was rust on the surface of numerous gates, cages, and feeders. Four self feeders in the outdoor kennels
were severely rusted and the rust was observed to be pitted and flaking. Two of these self-feeders had broken and
jagged edges along the bottom opening where the food is dispensed. Three outdoor kennels had pitted, flaking rust
located on the bottom of the kennel wall where the wall meets the floor. One outdoor kennel had a door which was
severely rusted on the bottom: The pipe which comprised the bottom frame of the door was rusted through, leaving
a hole that was approximately 1 inch wide by 3 inches long. Three indoor wire cages had pitted, flaking rust on the
doors and walls. Rust effects the structural strength of enclosures, and may lead break resulting in jagged edges or
holes that may injure the animals in the enclosure. Rust may interfere with cleaning and sanitation of the
enclosures. Correct by repairing or replacing rusted structures and surfaces. Correct by March 1st, 2019.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the facility owner.
