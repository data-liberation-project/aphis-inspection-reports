3.31(a)(1)

Sanitation.
The adult guinea pig enclosures had brown staining on the sides of enclosures and soiled and wet bedding on the floors
especially by the automatic waterers. The cleaning process (method and frequency) utilized is not effective in removing
soiled bedding which is leading to wet bedding and staining on the sides of enclosures. Ineffective cleaning and sanitation
exposes the guinea pigs to potential disease hazards such as bacterial, fungal and viral diseases. Correct by ensuring
enclosures are cleaned often enough to prevent accumulations of soiled and wet bedding and staining of sides.
Additionally, enclosures must to be sanitized at least once every 2 weeks. Correct by August 21, 2021.
3.31(b)

Sanitation.
Housekeeping. There are accumulations of dirt, debris, cobwebs, and hay hanging on the wire sides of enclosures. Loose
hay, spilled feed, empty feed bags, boxes, and debris are present on the floor of the animal room. Cobwebs and debris
were hanging from the ceiling over guinea pig enclosures. Animal facilities must be kept to prevent hiding and living
areas for pests and to protect the health of the guinea pigs. Correct by ensuring that the animal room is kept clean,
including: removing loose hay, spilled feed, bags, and boxes from the floor; removing dirt, debris from the wire sides of
enclosures; removing trash; sweeping floors and removing debris and cobwebs from the ceiling on a regular basis.
Correct by August 21, 2021.
