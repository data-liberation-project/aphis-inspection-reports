3.80(a)(2)(ix)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
*** The wood shelves and platforms in primate enclosures are not constructed in a manner that allows them to be
readily cleaned and sanitized. These are wood platforms that have been treated but are not impervious to moisture.
These hard surfaces must be spot cleaned daily and sanitized at least once every two weeks in accordance with
regulations 3.84. Surfaces must be constructed in a manner that allows them to be readily cleaned and sanitized or
replaced when worn. The surfaces for the non-human primates need to be addressed to facilitate cleaning for their
health.
Correct by: Dec 1, 2017
3.84(d)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
*** There was evidence of mice along the back walls, on shelves and on top of a food container in a primate
building. There were mice feces on top of the yellow food container lid that was cracked, potentially causing
contamination to the primate diet. For the protection of the food supplies and the animals, an effective program for
the control of pests must be established and maintained in this building.
Correct by: Oct 31, 2017
