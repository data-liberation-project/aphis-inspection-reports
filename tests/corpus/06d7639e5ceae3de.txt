2.75(b)(1)

Records: Dealers and exhibitors.
Records of acquisition were not available for the animals at this facility (30 Zebu, 10 Watusi, 15 Pere David’s Deer, and
15 Fallow Deer). Recordkeeping is important for animal welfare because it may help in locating stolen animals,
investigating disease outbreaks in animals, and identifying illegal sale or movement of animals. Correct by making,
keeping, and maintaining accurate records which fully and completely disclose acquisition and disposition records for
animals, and by maintaining all records for at least 1 year after an animal leaves the property. Records should include all
animals bought and sold by the licensee under this license and site.

3.131(c)

Sanitation.
At the time of inspection, there was a variety of miscellaneous items, metal debris, machinery parts and clutter in the front
enclosure of the facility. The licensee explained that all the animals have access to the area, but the zebu are the ones
that frequent this enclosure the most. Metal debris and machinery parts can cause injury and can a pose a potential risk
to animals. All buildings and premises must be kept clean, cleared and in good repair in order to prevent animals from
injury and to keep them in good health. The licensee limited the animals’ access to this area by the end of the inspection.
