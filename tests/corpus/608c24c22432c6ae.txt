REQUIREMENTS AND APPLICATION.
This is a search inspection.
(cid:9)
*Facility is currently exhibiting regulated animals at the Putnam County Fair at the Fairgrounds in Cookeville, TN
without a valid USDA Animal Welfare Act license. The facility is exhibiting the following animals in a petting zoo: 1
dromedary camel, 1 kangaroo, 9 goats, 4 sheep, 1 llama, 1 cavy, 1 zebu, 3 cattle and 7 pigs. The facility is selling
animal feed on site so the public can purchase food to feed the animals. Inspector talked to owner via phone call.
*A valid license is necessary to protect the health and well-being of the animals and to comply with the Animal
Welfare Act and Regulations.
*The facility needs to obtain a USDA license if continuing to exhibit exotic/wild animal species. No USDA license is
necessary if facility decides to continue exhibiting only domestic farm animal species.
No regulated activities (exhibition of exotic/wild species) may be conducted until USDA license is obtained.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with facility representative.
