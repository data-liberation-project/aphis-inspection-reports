3.25(d)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
Waste disposal. The gutters below the guinea pig enclosures and water lines were full of liquid waste, bedding, hay,
and debris. Provisions must be made to remove and dispose of animal waste, food waste, bedding, and debris to
minimize vermin infestation, odors, and disease hazards. Correct by ensuring the gutters are operating properly and
cleaned so that stagnant liquid waste, bedding, hay, and debris are not accumulating and blocking drainage to
minimized infestations, odors and disease hazards. Correct by July 12, 2018.
3.31(a)(1)
SANITATION.
Cleaning and sanitation of primary enclosures. Enclosure # 3 housing 9 adult guinea pigs, enclosure # 4 housing 9
adult guinea pigs and 2 offspring, and enclosure # 6 housing 8 adult guinea pigs had feces covering the entire floor
area with little to no bedding present. There was no area with clean bedding in these three enclosures for the
animals to get out of the fecal material. Not having clean bedding exposes the animals to potential infections with
bacteria, fungi, and parasites. Correct by ensuring that all primary enclosures are clean and sanitized often enough
to prevent a buildup of feces. Additionally, ensure that the primary enclosures are sanitized at least once every two
weeks. Correct by July 12, 2018.
3.31(b)
SANITATION.
Housekeeping. The floor contains accumulation of spilled feed, dirty bedding, hay, dirt, and debris. There are
accumulations of dust, dirt, cobwebs, and debris, on the ceiling, walls, windows, ledges, supports, drainpipes, and in
empty and occupied enclosures. Empty enclosures also contain dirty bedding, feces, feed, supplies, tools and
discarded items. Ineffective cleaning exposes the animals to disease hazards and health problems. Animal facilities
must be kept clean to protect the heath of the animals. Correct by ensuring the premises are kept clean, including:
removing accumulations of dirt, dust, cobwebs, and spilled feed; routinely dusting off and cleaning surfaces;
cleaning empty enclosures of feed, dirty bedding, feces, and miscellaneous items; and maintaining a level ofgeneral cleanliness to support good animal husbandry and health. Correct by July 14, 2018.
