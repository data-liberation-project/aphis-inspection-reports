2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
A Newfoundland black and white female with USDA Tag #273 was seen to have a thick mucus yellow green
discharge coming from the right eye. The animal squinted the eye occasionally.
A Yorkshire Terrier female with USDA Tag #197 was noted to have extremely long nails that curled under. The gait
was effected but the animal did not appear lame. The nails are not currently effecting the pads.
A Yorkshire Terrier male with USDA Tag # 207 had multiple missing teeth, extensive tartar build up and two mobile
teeth. During the exam the animal did not appear uncomfortable nor did it have overt signs of infection in the gums.
The licensee must provide adequate veterinary care for the kennel as dictated by the attending veterinarian.
Adequate veterinary care is necessary to ensure optimal animal health.
Correct by contacting your veterinarian by 7/21/2017. The attending veterinarian must provide treatment for dogs
with USDA Tag #'s 207 and 273 no later than 7/27/2017. The nails for dog with USDA Tag# 197 must be cut by
7/21/2017.
3.1(c)(3)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
A brown male 3 year old Newfoundland with USDA Tag # 271 is being housed in a dirt floor horse stall with horse
fecal material all over the stall.The owner was not sure how long the dog has been housed in the barn but
suspected it had been a week. During the inspection the licensee removed the dog and put it in an acceptable
outside run.
Dogs must be housed in facilities that are impervious to moisture and can be spot cleaned everyday and sanitized
every two weeks. If the facilities can not be cleaned appropriately the disease hazard increases. Appropriate
housing is necessary to ensure optimal animal health. Correct by removing the dog identified as USDA Tag # 271 from the horse stall and providing appropriate housing.
Complete by 7/21/2017.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the licensee.
