3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
Section 3.125(a) Structural strength: The following enclosures need repairs:
1) The enclosure housing one Angus calf has the wire fencing detached from the bottom of the fence creating a gap
where the fencing has pulled away from the wood frame on the right side near the gate. The calf may try going
through this gap under the fence. Animal enclosures must be structurally sound and maintained in good repair to
protect the animals from injury and to contain the animals. Correct by repairing or replacing this section of fence.
Correct by April 28, 2017.
2) The wood shelter for the Scotch Highland cow has gaps with jagged wood between the shelter walls and the
ground. The cow may be injured by the jagged wood. Animal shelters must be structurally sound and maintained in
good repair to protect the animals from injury. Correct by repairing the shelter walls. Correct by May 4, 2017.
This inspection was conducted with a facility partner. The exit interview was conducted with a facility partner and a
facility cashier.
