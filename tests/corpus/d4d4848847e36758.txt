3.31(a)(1)
SANITATION.
During this inspection the floor of the 13 guinea pig enclosures had approximately a half inch layer of compacted
soiled/wet bedding that was underneath fresh hay. The primary enclosures are not being cleaned adequately or
often enough to remove accumulations of excreta and soiled bedding. Ineffective cleaning and sanitation exposes
the animals to disease hazards such as bacterial, fungal, and viral diseases. Primary enclosures must be cleaned
often enough to prevent and accumulation of excreta/soiled bedding and must be sanitized at least once every 2
weeks. Correct by ensuring that enclosures are cleaned and sanitized as required. Correct by February 12, 2016.
3.31(b)
SANITATION.
Housekeeping: The floor of the animal building contained accumulations of loose hay, dust, and debris. Animal
facilities must be kept clean to protect the health of the animals and to prevent unnecessary disease risks.
Ineffective cleaning exposes the animals to disease hazards and health problems. Correct by ensuring the floors
are cleaned regularly to support good animal husbandry and health. Correct by February 12, 2016.
An exit briefing was conducted with the licensee.
