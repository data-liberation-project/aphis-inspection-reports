2.40(b)(2) DIRECT REPEAT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
(b)(2)
*A bottle of Azithromycin stored in the whelping facility had a label with an expiration date of 6/25/2013. Medications
used after their expiration date may not have the strength or effect intended. The licensee shall provide adequate
and appropriate veterinary care. Medications used to prevent and treat disease and illness should be clearly labeled
and discarded when out of date.
* A bottle of Combi Pen was stored on the shelf in the whelping facility. The bottle was labeled with the instructions
"store under refrigeration between 36-46 degrees F" . Medications not stored under the intended conditions may not
have the strength or effect intended. The licensee shall store medications as instructed by the manufacturer label.
* At least two adult dogs at the facility had matted hair. An adult male Poodle ID 715 had mats of hair around his
eyes and mouth. Sparky the male Yorkshire Terrier ID 764 had matted hair on his legs and body. His overall hair
coat was soiled and appeared wet. Matted hair can be irritating and even painful and may lead to health conditions
Routine grooming is an important aspect of animal husbandry and should be done with a proactive approach so that
mats do not form.
* A male Poodle ID 715(cid:29) had a mass of hair entangled in his bottom front teeth. Once identified the licensee pulled
the hair from the teeth. The teeth had buildup of a brownish substance on the surface. And black material in
between the teeth. At least one of the top teeth had broken off. His gums were bleeding. During a yearly exam in
2013 dog was identified by the attending veterinarian with a need for dental treatment. On a scale from 1 to 5, with 5
being the most severe the dog was marked as a 5. Bleeding gums, broken teeth and general dental complications
can be painful and affect the overall health of the animal. The licensee shall obtain a written diagnosis and
treatment plan from a licensed veterinarian and provide care as prescribed. Documentation shall be available at the
next inspection.
**** The licensee was informed during the inspection that the dog would need to be seen by a licensed veterinarian
within 24 hours for treatment. An appointment was made for that afternoon.
3.1(c)(3) REPEATHOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
(c)(3)
There was an accumulation of feces stuck between two layers of wire coated flooring in the 2nd and 3rd enclosures
of the raised outdoor housing facility. The licensee stated that they had already cleaned that morning. There was
also an accumulation of waste and bedding material along the sides of the concrete outdoor housing area. Sufficient
cleaning is necessary to prevent disease and to provide for the health and well being of the animals. The licensee
shall spot clean daily and sanitize accordingly.
3.4(b)(3) REPEAT
OUTDOOR HOUSING FACILITIES.
(b)(3)
The doghouses provided as shelter for the outdoor enclosures had no wind and rain breaks at their entrance. The
long metal roof that covers the enclosures offers protection from rain however; there is no barrier from the wind.
Adequate shelters offer protection from harsh weather conditions and aid in the ability of an animal to retain body
temperature. The licensee shall provide wind and rain breaks at the entrance to all outdoor shelter structures.
