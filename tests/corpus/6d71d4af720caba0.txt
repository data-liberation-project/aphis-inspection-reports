2.134(a)

Contingency planning.
The facility has not yet developed and documented a contingency plan. Planning for emergencies helps provide for the
humane handling, treatment, transportation, housing and care of the facility's animals in the event of an emergency or
disaster. The facility shall develop and document an appropriate plan to provide for the care of the animals in the event of
an emergency or disaster. Correct by 11/30/22.
3.84(b)(2)

Cleaning, sanitization, housekeeping, and pest control.
The non-human primate indoor enclosures are currently pressure washed using water. Debris was noted accumulating on
the walls and floors of the enclosures.. The enclosures must be adequately cleaned and sanitized at least once every two
weeks or more often if necessary to prevent an excessive accumulation of dirt, debris, food waste, and excreta and to
reduce disease hazards. Sanitization must be done using one of the following methods: live steam under pressure,
washing with soap and water of 180degreesF or with regular water plus a detergent and disinfectant. The indoor hon-
human primate enclosures must be sanitized at least once every two weeks using one of the required methods. Correct
by 10/28/22.

3.84(d)

Cleaning, sanitization, housekeeping, and pest control.
A large number of flies and other insects were noted in the sheltered portion of the non-human primate housing. Insect
pests can bother the animals and can spread disease. The facility shall establish and maintain an effective program for
control of insect pests in the non-human primate indoor housing. Correct by 11/1/22.
