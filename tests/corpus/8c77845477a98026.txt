3.25(a)

Facilities, general.
(a) Structural strength.
The wall, directly behind the hamster enclosure has a 6 inch long by 4 inch high hole in the drywall at the base. This wall
is no longer in good repair and can allow the entrance of other animals. This wall should be repaired to maintain
structural strength and to keep it in good repair as required.
3.31(b)

Sanitation.
(b) Housekeeping.
Soiled and spilled bedding is present, along the base of the wall, directly behind the hamster enclosure. Premises shall
be kept clean in order to facilitate the prescribed husbandry practices.
3.31(c)

Sanitation.
(c) Pest control.
During this inspection, the APHIS official observed a white mouse come through a quarter sized hole in the rubber wall
cove base. This hole is adjacent to the hole in the drywall directly behind the hamster enclosure. Damaged walls allow

the entrance of unwanted animals and vermin. An effective program for the control of mammalian pests shall be
established and maintained.

This is a 1st Re-License Inspection for a Class “B” license. The applicant has 60 days to come into compliance.

The inspection and exit briefing were conducted with the applicant.
