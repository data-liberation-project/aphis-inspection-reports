2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
There were 3 piglets in Farrowing room #3 and 8 piglets in Farrowing room #4 that had open sores surrounded with
brown scabs on both cheek areas below their eyes or on the side of their snouts. The sores were either crusted
over with scabs or were open, moist and raw red in the middle where there was continual irritation.
In the Gestation barn room #1 there was a sow #1001 that had 2 abscesses, one on her right shoulder area
approximately 2 inches in diameter and one smaller one on her left shoulder area. Sow #1624 had a ruptured
abscess on her right shoulder that was oozing large amounts of yellow/white puss from an abscess approximately 2
inches in diameter with an additional open sore above the ruptured abscess. The sores on the piglets faces and
abscesses on the sows have not been treated and the veterinarian has not been contacted in regards to treatment.
It is important that all illnesses, injuries or health issues are identified, treated and communicated to the veterinarian
for treatment protocols.
The licensee must contact the veterinarian for proper diagnosis and treatment of health issues and to oversee the
adequacy of other specs of animal care and use.
To be corrected by: January 31, 2014
2.40(b)(3)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
There were 3 piglets in Farrowing room #3 and 8 piglets in Farrowing room #4 that had open sores surrounded with
brown scabs on both cheek areas below their eyes or on the side of their snouts, and 2 sows (#1001, #1624) in
Gestation barn #1 that had abscesses on their shoulders that had not been identified or treated. There was norecord at the facility that these issues had been identified or were being treated or that the veterinarian had been
contacted for treatment options.
It is important that all illnesses, injuries or health issues are identified, treated and communicated to the veterinarian
in a timely manner in order to prevent prolonged discomfort or pain to the regulated animals and to prevent a
treatable issue from becoming a chronic untreatable condition.
The licensee must conduct daily observations of all regulated animals and identify health issues in a timely manner
and contact the veterinarian for proper diagnosis and treatment of health issues.
To correct by: January 31, 2014
Inspection and exit interview conducted with facility representative.
