3.1(c)(3)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
(c) Surfaces--(3) Cleaning.
***In the outdoor facility, the plastic type housing shelters were dusty and dirty. Three of these housing shelters
were chewed around the doorways and the sides where the top of the shelter attached to the bottom. There was
grime build up in the crevices of the teeth marks. Cleaning of hard surfaces is essential for a healthy environment
and reduces disease hazards by eliminating harboring places for diseases and pests. The licensee must remove
the dust, dirt and grime from these areas and assure that all hard surfaces are spot cleaned daily and sanitized as
required.
To be corrected by June 3, 2015.
3.11(d)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
(d) Pest control. ***In the barn with the hutch type enclosures, there was an excessive amount of mice droppings on the top ledges
of the housing shelters. Pests carry diseases which increases the risks of contamination to the dogs, water and food
supply. The licensee must ensure that measures are taken to control these pests and establish an effective pest
control program to eliminate pests throughout the entire facility as required.
To be corrected by June 3, 2015.
