2.40(a)(1)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
The program of veterinary care was not complete.
The licensee failed to provide the frequency and treatment methods for, intestional parasites, ectoparasites and
blood parasites.
In the vaccinations section it does not address rabies for adults or puppies, or lepto for puppies and only shows
some vaccinations given at 6 weeks and 1 vaccination at 4 weeks.
In the section for internal parasites it only listed 2 times per year for adults and puppies. it did not list what
medications. It did not list an adequate frequency of use for puppies.
In the section on blood parasites it listed none.
In the section on ectoparasites it only listed paramite / bath, it did not list concentration or frequency of use.
In the euthanasia section the method used is not listed.
In the emergency section the licensee failed to provide enough information such as phone number and name of
veterinarian to contact. Other people at the kennel may need to know who to contact in times of emergency.

The program of veterinary care must show all current practices at the facility. To insure the animals are recieving
adequate preventive veterinary care and the licensee must consult with the attending veterinarian to update the
program of veterinary care to show the current practices at the facility.
This affects all dogs.
To be corrected by march 7 2014
