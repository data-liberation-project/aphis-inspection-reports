2.40(b)(2) Direct

Attending veterinarian and adequate veterinary care (dealers and exhibitors).
A female Yorkshire Terrier, microchip #020780034, was observed in her enclosure with an unkept haircoat. Upon closer
observation it is noted that she has crusty flaky particles on her head and upper back and side area. In addition to the
skin issue it was also noted that she was very thin. Her ribs and vertebrae were easily palpable. She was having a
difficult time breathing and was observed to be having abdominal type breathing.
The licensee stated that her skin was affected from being in an enclosure with some rambunctious puppies about a week
ago and that when he noticed her skin condition he removed her from the enclosure.
This dog could have a medical condition that requires veterinary care.
The licensee did not have any medical records nor contacted a veterinarian with regards to this dogs condition.
The licensee must ensure that all dogs receive adequate veterinary care at times
The licensee was instructed to have this dog examined by a veterinarian by close of business today 22 Sept 21.
