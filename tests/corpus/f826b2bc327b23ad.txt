2.131(d)(2)
HANDLING OF ANIMALS.
A responsible, knowledgeable, and readily identifiable employee or attendant must be present at all times during
periods of public contact.
During this inspection it came to the attention of the inspector that members of the public have been feeding the
animals. There was no readily identifiable employees present when the public was feeding and coming into contact
with the animals to prevent injuries to the animals or the public and help ensure that the public is only feeding
appropriate feed to the animals.
During a conversation with the director it was learned that this feeding is frequent and ongoing. The public has
access/contact with the animals through the chain link fence or some animals run free around the facility.
Public contact and feeding of the animals may lead to dietary imbalances, injury to the animals or injury to the
public.
A responsible, knowledgeable and readily identifiable employee or attendant must be present at all times during
periods of public contact or the facility can avoid public contact with the animals by having sufficient distance and/or
a barrier between the public and the animals and keeping all animals inside of the enclosures.
Correct by October 18, 2014.
