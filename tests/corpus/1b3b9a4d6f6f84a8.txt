2.1(a)(1)
REQUIREMENTS AND APPLICATION.
** The applicant was operating as an exhibitor without a valid USDA license. The applicant had regulated animals
on exhibit to the public in the form of a petting zoo. The applicant must apply for and obtain a valid USDA license for
the regulated activity or cease the regulated activity. Any person operating as a exhibitor must have a valid license
to ensure that they are in compliance with the Animal Welfare Act.
2.40(a)(1)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
** The applicant did not have a written program of veterinary care. Exhibitors must have formal arrangements with
an attending veterinarian, to include a written program of veterinary care and regularly scheduled visits. The
applicant must have the attending veterinarian complete a written program to ensure that the animals at the facility
are receiving adequate veterinary care.
3.56(b)(1)
SANITATION.
** The rabbit enclosures had hair and dirt accumulated on the surfaces. The enclosures must be cleaned and
sanitized to reduce disease hazards and provide for the health and well-being of the animals. All rabbit enclosures
must be sanitized at least every 30 days.
3.127(d)
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
** One fox enclosure had a perimeter fence that was 3 feet high, the perimeter fence must be 6 feet high or have a
variance from the regional office. The applicant must provide a perimeter fence that is 6 feet high, restricts animals
and unauthorized persons from going through it or under it and having contact with the animals in the facility, and so
that it can function as a secondary containment system. The perimeter fence must be 3 feet in distance from the
primary enclosure.This was a complaint inspection.
This inspection and exit briefing was conducted with the owner.
Conducting regulated activities without a valid USDA license is a violation of the Animal Welfare Act.
End of report.
