3.125(a)

Facilities, general.
The primary enclosure housing 4 raccoons has two 2”X6” boards running horizontally along the back wall of the
enclosure. These boards are used to secure the fencing material for the enclosure and are both showing significant signs
of rot and deterioration. The screws used to secure the fencing to the rotting boards may become detached and create
areas of structural instability. Detached fencing can cause injury and/or a means of escape for the animals. The indoor
and outdoor housing facilities shall be structurally sound and shall be maintained in good repair to protect the animals
from injury and to contain the animals. To be corrected by March 1, 2022.

This is a 1st Relicense Inspection for a Class “B” license. The inspection and exit briefing were conducted with the
Applicant. All items must be in compliance within 2 more inspections or by April 1, 2022 or the applicant will forfeit the
license fee and must wait 6 months to reapply.
