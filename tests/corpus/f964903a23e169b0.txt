3.25(a)

Facilities, general.
Structural strength. The bottom of the doors and walls have gaps allowing the entrance of pests. There were rodent
droppings along the back wall by the double doors. The opened windows do not have screens allowing flies to enter the
animal room. Numerous flies were present in the animal room. Wild rodents and flies can increase the risk of disease in
the caviary.

Indoor guinea pig facilities must be structurally sound and maintained in good repair, to protect the animals from injury
and to restrict the entrance of other animals and pests. Correct by closing gaps along the bottom of doors and walls to
prevent the entrance of rodents. In addition, open windows need screens or other measures to prevent the entrance of
flies. Correct by August 30, 2021.
3.31(c) Repeat

Sanitation.
Pest control. Numerous flies were present in the animal room. The fly strips in guinea pig room were full of dead flies.
Open windows in the animal room do not have screens to prevent the entrance of flies and other insects. Rodent
droppings were present along the back wall by the double doors. There are gaps along the bottom of the doors and walls
allowing the entrance of mice. Pest control measures in place do not appear effective in controlling fly population or

controlling mice. Licensee must establish and maintain an effective pest program for flies and mice to protect the health of
the guinea pigs. Remains uncorrected.

This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the licensee.
