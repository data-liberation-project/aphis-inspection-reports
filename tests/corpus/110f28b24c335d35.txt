2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
"Camie" (no individual identification), a female labradoodle has wadded, matted hair along her chest, legs, muzzle,
ears and around the eyes. These areas of matted hair are hanging off the muzzle and ears in solid wads. The legs
and chest have hair that has twisted and is matted down to the skin. The matted hair around the eyes is of such a
solid mass that it appears to partially obscure vision and contain an organic matter that may be eye discharge. This
matted hair can irritate the skin, cause pain to the dog, contain debris, moisture and harbor other disease causing
organisms. Licensee must ensure that a method of hair coat maintenance is adhered to in order to
minimize/prevent matted hair, skin irritation and disease causing organisms for the health and well-being of the dog.
* To be corrected by: 11/2/19
2.50(a)(1)
TIME AND METHOD OF IDENTIFICATION.
."Camie" a female Labradoodle born November 28, 2018 had no unique individual identification. Appropriate
unique, individual identification of dogs is necessary to meet record keeping requirements for inventory, disposition,
transport and healthcare documentation. Licensee must ensure that the dogs are identified by one of the following:
1) a collar that contains the letters USDA, a unique identifying number, and the license number; 2) a tattoo
approved by USDA; or 3) a microchip.
* To be corrected by: 11/1/19
