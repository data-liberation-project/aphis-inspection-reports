2.75(b)(1)
RECORDS: DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS.
The facility was missing records regarding several animals that have been transferred elsewhere. Licensees must
make, keep, and maintain records or forms which fully and correctly disclose the required information reflective of
animals purchased, sold, and under its control, including animals that are sold or transferred away from the facility.
Records must include the name and address of the person to whom an animal was sold or given, date of the
transaction, species of the animal(s), and number of animal(s). Records are necessary to allow traceability of
ownership. Correct by making and keeping records with all required information. Correct by May 15, 2019.
3.81
ENVIRONMENT ENHANCEMENT TO PROMOTE PSYCHOLOGICAL WELL-BEING.
At the time of inspection, the facility did not have a documented Environmental Enhancement Plan reflective of the
ring-tailed lemurs currently maintained at the facility. The facility also indicated it currently is not following its
documented Plan specified for its common brown lemur. The facility must develop, document, and follow an
appropriate plan for environmental enhancement adequate to promote the psychological well-being of the
nonhuman primates. The written plan must be made available for inspection. Correct by May 1, 2019.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the Senior Zookeeper.
