2.134(a)

Contingency planning.
There was no Contingency Plan developed or documented by the Licensee at the time of the inspection.

Per this Section, exhibitors must develop, document, and follow an appropriate plan to provide for the humane handling,
treatment, transportation, housing, and care of their animals in the event of an emergency or disaster (one which could
reasonably be anticipated and expected to be detrimental to the good health and well-being of the animals in their
possession). The contingency plan requirement ensures that the animals receive proper care during emergency
situations. Correct by 9/19/22.
3.80(a)(1)

Primary enclosures.
One end of the wooden trim piece along the back wall of the outdoor portion of the Lemur enclosure is coming loose from
the wall. As a result, there is a gap or space between the trim piece and the back wall that a lemur’s limb or foot could be
caught in or injured.



Per this Section, primary enclosures for non-human primates must be structurally sound and maintained in good repair to
protect the animals from injury. Correct by 8/22/22.
