3.1(c)(3)

Housing facilities, general.
The surfaces underneath the enclosures of the housing facility are not being cleaned and sanitized when necessary to
satisfy generally accepted husbandry standards and practices. Most of the primary enclosures which are housing the
adult dogs have an accumulation of hair and organic matter hanging from the slatted flooring underneath the enclosure.
These accumulations lead to unsanitary conditions that can adversely impact the health and wellbeing of the animals.
Correct by ensuring hair and organic debris are cleaned from the underneath the enclosures as often as necessary to
satisfy generally accepted husbandry standards and practices.
3.11(c)

Cleaning, sanitization, housekeeping, and pest control.
At the end of the exterior washdown, there is an accumulation of moldy feces and soiled bedding on the ground and on
the fence immediately surrounding the area. Improper cleaning in the surrounding grounds can be a living and breeding
area for rodents, which could be injurious to the dogs health and wellbeing. Correct by ensuring that premises where
housing facilities are located, including buildings and surrounding grounds, are kept clean to facilitate acceptable
husbandry practices and to reduce or eliminate breeding and living areas for vermin.
