3.13

Veterinary care for dogs.
The last visit to the facility by the attending veterinarian was July 31, 2019. Medical records for dogs are not being kept to
include annual exams and their findings. The program of veterinary care does not include heartworm testing and fecal
testing. A frequency to prevent and treat for un-matted hair coats, overgrown toenails, eyes, ears, skin and teeth has not
been listed and approved by the attending veterinarian.

Each dealer must follow an appropriate program of veterinary care for dogs that is developed, documented in writing, and
signed by the attending veterinarian. Dealers must keep and maintain the written program and make it available for
APHIS inspection. The written program of veterinary care must address the requirements for adequate veterinary care for
every dealer in 2.40 and 3.13 in the regulations and standards of the Animal Welfare Act.

The attending veterinarian must visit the facility no less than every 12 months. Medical records for dogs must be kept,
maintained, and contain all veterinary requirements listed in standard 3.13. These include heartworm testing and results,
fecal testing and results, frequency to check and treat dogs hair coats, toenails, eyes, ears, skin and teeth. The record
must also include the veterinarians findings on the dogs annual exam, and all veterinary issues treated.
