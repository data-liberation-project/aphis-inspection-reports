2.40(a)(1)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
****
Under section II. A. Vaccinations for Dogs and Cats, the licensee had check marks in the juvenlile and adult boxes.
There was no frequency specified for either juvenile or adult dogs liisted. Frequency of vaccinations is necessary to
ensure that the dogs are receiving adequate protection from the vaccines.
Under section III. A. Vaccinations for Wild and Exotic Animals, the licensee had listed the species Lynx receiving
Distemper, Rhinotraccheitis, Chlamdea, Panleukopenia, Calici but did not list the frequency. When questioned the
licensee stated that they also vaccinate the Foxes and Skunks with Vanguard 5 but did not have them listed on the
Program of Veterinary Care nor the frequency under the vaccination section. Frequency of vaccinations is
necessary to ensure that all affected animals are receiving adequate protection from the vaccines.
The licensee must update the current Program of Veterinary Care to include frequency of vaccinations for dogs, and
all other wild animals that require vaccinations to include the frequency.
To be corrected by: 27 Mar 14.

ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
****
There are at least 5 Sknuks, 1 Albino and 4 black and white, that have excessively long toe nails. On at least one of
the skunks some the the nails have broken. Excessively long toenails can break causing pain and/or injury, and
can cause the feet to splay abnormally affecting the animals natural posture. The licensee must trim the nails of
these skunks feet.

2.50(b)(1)
TIME AND METHOD OF IDENTIFICATION.****
There are four Westhighland Terriers present that did not have any form of official identification. Official
identification is required in order to facilitate the required record keeping and to give each dog their own unique
identification. Licensee must provide these dogs with proper identification. Licensee must ensure that all dogs
have proper identification at all times.

2.75(a)(1)
RECORDS: DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS.
****
The licensee did not have the required Record of Acquisition for Dogs and Cats (APHIS form 7005). The APHIS
form 7005 (Record of Acquisition for Dogs and Cats) is necessary to allow for proper record keeping, identification,
and tracebacks. The licensee must fill out the APHIS form 7005 (Dogs on Hand) completely to reflect the
necessary information on all dogs.
