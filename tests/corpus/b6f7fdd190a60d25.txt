CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
There were at least 2 enclosures with an excessive amount of feces on the outside portion of the enclosure. One
enclosure had feces that was starting to turn white in color. In addition, about 75% of the wash downs had a mild to
excessive accumulation of feces, food waist and mineral deposit build up. Not having a proper routine for cleaning

reduce disease hazards pests, insects and odors.

3.11(b)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
On today's inspection, about 50% of of the primary enclosures were not cleaned as often as necessary to prevent
an accumulation of dirt, debris, food wast and excreta. Several of the enclosures had an moderate to excessive
amount of black brown material adhered to the doggy doors. In addition, there were several enclosures that had an
excessive amount of hair, food wast and other organic material built up on the wire mesh of the enclosure.
Enclosure that have not been properly cleaned or have not been cleaned often enough expose the dogs to
unnecessary disease hazards. All enclosures must be sanitized at least once every 2 weeks using one of the
methods prescribed in paragraph (b)(3) of this section, and more often if necessary to prevent an accumulation of
dirt, debris, food waste, excreta, and other disease hazards.
