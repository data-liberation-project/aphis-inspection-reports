2.40(b)(2) DIRECT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
Two guinea pigs were noted to appear un-thrifty and have increased respiration at the time of inspection. Upon
examination, one was noted to have long teeth that were worn from one side to create a sharp edge. Both animals
were subjectively underweight and the veterinarian had not been contacted by the facility. Failure to involve the
veterinarian early in stages of disease can lead to unnecessary suffering. Correct by having these animals
examined by a veterinarian who additionally will establish and maintain programs of adequate veterinary care that
include. The use of appropriate methods to prevent, control, diagnose, and treat diseases and injuries, and the
availability of emergency, weekend, and holiday care. Records of diagnosis and treatment of the animals must be
maintained for review by USDA inspector. To be corrected by 15 November 2016.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the owner.
