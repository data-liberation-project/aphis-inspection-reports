3.31(a)(1) REPEAT
SANITATION.
Several hamster enclosures contained dirty bedding and a build up of feces. The odor of ammonia was strong
around these enclosures. A strong odor of ammonia is an indication that the bedding in these enclosures needs to
be changed. Bedding contaminated with a build up of feces and urine creates a disease hazard for the animals . All
enclosures must be cleaned as often as necessary to prevent and accumulation of excreta and sanitized once every
2 weeks.
3.53(a)(1)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
Three ( 3 ) rabbits are housed in a wire enclosure that has excessive rust on the front of the enclosure. Excessive
rust prevents proper sanitation and weakens the strength of the wire to the point it may break. The wire enclosures
must be repaired or replaced and maintained in good repair to protect the animals from injury.

3.125(d) REPEAT
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
Two ( 2 ) gerbil enclosures each contained a decomposing /mummified animal .They appear to have been dead for
greater than 24 hours . Licensee states he checks enclosures daily for deceased animals but had not yet seen
these animals. Decomposing animals pose a disease hazard to healthy animals in the enclosure and must be
promptly removed.
The inspection and exit briefing were conducted with the licensee.
