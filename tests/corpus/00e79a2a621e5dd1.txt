2.126(a)(2)
ACCESS AND INSPECTION OF RECORDS AND PROPERTY; SUBMISSION OF ITINERARIES.
Records of acquisition for all of the mammals present, as well as the written program of veterinary care, were
unavailable for examination during this inspection. Record are necessary to account for the animals present and to
ensure that veterinary care is being provided. Facility must maintain these records at all times while conducting on
the road exhibitions.
TO BE CORRECTED BY: JUNE 7, 2016
2.131(d)(2)
HANDLING OF ANIMALS.
During this inspection, APHIS Officials observed the public climbing on animal fencing and dropping Ziploc bags
into the goat/sheep enclosure while feeding the animals. Readily identifiable employees/attendants were not
present in this animal area while the public was present. A readily identifiable employee/attendant must be present
at all times during periods of public contact to ensure that the animals are protected.
TO BE CORRECTED BY: JUNE 2, 2016
3.130
WATERING.
The water trough for the goats/sheep had an accumulation of spilled feed and hay present. Additionally, the water
was low in the trough and the smaller goats had a little difficulty in reaching the water level. Animals of various
heights should have access to water for their health and well being. All water receptacles shall be kept clean and
sanitary for the health of the animals involved.
CORRECTED AT THE TIME OF INSPECTION.3.131(c)
SANITATION.
During this inspection children were feeding the goats/sheep with food that was provided in small Ziploc bags.
Many of these bags were dropped into the enclosure, thus presenting a hazard to the animals housed therein.
Additionally, the bags were accumulating on the ground of this enclosure. Accumulations of trash shall be placed
in designated areas and cleared as necessary to protect the health of the animals.
CORRECTED AT THE TIME OF INSPECTION.
