2.1(a)
REQUIREMENTS AND APPLICATION.
The exhibitor was engaged in interstate exhibition while attending the Santa Clara County Fair in San Jose, CA on
16-Sep-2017 and did not have a site registration for travel (TRA Site).
Registration with a travel site provides notice to USDA Animal Care that the exhibitor travels overnight with
regulates species. Such notice is necessary such that inspectors can assure that the exhibitor submits itineraries
and otherwise fulfills the requirements of the Animal Welfare Act, notably for the care and welfare of the animals.
The licensee must request a TRA site from the Animal Care Regional Office.
Correct prior to any further traveling exhibition.
2.126(c)
ACCESS AND INSPECTION OF RECORDS AND PROPERTY; SUBMISSION OF ITINERARIES.
No itinerary was submitted by the Exhibitor for the venue (Santa Clara County Fair, San Jose, CA, 16-Sep-2017).
Itineraries are necessary to allow for the inspection of traveling exhibitors to confirm compliance with the Animal
Welfare Act, notably regarding the animal care and welfare while traveling.
Submission of itineraries as detailed in this regulation must be submitted for any overnight travel of regulated
species.
Correct from this date forward.
3.127(a)
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
While at the Santa Cruz (CA) County Fair, the exhibitor used four camels to provide rides for a fee to the public. The
camels were used in a rotation, with one camel rested for an hour while the other three were used for rides. Whileshade was provided for the public, no shade or shelter was provided either during the rest or working periods.
When sunlight is likely to cause overheating or discomfort of the animals, sufficient shade by natural or artificial
means shall be provided to allow all animals kept outdoors to be protected from direct sunlight.
Correct from this date forward.
This inspection and exit briefing were conducted with a facility representative.
