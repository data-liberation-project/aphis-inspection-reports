2.40(a)(1)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
There was no written Program of Veterinary Care (PVC) available for review at the time of inspection. In order to
ensure problems of animal health are addressed adequately, there needs to be a written PVC. The written PVC
shall include the species being utilized or sold in regulated activities, preventative care, common health problems
that they may have and how these will be addressed by the licensee and the Attending Veterinarian.
Ensure that the formal arrangement with the Attending Veterinarian includes a detailed written Program of
Veterinary
Care and is made available for review during the inspection process.

RECORDS: DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS.
During the inspection there were no records available for review which fully and correctly disclosed the required
information for all the animals being utilized in regulated activities. Maintaining current records of animals on hand,
including acquired and disposed of, is required in order to be able to accurately track the animals
Make, keep and maintain records which fully and correctly disclosed the required information regarding acquisition
and disposition of all the animals. Ensure that all records of acquisitions and dispositions are available for review
during the inspection process.
