3.55
WATERING.
The inside of the water bottle, for the rabbits housed in the hutch next to the duck enclosure, had a heavy
accumulation of algae. All watering receptacles shall be sanitized when dirty; Provided, however, That such
receptacles shall be sanitized at least once every 2 weeks.
3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
One metal fence panel, nearest the road, is severely bent and not structurally sound. This damaged fence panel
must be replaced in order to adequately contain the animals and to maintain the structural strength of the primary
enclosure fence.
3.125(c)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
In the feed storage room, the cans that contain feed has dirt and other debris on their lids. Additionally, the lid on
the cooler that contains the rabbit feed, has an accumulation of dirt and grime. All feed containers must be kept
clean and sanitary to protect such supplies from deterioration molding and contamination by vermin.
3.130
WATERING.
Several water receptacles, that are provided for the pigs and goats, were filled with accumulations of dirt and a filmof grime on the interior walls. All water receptacles shall be kept clean and sanitary for the health and well-being of
the animals involved.
3.131(c)
SANITATION.
HOUSEKEEPING - Premises (building and grounds)
Throughout the entire housing areas for the pigs, goats, sheep and ponies, there are accumulations of the following
items:
metal fence panels, plastic, metal plows, wood, logs, plywood with exposed nails, a metal shelf, bailing twine,
broken plastic lattice, metal parts, tires with and without rims, and miscellaneous items
These items must be removed to keep enclosures clean and free from items that could cause injury to the animals
housed therein. Items that are necessary must be organized and separated from the animals to prevent injury and
harborage areas for unwanted pests.
