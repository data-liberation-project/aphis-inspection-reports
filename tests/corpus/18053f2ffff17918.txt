2.75(b)(1)

Records: Dealers and exhibitors.
During a review of records, the licensee's inventory of animals on hand did not include the two potbelly piglets that were
being exhibited in addition to the white-tailed deer fawns.

Exhibitors need to make, keep, and maintain records which fully and correctly disclose information concerning animals
acquired, owned, held, leased, or otherwise in their possession or under their control. Failure to do so can can impede
animal ownership and health tracking efforts.

Correct by maintaining records that correctly disclose animals on-hand while on a traveling exhibit.
Correct by: August 12, 2021
2.126(c)

Access and inspection of records and property; submission of itineraries.
The licensee was observed to be exhibiting seven white-tailed deer fawns and two potbelly piglets at the Wayne County
Fair but no itinerary had been submitted to APHIS officials.



Traveling exhibitors must submit an itinerary to APHIS no fewer than 2 days in advance of any travel and shall contain
complete and accurate information concerning the whereabouts of any animal intended for exhibition at any location other
than the person's approved site. Failure to do so impedes APHIS officials ability to perform unannounced inspections of
traveling exhibits.
Correct by submitting an itinerary to APHIS no fewer than 2 days in advance of any future traveling exhibits of regulated
species.
Correct by: August 12, 2021

This inspection took place at the Wayne County Fair in Honesdale, PA.
