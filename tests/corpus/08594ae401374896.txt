2.1(a)

Requirements and application
The applicant has been operating as a dealer after the license had expired on September 23, 2022. Conducting regulated
activity without a valid license prevents APHIS officials from determining compliance with the Animal Welfare Act. Correct
by ensuring that a valid license is obtained before operating as a dealer, exhibitor, or operator of an auction or sale.
3.1(c)(2)

Housing facilities, general.
There is excessive chew damage present on the surfaces on the front walls of the kennel around the top rim. The chew
damage is also present around the tops of the dividers between the kennel stalls. The cage carpets used as whelping
mats in the facility have chew damage resulting in frayed ends and loose pieces of material hanging from them. Chew
damage on walls and dividers cannot be properly sanitized and can create sharp points that can cause injury to the
animals. Chew damage on whelping mats with loose or frayed ends can lead to animal entanglement or accidental
ingestion. Correct by ensuring that all surfaces including houses, dens, and other furniture type objects within the facility
that cannot be readily cleaned and sanitized, are maintained or replaced when worn or soiled.
3.8(a)

Exercise for dogs.

The facility has a exercise plan in place that has not been approved by the Attending Veterinarian. An Attending
Veterinarian approved exercise plan ensures that all dogs are provided an appropriate opportunity for exercise and
promotes animal welfare. Correct by providing an exercise plan that developed, documented, and followed. In addition to
being approved by the Attending Veterinarian.
3.13(a)(3)

Veterinary care for dogs.
The Program of Veterinary care for the facility does not currently have testing schedule for heartworm or fecal testing. The
facility has performed heartworm and fecal tests but do not have an Attending Veterinarian approved schedule to follow.
Failure to have and follow a written and Attending Veterinarian approved schedule for sampling and treatment of parasites
and other pests can lead to a delay in treatment for the animals in the facility. Correct by ensuring that sampling and
treatment of parasites and other pests in accordance with a schedule approved by the Attending Veterinarian is followed.
