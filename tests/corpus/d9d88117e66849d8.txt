3.81
ENVIRONMENT ENHANCEMENT TO PROMOTE PSYCHOLOGICAL WELL-BEING.
There is no program for environmental enhacement approved by an attending veterinarian.
All exhibitors must develop, document, and follow an appropriate plan for environmental enhancement adequate to
promote the psychological well-being of nonhuman primates. The plan must be in accordance with the currently
accepted professional standards as cited in appropriate professional journals or reference guides, and as directed
by the attending veterinarian.
Develop a program for enrichment for the non-human primates held at the facility. All enrichment must be
documented by the facility and the plan must be approved by the attending veterinarian.
3.84(b)(2) REPEAT
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
The primary enclosures for the ring-tailed lemurs and vervets are made of wood and rusted wire. Rusted metal and
wood are not impervious to moisture and therefore cannot be sanitized.
Indoor primary enclosures must be sanitized at least once every 2 weeks and as often as necessary to prevent an
excessive accumulation of dirt, debris, waste, food waste, excreta, or disease hazard.
Replace the wire with new wire and replace or seal the wood so that it can be sanitized a minimum of once every
two weeks for the health of the non-human primates.

FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
One of the shelters for the camels has a holes in it. There is a small group of goats separated from the rest of the
farm animals. This small group of goats has a shelter with soiled, frozen bedding inside. There are two adult and
one juvenile Cape/African Crested porcupines housed outdoors with one Igloo shelter with no bedding in it.
Shelter appropriate to the local climatic conditions for the species concerned shall be provided for all animals kept
outdoors to afford them protection and to prevent discomfort to such animals. A sufficient amount of clean, dry,
bedding shall be in each enclosure to create a barrier between the animal and the cold ground.
Temperatures have dipped below zero in Michigan. Appropriate shelter for the climactic condition in Michigan must
be provided for all animals to give them protection from the elements and prevent discomfort. Bedding shall be
sufficient, clean and dry to provide insulation from the cold ground.
3.127(d) REPEAT
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
There is no perimeter fence for the camels.
All outdoor housing facilities (i.e., facilities not entirely indoors) must be enclosed by a perimeter fence that is of
sufficient height to keep animals and unauthorized persons out. The fence must be constructed so that it protects
the animals in the facility by restricting animals and unauthorized persons from going through it or under it and
having contact with the animals in the facility, and so that it can function as a secondary containment system for the
animals in the facility.
A perimeter fence must be constructed or a perimeter fence variance must be requested.
