3.27(b)
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
*** At the time of the inspection a guinea pig was being housed in an outdoor open enclosure with rabbits. Guinea
pigs can not be housed outdoors since they are not acclimated to the temperatures in the local climate. This guinea
pig needs to be moved to an enclosure that is indoors for its health and well being.
Correct by: Oct 2, 2015
3.33(b)
CLASSIFICATION AND SEPARATION.
*** The guinea pig housed in the outdoor enclosure was housed with several rabbits. Guinea pigs should not be
housed with rabbits. The vitamin C requirement that guinea pigs need in their diet can be a danger to rabbits. These
animals need to be separated for their well being.
Correct by: Oct 2, 2015
An exit briefing was conducted with the licensee at the time of the inspection.
