2.78(a)
HEALTH CERTIFICATION AND IDENTIFICATION.
The licensee acquired 4 adult male Catons from North Carolina in October 2015 and transported the dogs across
state lines into Missouri without health certificates. Health certificates are needed to ensure an animal is healthy and
free from infectious disease. The licensee shall obtain a valid health certificate executed and issued by a licensed
veterinarian prior to transporting dogs in commerce, across state lines. TO BE CORRECTE BY: 12/9/2015
3.1(c)(1)(2)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
At least 3 of the metal support beams of the indoor enclosures of the adult housing facility were rusted through and
had open areas of sharp, jagged metal exposed at animal level. Rusted, sharp metal with jagged edges could injure
the dogs. The licensee shall ensure all enclosures are free of jagged edges or sharp points that might injure the
animals. TO BE CORRECTED BY: 12/11/2015
3.1(c)(1)HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The carpet squares used to cover the flooring of the raised whelping enclosures were soiled, torn and smelled of
ammonia. Adequate cleaning and proper maintenance of surfaces is a crucial aspect of animal husbandry and aids
in disease prevention. The licensee shall ensure the surfaces of housing facilities are constructed in a manner and
made of materials that allow them to be readily cleaned and sanitized, or that they are removed or replaced when
worn or soiled. TO BE CORRECTED BY:12/11/2015

3.6(a)(2)(10)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
The raised flooring sections of at least two of the indoor enclosures of the adult housing facility were no longer
secured and had gaps that the dogs feet and legs could fit through. The animals could become entrapped or
injured due to these gaps. The licensee shall ensure that the flooring of each enclosure is constructed in a manner
that protects the dogs' and cats' feet and legs from injury, and that, if of mesh or slatted construction, do not allow
the dogs' feet to pass through any openings in the floor. TO BE CORRECTED BY: 12/14/2015
