HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
*The southwestern end enclosure of the Old Whelping Building, housing four adult dogs, had an approximate 3 feet
long by ½ inch wide crack in the concrete flooring. There was black debris in the crack.
In the Whelping Building, there were four plastic whelping boxes used for three adult dogs and 15 puppies. These
boxes were excessively chewed and scratched along the top. There was brown grime in the chewed areas.
The Breeding House had a plastic dog door for an enclosure housing two adult dogs that was excessively chewed
and scratched along the top.
The Old Whelping Kennel had the plastic framing for two dog doors. This framing was excessively chewed and
scratched around the door. There was six adult dogs in the enclosures with the framing. There was a plastic box in
one of the enclosures of this Kennel that was excessively chewed and scratched along the top. This enclosure that
had three adult dogs had brown grime in the chewed are of the box. These surfaces are not being maintained and
prevent required cleaning and sanitization. Thus, increasing the disease potential for the dogs.
All surfaces must be maintained and kept in good repair. This will allow for required cleaning and sanitization for the
prevention of diseases.
To be corrected by, 1-November-2016
