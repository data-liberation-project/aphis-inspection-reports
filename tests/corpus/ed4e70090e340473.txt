3.31(b)

Sanitation.
There are accumulations of cobwebs, dust, dirt, and debris on the ceiling, walls, windows, sills, and racks. Animal facilities
must be kept clean to ensure good quality of air for the animals and to prevent unnecessary disease risks. Ineffective
cleaning exposes the animals to disease hazards and health problems. Correct by ensuring the premises are kept clean,
including: removing accumulations of cobwebs, dust, dirt, and debris from surfaces; routing dusting off and cleaning all
surfaces on the inside of facility, and maintaining a level of general cleanliness to support good animal husbandry and
health. Correct by January 24, 2021.

This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the licensee.
