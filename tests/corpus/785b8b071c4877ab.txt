2.31(c)(3)

Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC).
The semiannual program review from November 2019 and June 2020 noted minor deficiencies regarding the cage
washer. The reports did not contain a schedule with dates for correcting these deficiencies. Deficiencies noted during the
semiannual program review must include a plan and timeframe for correction, in order to ensure the timely and complete
correction of problems identified. Correct on September 25th, 2020, by including reasonable and specific plans and
correction dates for all deficiencies identified during future semiannual program reviews and facility inspections.
2.31(c)(2)

Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC).
Protocol 15101501, approved on October 25, 2018, states that sheep will be housed for the duration of the protocol at a
facility not owned or operated by the University. This facility was not included in the semiannual facility inspections and
program reviews conducted in November 2019 or June 2020. Inspection of animal housing facilities is important to ensure
animals are housed in facilities that are appropriate, safe, and in compliance with the applicable regulations under the
Animal Welfare Act. Correct on September 25th, 2020, by including all animal housing facilities in future semiannual
program reviews and facility inspections.
2.31(e)(2)



Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC).
Protocols 15101501, 17101206, and 18022801, did not include a rationale or explanation for the species used. A rationale
is required in order to demonstrate the appropriateness of the species selected for use in research. Correct on September
25th, 2020, by including a rationale for the species of animals to be used in research protocols.
2.36(b)(7)

Annual report.
The 2019 annual report submitted by the institutional official to USDA APHIS Animal Care included 0 rabbits and 0 sheep.
The USDA inspection conducted in July 2019 included the observation of two rabbits at the research facility. These
rabbits were not included on the 2019 annual report. Facility representatives stated that these rabbits were used in study
17101206, and should have been reported in column D on the 2019 annual report. Facility representatives reported that in
fiscal year 2019, 4 rabbits were used in total and should have been reported in column D. Facility representatives also
reported that 6 sheep were used in fiscal year 2019 which should have been reported in column D. Correct reporting of
information on the annual report is required so that the public and Congress can have knowledge of the number, type,
and pain category of animals used- or held for use in research. Correct by September 30th, 2020, by submitting a
corrected annual report to USDA APHIS Animal Care.

This inspection and exit interview were conducted with facility representatives.
