2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
The following medications were expired and located in a medicine cabinet comingled with other unexpired
medications: Nitrofurazone ointment (expiration date of 08/16/15), Meloxicam suspension (expiration date of
11/17), Vetropolycin ophthalmic ointment (expiration date of 06/17), Benazepril tablets (expiration date of 11/26/17)
and Furosemide tablets (expiration date of 11/26/17). Failure to properly label medications or other medical
products as being expired and storing them with unexpired medications may lead to the unintentional use on
regulated animals. As part of the program of adequate veterinary care, a system of timely and appropriate
identification, separation, and labeling of expired medications must be in place to ensure the health and well-being
of the animals.
Correct by 03/19/2018.
This inspection and exit briefing were conducted with a facility representative.
