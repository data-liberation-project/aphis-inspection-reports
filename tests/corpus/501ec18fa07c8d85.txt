3.1(c)(1)(2)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
Sharp points and edges
In an outdoor enclosure, containing 2 dogs, the metal self feeder has sharp points and edges in the trough area.
There is also a coated wire used to secure the self feeder that has a sharp point exposed at the dogs eye level
while feeding. These sharp points and edges could cause injury to the dogs. The licensee must repair or replace
this feeder to remove the sharp points and edges. The licensee must ensure that all enclosures are free of sharp
points and edges to prevent the dogs from becoming injured.
To be corrected by: 30 Mar 16
3.4(b)(3)
OUTDOOR HOUSING FACILITIES.
Wind and Rain break
In 2 outdoor enclosures, housing 4 dogs, there is no wind and rain break at the entrance to the shelter. A wind and
rain break is essential to protect the dogs from the effects of wind and/or rain. The licensee must provide these
shelters, and ensure that all outdoor shelters, are provided with an effective wind and rain break at all times.
To be corrected by: 30 Mar 16
3.4(b)
OUTDOOR HOUSING FACILITIES.
Shade
In an outdoor enclosure, containing 1 dog, there is no shade provided. Shade is necessary to protect the dog from
the direct rays of the sun. The licensee must provide this enclosure and ensure that all outdoor enclosures are
provided with a shade structure to protect the dogs from the direct rays of the sun during all daylight hours.
To be corrected by: 30 Mar 16
3.9(b)
FEEDING.Food receptacle
In 2 outdoor enclosures, containing 4 dogs, there are metal self feeders that have wet food and caked food on the
inside area. Wet and caked food can become moldy and cause illness to the dogs. The licensee must remove the
wet and caked food from and clean the self feeders. The licensee must also protect the food receptacles from rain
and/or snow.
The licensee must ensure that all food receptacles are protected from the rain and snow and ensure that measures
must be taken to ensure that there is no molding, deterioration, and caking of feed in the self feeders.
To be corrected by: 30 Mar 16
Inspection and a verbal exit interview was conducted with licensee and ACI Stephanie Osborne.
