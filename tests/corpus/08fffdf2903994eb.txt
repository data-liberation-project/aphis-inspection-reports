OUTDOOR HOUSING FACILITIES.
The dog house provided for the two adult dogs in the outdoor enclosure did not have bedding. The National
Weather Service recorded low temperatures of 43 degrees F the previous night. Bedding in outdoor shelters or dog
houses assists the dogs in retaining their body temperature. The licensee shall ensure the shelters in outdoor
facilities for dogs contain clean, dry, bedding material if the ambient temperature is below degrees F and additional
clean, dry bedding is required when the temperature is 35 degrees F or lower. TO BE CORRECTED BY: 12/8/2015
as instructed during the inspection.

WATERING.
One adult female did not have a water receptacle in her enclosure. When the inspectors requested the licensee
supply water to the dog, she drank continiously for a length of time; demonstrating excessive thirst. Additionally,
there were at least 5 of the water receptacles in adult enclosures that had green algea covering thier interior
surface. Adaquate, potable water supplied in clean recepticales is nessicary for animals to remain healthy. If water
is not made continually available, the licensee shall provide water as often as necessary to ensure the health and
well-being of the animals. Water receptacles must be kept clean and sanitized in accordance with Sec. 3.11(b) of
this subpart. TO BE CORRECTED:12/7/2015 as instructed during the inspection.
