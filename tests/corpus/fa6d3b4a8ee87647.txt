2.40(a)(1)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
The required Program of Veterinary Care (PVC) was not available for review at the time of inspection. Each
dealer/exhibitor must employ an Attending Veterinarian (AV) under formal arrangements, who must provide
adequate veterinary care to all regulated species of animals at the facility. The formal arrangements between the
facility and its AV must include a written PVC and regularly scheduled visits to the facility's premises. In order to
ensure adequate veterinary care for regulated animals, the facility must find/update/create its PVC, and have it
available for USDA inspection at all times.
Correct by: May 12, 2017

A female ring-tailed lemur was observed with a reddened/hairless lesion at the upper area of the base of it's tail;

3.81
ENVIRONMENT ENHANCEMENT TO PROMOTE PSYCHOLOGICAL WELL-BEING.
The required Environmental Enhancement Plan (EEP) for nonhuman primates (NHP) was not available for review at
the time of inspection. Dealers and exhibitors, must develop, document, and follow an appropriate EEP adequate to
promote the psychological well-being of NHP. The plan must be in accordance with the currently accepted
professional standards as cited in appropriate professional journals or reference guides, and as directed by the
facility's attending veterinarian (AV). The EEP must be made available to APHIS upon request during facilityinspections. Also, the plan must be reviewed and signed to indicate that implementation is under the direction of
the AV. Lastly, in order to ensure adequate veterinary care for regulated NHP, the facility must find/update/create
its EEP, and have it available for USDA inspection at all times.
Correct by: May 12, 2017
