3.53(a)(1)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
A raised wire enclosure housing seven rabbits in the main barn has broken wire that extends approximately one half
the length of the top. The enclosure partially gaps in the damaged area and may not be structurally sound. Primary
enclosures shall be maintained in good repair and structurally sound to protect the rabbits from injury, to contain
them and keep predators out. This enclosure should be repaired or replaced.
Correct by: August 31, 2014.
3.53(c)(2)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
Seven rabbits weighing less than 4.4 lbs. were housed in a primary enclosure that provided 6 square feet (2 ft x 3
ft).of floor space. The minimum required floor space for these seven animals should be 10.5 square feet. An
enclosure measuring 6 sq. ft. is only adequate to house four rabbits weighing less than 4.4 lbs.
Each rabbit will be housed in a primary enclosure that at least provides the minimum amount of floor space
necessary to allow normal postural adjustments and adequate freedom of movement. Alternative housing
arrangements must be provided allowing each rabbit appropriate space.Correct by: August 24, 2014.
