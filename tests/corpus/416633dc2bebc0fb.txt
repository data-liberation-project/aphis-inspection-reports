2.40(b)(2) REPEAT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
An adult, female, white and brown Olde Bulldog (#238) has an abnormal, thickened lesion about the size of a
marble on its left hind foot. The affected area is reddened and firm to the touch.
An adult, tri-colored, female Cavalier Spaniel (tattoo #51) has a right eye with an abnormal appearance. The white
part of the eye is extremely reddened and there is a creamy yellowish discharge coming from the eye. A
greenish-yellow, dried crusty material is matted in the fur surrounding the eye.
An adult, white, male Woodle (tattoo #02) has a swollen and reddened sore between its toes on its left hind foot
near the top right side. The affected area is approximately pea sized, reddened, moist and firm to the touch. The fur
surrounding the sore is wet in appearance.
An adult, red, female Shar-pei (tattoo #15) has an estimated 4x8 inch sore on her neck and chest region. It’s moist
and inflamed with blister like bubbles on the skin. The hair remaining in the area is clumped together and wet. An
approximately quarter size red lesion is located behind the left arm pit. Dark colored, dried material is located along
the outer areas of the lesion.
Conditions such as these can be painful and can lead to further health complications. The animals listed above
must be examined by a licensed veterinarian in order to ensure that an accurate diagnosis is obtained and an
appropriate treatment plan is developed and followed. This information, including the diagnosis, treatment and
resolution of the condition, must be documented and made available to the inspector upon request. The licensee
must ensure that all animals receive appropriate veterinary care at all times.
