2.40(b)(2) DIRECT REPEAT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
***An adult female Shih Tzu, USDA tag 66, was identified as having a lesion on top of the front right foot. The
lesion was approximately 1cm x 1 cm and bright pink and red hairless tissue was exposed. The exposed tissue
appeared to be reddened and inflamed. The animal appeared to be walking normally on this foot, however would
hold the foot off the ground when resting.
***An adult male Shih Tzu, USDA tag 78, was identified as having an open lesion between the toes on the front right
foot. The exposed tissue appeared to be reddened and inflamed. The animal appeared to be walking normally on
this foot, however would hold the foot off the ground when resting.
*** The five year old female Shih Tzu, USDA tag 65, noted to have multiple health issues during the October 1,
2015 inspection was addressed by a licensed veterinarian. The animal's skin and eye issues were addressed by
the veterinarian on October 2, 2015 and the licensee has followed appropriate veterinary instruction. The animal
was also groomed to address the long toe nails identified during the inspection. The dental issues identified were
not addressed by the veterinarian.
The issues noted may cause unnecessary pain and distress to the animal. The program of veterinary care provides
instructions as to the proper care of the animals and must be followed to ensure that adequate care is given to the
animals in a timely manner. Daily observations of this animal are also needed to monitor the progression of any
treatments given by the attending veterinarian. The licensee shall use appropriate methods to prevent, control,
diagnose and treat diseases and injuries.
The Inspection was conducted with the licensee's wife, Randy Coleman, ACI and Michael Neafsey, VMO. The exit
interview was conducted with licensee's wife and Michael Neafsey, VMO.
