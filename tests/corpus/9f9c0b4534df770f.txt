2.40(a)(1)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
Written program of Veterinary Care is not current. Licensee stated she is using an attending vet that is not on the
latest available written program. Written program must be completed and signed by both the licensee and the
current attending veterinarian.
To be corrected by next inspection.
2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
Several dogs were not adequately groomed, with large mats visible, and skin not able to be observed. Matting can
lead to skin problems, and prevent adequate observation and evaluation of the health of an animal on a daily basis.
Dogs that were most matted included Gadget, Ripley, and the Airedale cross. A system of routine grooming must be
in place.
To be corrected by next inspection.
2.50(c)
TIME AND METHOD OF IDENTIFICATION.
Not all dogs and cats had identification tags. Tags were on the wrong enclosures, and did not match the housed in
the enclosure. Several ID tags did not have identifying numbers. Animals must be identified with a tag, and a
matching tag must be available in the records and to accompany the animal off the property. A system of such ID
must be in place and maintained.
To be corrected by next inspection.
2.75(a)(1)
RECORDS: DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS.
Acquisition / disposition records are incomplete. The most recent acquisitions, such as Flash, Calvin, and Remy are
not included in the records. The most recent dispositions do not have the dates of disposition. Each exhibitor shall
make, keep, and maintain records or forms which fully and correctly disclose the required information concerningeach dog or cat purchased or otherwise acquired, owned, held, or otherwise in his or her possession or under his or
her control, or which is transported, euthanized, sold, or otherwise disposed of by that dealer or exhibitor. Records
must be current and maintained.
To be corrected by next inspection.
3.1(e)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
Problems were noted in several food prep and storage areas.
-Sweet feed for goats had a bug infestation present, with numerous small black bugs in the container.
-Rice hulls used as bedding for Guinea Pigs and Hamsters were stored in open bags on the floor in the garage area
that also houses dogs and cats.
-Garage food area had a dirty cutting board, an open bag of bird seed, and rodent droppings all in close proximity
on the main food prep surface. No pest control measures were observed in this area.
-Food shed had rodent droppings in drawers and on baseplate of the wall studs. Some pest control was present in
area.
Supplies of food and bedding must be stored in a manner that protects the supplies from spoilage, contamination,
and vermin infestation. All open supplies of food and bedding must be kept in leakproof containers with tightly fitting
lids to prevent contamination and spoilage. A system of storage and regular cleaning of food prep areas must be in
place to protect food and bedding supplies.
To be corrected by next inspection.
3.6(a)(2)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
Lower kennel area: old rusted sink is present along the perimeter of the large main exercise yard, with ragged rusty
edges possibly accessible to the animals. Sink also has exposed fiberglass insulation present. Primary enclosures
must be constructed and maintained so that they have no sharp points or edges that could injure the dogs and cats.
A system of maintenance must be in place to ensure there are no possible hazards to the animals housed in an
area.
To be corrected by next inspection.
3.58
CLASSIFICATION AND SEPARATION.
Rabbits are currently housed in an enclosure with chickens and Scarlet Macaws. Rabbits shall not be housed in the
same primary enclosure with any other species of animals unless required for scientific reasons.
To be corrected by next inspection.This inspection and exit briefing were conducted with a facility representative.
