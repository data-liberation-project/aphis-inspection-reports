3.131(a) REPEAT
SANITATION.
A moderate to strong odor of organic material/ excreta was noted in the indoor housing room. The opossum has
free range of this room and it is her primary housing enclosure.
The litter box provided for the animal is moderately full and 7 out of 30 empty, soiled, animal enclosures used for
the rehabilitation animals have not been cleaned since they were last occupied.The build-up of excreta and other
organic material can increase disease hazards and odors in animal areas.
Excreta should be removed from primary enclosures as often as necessary to prevent contamination of the animals
contained therein and to minimize disease hazards and to reduce odors.Original correction date: June 21, 2014

SANITATION.
The following was noted in the opossum indoor housing room:
-There is a moderate accumulation of old food and other debris collecting beneath and in-between at least 4 of the
animal cages.
-There are 7out of 30 empty, soiled, animal enclosures used for the rehabilitation animals that have not been

-There is a heavy collection of cob webs in the window sills, in the corners and behind the cages.
- The floors, including the linoleum and the rug, are soiled with food and other debris.
- There are at least 2 areas where the linoleum tile has peeled up and the underlying concrete floor is exposed.
- The exercise wheel appears soiled.
- There is a mild to moderate amount of small dead insects and other debris collecting on the bottom shelf of the
refrigerator.
- At least two walls have areas that appear to be splattered with food.
Failure to keep animal rooms clean and sanitary may adversely affect the health and well-being of the animals.
Premises (buildings and grounds) shall be kept clean and in good repair in order to protect the animals from injury
and to facilitate the prescribed husbandry practices set forth in this subpart.
