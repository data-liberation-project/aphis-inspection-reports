2.131(c)(1)
HANDLING OF ANIMALS.
The enclosures for the raccoons, north american porcupines, an arctic fox, and a young coyote did not have public
barriers in place and visitors could stick their fingers into the enclosures and possibly contact the animals.
Adequate public barriers must be in place to protect the public and to protect the animals. Ensure that sufficient
distance and/or barriers are in place between exhibited animals and the general viewing public to assure the safety
of the animals and the public.
Correct by 6/12/17.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with facility representatives.
