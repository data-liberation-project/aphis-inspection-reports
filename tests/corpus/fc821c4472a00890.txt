3.84(a)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
The primates primary enclosures have dirt floors and have shelter houses that require to be cleaned on daily basis.
Dirt floors must be spot-cleaned with sufficient frequency to ensure all animals the freedom to avoid contact with
excreta, or as often as necessary to reduce disease hazards, insects, pests, and odors.
To be corrected by 6-2-2017.
3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
One rabbit exhibit had underground wire that was broken and the animal was starting to dig a hole into the ground.
The raccoons were missing house boxes which are recommended for them to hide during the day and they were
laying on the dirt ground.
In order to protect the animals from escaping and unnecessary stress housing facilities shall be structurally sound
and shall be maintained in good repair.
To be corrected by 6-2-2017.
3.129(a)
FEEDING.
The feed container in the llama/alpaca exhibit was open and exposed to rain water. It was observed to be caked
and flies were all over the feed.
The food shall be wholesome, palatable, and free from contamination and of sufficient quantity and nutritive value
to maintain all animals in good health.
To be corrected by 5-26-2017.
3.130
WATERING. Water receptacles in some enclosures were observed to be dirty and had hay/dirt mixed with the drinking water
inside the bowls.
All water receptacles shall be kept clean and sanitary in order to avoid health issues that may arise from the animals
drinking dirty water.
To be corrected by 5-26-2017.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the facility representative.
