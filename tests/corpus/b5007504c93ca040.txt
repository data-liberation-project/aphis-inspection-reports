2.1(a)(1)
REQUIREMENTS AND APPLICATION.
On January 8, 2019, while conducting a licensee's site approval at this property/facility, unlicensed regulated activity
was confirmed by this inspector. Animal species covered by the regulations and standards of the Animal Welfare
Act (AWA) both, exotic and domestic animal species were identified by this inspector. The animals' owner name
has been identified in several APHIS Form 7020 (Record of Acquisition, Disposition or Transport of Animals - Other
Than Dogs and Cats) presented for inspection by other USDA licensees.
Any person operating or intending to operate as a dealer, and/or exhibitor, except persons who are exempted from
the licensing requirements under paragraph (a)(3) of this section, must have a valid USDA license as required by
the AWA. The owner/manager of the facility must apply for a license by submitting the required
information/documentation to the USDA-APHIS-AC Regional Director in Fort Collins, Colorado. In the license
application form, the establishment must provide the information requested, including a valid mailing address
through which the owner/manager could be reached at all times, and a valid premises address where animals,
animal facilities, equipment, and records may be inspected for compliance.
After touring the property, this inspector discussed with the facility representative, the need for the owner of
regulated animals to have/obtain a USDA license and compliance with the requirements of the AWA. A packet
containing license application materials and information has been mailed; a booklet of all AWA regulations and
standards was also mailed.
NO REGULATED ACTIVITIES MAY BE CONDUCTED WITHOUT OBTAINING A VALID USDA LICENSE.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with a facility representative.
