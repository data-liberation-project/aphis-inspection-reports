2.50(a)(1)
TIME AND METHOD OF IDENTIFICATION.
* At least seven adult cats had no official identification on them at time of inspection. The licensee is using tags as
the identification of choice. Official identification is needed to ensure each animal(cid:25)s identity can be known with
certainty and to facilitate the ability to trace all cats. The licensee must ensure that all cats are officially identified by
collar, tattoo or microchip means. If the licensee uses microchips then a microchip reader must be made available
to the inspector at time of facility inspection. Correct by 10/17/15.
2.75(a)(1)
RECORDS: DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS.
* No inventory information was available on 26 unweaned kittens. Inventory information is needed to ensure the
individual identity can be known with certainty and to facilitate the ability to trace all kittens. The licensee must
make, keep and maintain complete and accurate inventory records that fully and correctly disclose information on all
animals at the facility. Correct by 10/17/15.
3.1(c)(2)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
* An estimated one foot section of plastic base board was observed separated from the interior enclosure wall.
Twenty five cats are in contact with the affected area located in indoor housing. Surfaces not maintained on a
regular basis cannot be readily cleaned and sanitized. The potential for dirt, hair or dust to collect in the area where
the base board has separated from enclosure wall exist. The licensee must ensure all surfaces are maintained on a
regular basis. In addition, if any surface cannot be readily cleaned and sanitized it must be replaced when worn or
soiled. Correct by 10/1/15.
