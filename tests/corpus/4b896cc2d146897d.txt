2.134(a)

Contingency planning.
The licensee has not developed a written contingency plan. All licensees must have a contingency plan in place to
provide humane care, housing, and transportation for the animals in case of an emergency or disaster situation. The
facility must develop a contingency plan as outlined in Subpart 2.134 Contingency Planning to ensure the safety of
animals and to minimize distress or harm during a disaster or emergency. Correct by October 15, 2022

This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the licensees.

Information on the Contingency Plan was provided to the licensee. Also, the re-license process was discussed.
