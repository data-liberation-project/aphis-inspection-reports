3.53(c)(2)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
There were three rabbits in enclosures measuring 12 inches in height in the holding building. The minimum interior
height of rabbit enclosures is 14 inches. Rabbit enclosures must be at least 14 inches in height to provide adequate
head height to allow the animals to make normal postural adjustments. Rabbit enclosures need to be repaired or
replaced so that they are at least 14 inches in height. Correct by March 23, 2017.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the owner.
