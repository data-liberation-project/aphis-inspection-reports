3.1(c)(3)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
(c) Surfaces- (3) Cleaning
****
In the indoor facility there is an excessive accumulation of hair, dirt, grime, and other organic material caught on the
underside of the metal flooring. On the outside section of the sheltered facility "Trailer House", there is an
excessive amount of hair, dirt, grime and other organic material caught on the underside of the metal flooring. On
the outside section of several of the primary enclosures of the sheltered facility, "Trailer House", there is an
accumulation of hair, dirt, grime, organic material and fecal material along the sides and walls. In all of the outdoor
enclosures of the "Big Building" there is an accumulation of dust, dirt, and hair on top of the shelters. There is a
chain link panel covering the exhaust fan on the wall of one enclosure that has an accumulation of hair, dust and
dirt. This accumulation of hair, dust, dirt, grime, organic material and fecal material can become a disease hazard
to the animals, produce odors, and attract pest and vermin. The licensee must clean the affected areas and ensure
that all surfaces in contact with that animals are spot cleaned daily and sanitized at least once every two weeks or
more often if necessary and all other surfaces of housing facilities are cleaned and sanitized when necessary to
satisfy generally accepted husbandry standards and practices. This affects 101 adult dogs and 54 puppies.
To be corrected by: 15 June 2014
3.6(a)(2)(1)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
Primary enclosures for dogs must meet the following minimum requirements:
(a) General requirements. (2) Primary enclosures must be constructed and maintained so that they: (i) Sharp
points and/or edges
****
In at least 4 enclosures, affecting 9 dogs, there were metal water bowls that had areas of sharp points and edges. In
one enclosure, affecting 2 dogs, the metal divider panel had rusted through at the bottom creating sharp points and
edges. These sharp points and edges create a hazard that could cause injuries to the dogs. Licensee must repair
or replace the affected water bowls and divider panel and ensure that all primary enclosures are free of sharp pointsand edges at all times.
To be corrected by: 14 June 2014
3.11(a) REPEAT
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
(a) Cleaning of primary enclosures.
****
The outside portion of two enclosure on the north side of the "Trailer house" , affecting four dogs, there is an
excessive build up of compacted fecal material that was embedded on the flooring surface. This excessive amount
of fecal material can harbor disease causing organisms and soil the dogs. The licensee must clean the affected
enclosures and ensure that fecal material is removed from primary enclosures daily to prevent an excessive
accumulation of feces and food waste and to reduce disease hazards pests, insects and odors.
This is a repeat non compliant item.
A joint inspection with Iowa Department of Agriculture and the licensee was conducted on 10 June14. An exit
