3.11(d)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
(d) Pest control. An effective program for the control of insects, external parasites affecting dogs and cats, and birds
and mammals that are pests, must be established and maintained so as to promote the health and well-being of the
animals and reduce contamination by pests in animal areas.
* At both ends of the North Building (housing 34 dogs) were swarms of gnats. These swarms of gnats caused
distress by flying around the heads of the dogs and the inspector while inspecting. Pests can carry disease, cause
distress and contaminate food. Licensee must establish an effective program to reduce or eliminate these pests to
minimize food contamination, stress and other disease hazards.
