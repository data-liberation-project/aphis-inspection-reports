3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
In the hog facility, there are two pens (two pigs each) that have holes on the back walls. There is an approximate
hole of about 6 inches x 12 inches in one pen and a circular hole of about 9-10 inches in diameter in the other.
The facility is to create a system to keep the housing facilities in good repair, to be structurally sound and to protect
the animals from injury.

3.127(c)
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
There are two cattle in a pen that the water trough was emptied into the pen. This creates some standing water
which will not rapidly drain from the pen. Standing water can breed insect pests and other microbial diseases.
A suitable method shall be provided to rapidly eliminate excess water. The method of drainage shall comply with
applicable Federal, State, and local laws and regulations relating to pollution control or the protection of the
environment.
To be corrected by 11/5/15
3.131(c)
SANITATION.
Several fly sprays (CV80D, Permectrin II) are being stored on the shelf over the pen of the eight hogs. In order to
protect animals from injury and to facilitate the prescribed husbandry practices (safe storage of pesticides) set forth
in this subpart, all toxic products are to be stored away from the animals. The facility is to create a system tomonitor the use and storage of pesticides.
