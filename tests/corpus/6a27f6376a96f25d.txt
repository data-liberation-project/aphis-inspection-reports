2.131(c)(1)
HANDLING OF ANIMALS.
Some enclosures including goats, cavy, llama, camel, zebra, guinea pigs,etc., did not have an effective barrier that
prevented the public from reaching the animals. The public is allowed to feed the animals without any supervision
from the facility .
During public exhibition, any animal must be handled with sufficient distance and/or barriers between the animal
and the general viewing public so as to assure the safety of animals and the public.
To be corrected by 7-31-2017.
3.75(c)(1)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The wooden surfaces in the spider monkey enclosures was not impermeable to moisture because it was not sealed
and it could not be properly cleaned and sanitized.
The surfaces of housing facilities must be constructed in a manner and made of materials that allow them to be
readily cleaned and sanitized.
To be corrected by 7-31-2017.
3.84(c)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
The spider monkey enclosure had trash (bags, miscellaneous pieces of plastic) inside the enclosure.
Premises including buildings and surrounding grounds, must be kept in order to protect the nonhuman primates
from injury, to facilitate the husbandry practices required in this subpart, Premises must be kept free of
accumulations of trash, junk, waste, and discarded matter.
To be corrected by 7-31-2017.
3.84(d)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
A rat was observed walking by the spider monkey enclosure and multiple holes were observed in the ground whichcould be rodent holes. There were empty feed bags laying around the area and food waste was observe on the
ground which could contribute to the increase population of pest.
An effective program for control of pests must be established and maintained so as to promote the health and
well-being of the animals and reduce contamination by pests in animal areas.
To be corrected by 7-31-2017.
3.125(a) REPEAT
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
Several enclosures had exposed underground wire and the wire was broken (porcupine, cavy, muntjack). Some
enclosures had detached fences (alpacas) with sharp points towards the inside of the enclosure (goats).
The outdoor housing facilities shall be structurally sound and shall be maintained in good repair to protect the
animals from injury and to contain the animals.
3.131(c)
SANITATION.
There were multiple enclosures with plastic bag inside, and foam cups and plastic bags around the enclosure.
Premises (buildings and grounds) shall be kept clean in order to protect the animals from injury and to facilitate the
prescribed husbandry practices set forth in this subpart.
To be corrected by 7-31-2017
3.131(d)
SANITATION.
The guinea pig shelter had an excessive amount of ants crawling out when the roof of the shelter was removed.
A safe and effective program for the control of insects shall be established and maintained.
To be corrected by 7-31-2017.
