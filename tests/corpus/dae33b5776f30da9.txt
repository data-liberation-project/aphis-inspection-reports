2.131(d)(2)

Handling of animals.
During this inspection, no readily identifiable employees were present in the zoo or around the exhibit animals. A
responsible, knowledgeable, and readily identifiable employee or attendant must be present at all times during periods of

3.131(c)

Sanitation.
The Aphis official observed a damaged resting platform, constructed of firehose and metal pipes, placed on the ground in
front of the entrance door to the kitchen of the main building. Overgrown weeds are also present in the building's gutter
and adjacent to this same door. The gutters were cleaned out during the inspection however, miscellaneous items such
as damaged equipment, weeds and other debris on the ground can be a harborage area for unwanted pests. Weeds and
damaged equipment should be removed to keep the grounds clean and in good repair to facilitate the prescribed
husbandry practices.

3.131(d)

Sanitation.
During this inspection, the Aphis official observed numerous Black Vultures roosting on the primary fences of the pot-
bellied pig, Red River hogs, and the Asiatic Black bear. They were also walking around on the grounds of each of these
enclosures and near the animals housed therein. Their droppings are present on the primary enclosure fence rails and
posts that they roost on. Due to the numbers and locations of the birds, their droppings could present a health hazard for
the animals. A safe and effective program to control these birds shall be established and maintained.
