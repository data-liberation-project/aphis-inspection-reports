3.75(c)(1)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The surfaces of the primate enclosure are not made of materials that will allow them to be readily cleaned and
sanitized. The primate enclosure is made out of press board which is not adequate for proper sanitation. Surfaces
for primates must be made of materials that allow for proper sanitation to reduce or prevent disease. The licensee
must replace or repair the surfaces to allow for proper sanitation to protect the health and well-being of the primate.
Correct by 8-31-2017
3.81
ENVIRONMENT ENHANCEMENT TO PROMOTE PSYCHOLOGICAL WELL-BEING.
The licensee acquired a (Lemur) primate and has not developed and documented written environment enrichment
plan. The plan must be adequate to promote the psychological well-being of the primate. This plan must be made
available to APHIS inspectors for review when requested. The licensee must develop a plan by working with the
attending veterinarian and have the plan available for review at all times. Correct by 8-31-2017
3.88(a)
PRIMARY CONVEYANCES (MOTOR VEHICLE, RAIL, AIR, AND MARINE).
The animal cargo space for the (Lemur) primate is not maintained in a manner that protects its health and
well-being. The cargo area of a 1999 van is not constructed in a manner that secures the primary enclosure the
primate is being transported in. Numerous large and small mammal transport enclosures are stacked on top of each
other and are not secured. In case of a sudden stop these enclosures on top would fly forward and could go through
the windshield. The licensee must secure all mammal enclosures to protect their safety when being transported.
Correct by 8-28-2017
3.125(c)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
Open bags of rabbit, fox and pig food were stored next to cleaning materials such as bleach and a box of fly strips.
On the floor in building #1, in the corner were several bags of feed stored next to cleaning materials and one bagwas left open. Food shall be stored adequately to protect such supplies from contamination. The license shall store
all cleaning supplies and products containing chemicals away from food supplies. All food must be stored properly
to protect it from molding and contamination. Correct from this day forward.
3.126(b)
FACILITIES, INDOOR.
The Red Kangaroo room has a lack of proper ventilation. The air vents are covered with dust and dirt and there was
virtually no air flow in this room. This room needs to have increased air flow to reduce odors and allow for proper air
flow to prevent discomfort of the animal at all times. The licensee shall clean the vents and increase air flow and
maintain the cleaning of the vents at all times. Correct from this day forward.
3.128
SPACE REQUIREMENTS.
A plastic or rubber type tub is too small of an enclosure for an African Crested Porcupine. The adult porcupine is
nearly half the size of the enclosure and does not have adequate freedom of movement. Inadequate space may
cause stress and abnormal behavior patterns. The licensee must house this porcupine in a larger enclosure to
protect the health and well-being of the porcupine. Correct by 8-28-2017
3.131(d)
SANITATION.
The Hedge Hog room has (10) fly strips covered with dead flies. (Numerous flies were noted by the inspectors in
this area) Fly strips must be replaced as often as needed with new ones to control these insects. A safe and
effective program for pest control must be established and maintained at all times. The licensee must remove and
replace all fly strips to control the numerous flies within and throughout the facility. Correct by 8-28-2017
