3.53(c)(2)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
The primary enclosure housing a rabbit weighing 3.1 kg provides 2.27 square feet of floor space. The enclosure
measures 21 in (l) x 13 in (w). Rabbits weighing 2-4 kg must be provided with a minimum of 3.0 square feet of floor
space as required under the Animal Welfare Act. Compliance with space requirements are necessary to ensure the
species can make normal postural adjustments, express species typical behaviors and provide for adequate
freedom of movement. Primary enclosures for rabbits must meet the minimum space requirements.
