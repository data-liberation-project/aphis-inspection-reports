3.28(c)(1)(iii)

Primary enclosures.
Space. Insufficient space was being provided to guinea pigs in the following enclosures in room #2:

Enclosure # 42 was housing at least 26 adult guinea pigs.
Enclosure # 55 was housing 25 adult guinea pigs.
Enclosure # 59 was housing 22 adult guinea pigs.

These three enclosure each measure 36 inches by 48 inches or 1728 square inches of floor space. Guinea pigs weighing
over 350 grams require at least 101 square inches of floor space each. Maximum number of weaning guinea pigs
weighing over 350 grams and adult guinea pigs in this size enclosure is 17.

Inadequate space can cause stress, overcrowding, and can increase aggression in animals which can result in ear
chewing, bites, and other injuries from fighting. Licensee must ensure that guinea pigs are provided with at least the
minimum amount of floor space per animal to prevent unnecessary stress, discomfort, and aggression. Correct by August
21, 2022.


This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the licensee.
