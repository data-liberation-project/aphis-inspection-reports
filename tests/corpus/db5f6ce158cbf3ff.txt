3.1(f)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The waste disposal drain for the wash-downs of the sheltered facility is clogged. There was dirty water and waste
covering the floor in the area of the drain and an abundance of flies were attracted to the area. The drain is directly
beside an enclosure which houses an adult, female English Bulldog. Waste that is not properly disposed of attracts
flies and is a source of bacteria and disease. The licensee shall ensure that the waste disposal system functions
correctly to remove waste from the facility and prevent odor, pests and disease risks. TO BE CORRECTED BY:
6/24/2016
3.3(a) REPEAT
SHELTERED HOUSING FACILITIES.
The indoor portion of the sheltered facility housing both English Bulldog adults and puppies measured 86 degrees F
at dog level at 11:30am. At 4pm the temperature had reached 90.2 degrees F when measured in the same
enclosure. The facility was cited for temperatures reaching 94.1 degrees on 6/20/2016. The temperature outside the
kennel was 96.5 degrees F. Heat stress can lead to serious health complications and may lead to death. The
licensee shall ensure that the sheltered part of housing facility is sufficiently cooled to protect the dogs from
temperature or humidity extremes and to provide for their health and well- being. The ambient temperature must not
must not rise above 85 degrees F for more than 4 consecutive hours when dogs are present.
This inspection was conducted with a facility representative. The exit briefing was conducted with both the facility
representative and the licensee.
