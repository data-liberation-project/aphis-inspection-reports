2.40(a)(1)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
The Program of Veterinary Care (PVC) was last updated in April of 2014 and states that the veterinarian will make
annual visits to the premises. These visits are needed to ensure that the veterinarian is familiar with the facility, its
husbandry practices, and capabilities. That knowledge allows the veterinarian to make the best recommendations to
assure the for maintaining the health and welfare of the animals. Correct by having the veterinarian visit the
premises and discuss medications and procedures at least yearly from this point forward. Correct By: 30 May 2015
3.31(a)(2)
SANITATION.
One enclosure containing 21 weaned guinea pigs had no visible bedding and the floor was covered with dried
feces. Weaned guinea pigs were last picked up by the broker 1 May 2015 indicating that these animals may not
have had their enclosure cleaned in 12 days. The animals appeared healthy and the fecal pellets and floor were dry
but contamination from feces could not be avoided by the animals in the enclosure. There were clean and newly
bedded enclosures available at the time of inspection and the animals were moved to a new enclosure. Correct by
ensuring Primary enclosures are cleaned and sanitized often enough to prevent an accumulation of excreta or
debris: Provided, however, That such enclosures shall be sanitized at least once every 2 weeks. Correct from this
point forward.
An exit interview was conducted with facility owner.
