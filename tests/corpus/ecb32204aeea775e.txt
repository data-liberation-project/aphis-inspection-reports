3.53(a)(1)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
** The wire flooring of a cage containing 3 young rabbits is no longer attached to the frame of the cage and is
sagging. This gap opening can allow a small mammal access into their enclosure, allow the rabbits to escape or
allow the rabbits to become wedged in between the frame and wire flooring. The rabbits primary enclosure shall be
structurally sound and maintained in good repair to protect the animals from injury, contain them, and to keep
predators out. Correct by 11/23/2015
The inspection and exit briefing was conducted with the licensee.
