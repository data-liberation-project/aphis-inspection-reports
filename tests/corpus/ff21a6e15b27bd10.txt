3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
1. The small clawed otters are in an enclosure that has a pool. However, the wood behind the waterfall outlet was
damaged and had peeling paint present. There was also a door to the right of the waterfall that leads into the den
that is also in need of attention. The slats of the wood door have begun to detach from the base. There are now
gaps present. The items in this enclosure will need to be addressed to ensure that the animals are securelycontained and other animals are not allowed access.
***To Be Corrected By: May 23, 2015
2. The Giraffe barn is in need of repair. There is a hole in the ceiling in the back, right corner. Although the giraffe
does not have direct access to this area it will need to be repaired to ensure the integrity of the ceiling.
***To Be Corrected By: May 23, 2015
3. The shelter barn for the camels is also in need of repair. The left side of the shelter has a wood door that is
covered with wood planks. However, a few wood planks are now missing and the top 12 inches of the door have
splintered and holes are now present. To ensure that the camels are not injured on this door it will need to be
repaired.
***To Be Corrected By: May 23, 2015
4. In the mini cow enclosure there is a 4 ft x 4 ft chain link gate between the shelter barn and the primary fence.
However, the bottom of this gate had a few pieces of chain link that had detached from the bottom pole and were
protruding into the enclosure. To ensure the safety of the cows this gate will need to be addressed.
***To Be Corrected By: May 17, 2015
5. The elk are housed in 2 adjacent pens. There is a solid wood fence that splits the pens in half. However, the
wooden door and the wood panel to the right have ~ 12 inch holes present. To ensure the safety of these animals
these areas will need to be addressed.
***To Be Corrected By: May 30, 2015
6. The African Crested Porcupine is being housed in an enclosure that has one metal wall. However, there is a
small area on the bottom right of that wall that is rusty and jagged. To ensure the safety of this animal this area will
need to be addressed.
***To Be Corrected By: May 23, 2015
7. The muntjacs and grey kangaroo are housed in a pen with small wooden shelter boxes. However, one shelter
has a wood slatted floor. At the time of this inspection it was noted that a few of the slats had begun to detach from
the wood frame and were bowing upward. These shelter boxes will need to be repaired to ensure the safety of the
animals.
***To Be Corrected By: May 30, 2015
8. The reindeer pen is also in need of attention. There is a door that leads into the barn that has begun to
delaminate. A section of the laminate is pulling away from the doors core material and a gap is now present. The
core of the door also appears to be water damaged. To ensure the integrity of the door, this door will need to be
addressed.
***To Be Corrected By: May 23, 20159. There are several fallow deer in a pen on the right side of the road. These deer have access to several shelter
