HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.

3.75(c)(3)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The nonhuman primate enclosures had accumulations of fecal material on the dirt floors and the wooden resting
shelves. Contact with excreta may expose the nonhuman primates to disease hazards and attract unwanted pests
and vermin. Surfaces that come in contact with nonhuman primates must be spot-cleaned daily and sanitized in
accordance with Sec. 3.84 of this subpart to prevent accumulation of excreta or disease hazards. Floors made of
dirt, and planted enclosures must be raked or spot-cleaned with sufficient frequency to ensure all animals the
freedom to avoid contact with excreta. All surfaces of housing facilities must be cleaned and sanitized when
necessary to satisfy generally accepted husbandry standards and practices.
Correct by March 7, 2014.
