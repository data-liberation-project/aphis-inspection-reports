2.40(b)(3)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
Several dogs had engorged ticks on their bodies. The licensee was not aware of this problem. Ticks can carry
diseases that are harmful to the health of the dogs. The licensee must do daily observations of all dogs to assess
their health and well-being.
To be corrected August 6, 2019.
3.11(c)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
Many of the outdoor enclosures had excessively high weeds and grass inside the enclosures or along the fence line
of the enclosures. Tall weeds and grass provide hiding areas for pests such as ticks, fleas, mice, and snakes. The
licensee must control the weeds and grass to facilitate pest control and cleaning to protect the health and well-being
of the dogs.
To be corrected by August 8, 2019.
3.11(d)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
At least 5 adult dogs had engorged ticks on their bodies. Ticks carry diseases that can affect the health of the dogs.
The licensee must establish and maintain an effective pest control program for the dogs and premises.
To be corrected by August 13, 2019.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the licensee.
