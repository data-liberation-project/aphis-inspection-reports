3.56(a)(1)
SANITATION.
***The rabbit cages have a dusty coating with hair and excreta. Inadequate cleaning of the primary enclosures
increases the risk of bacterial growth and other disease hazard. The enclosures shall be cleaned and kept
reasonably free of hair, excreta, cobwebs and other debris. Correct by April 2, 2014.
3.56(c)
SANITATION.
***The building housing the chinchilla's, rabbits and guinea pigs has a build-up of hair, debris, shavings, dust, etc.
on the floors, table and cage rack. The same can be found on old food storage containers or bins beneath the table
and racks holding the cages. There are other supplies and items to be discarded stored around the cages and on
the floor. Unclean conditions increase disease hazards, the risk of contamination of food and water, and the
presence of insects and vermin. The animal building shall be cleaned, items not necessary for the care of the
animals should be removed to add in proper husbandry thereby protecting the health and well-being of the animals.
Correct by April 9, 2014.
