WATERING.
There were accumulations of dirt and debris by the automatic water valves, wire surfaces, and PVC drainage pipes
under the valves. These accumulations on the water valves, wire surfaces, and drainage pipes exposes the guinea
pigs to potential infections with bacteria, fungi, and parasites. Inadequate cleaning and sanitation of the automatic
water valves, wire surfaces, and drainage pipes and the surrounding areas can result in contamination of the guinea
pig colony. Correct by keeping the water valves, wire surfaces, and drainage pipes and surrounding areas sanitized
when dirty to ensure the health and well-being of the guinea pigs and that such receptacles be sanitized at least
once every two weeks. Correct by September 7, 2017.
3.31(b)
SANITATION.
Housekeeping. The floor contains accumulations of dirt, debris, and grime. There are accumulations of cobwebs on
wire surfaces and ceiling of guinea pig enclosures. Animal facilities must be kept clean to ensure good quality of air
for the animals and to prevent unnecessary disease risks. Ineffective cleaning exposes the animals to disease
hazards and health problems. Correct by ensuring the premises are kept clean, including removing cobwebs,
routinely sanitizing all surfaces on the inside facility including the floor, and maintaining a level of general
cleanliness to support good animal husbandry and health. Correct by September 9, 2017.
