2.1(a)(1) CRITICAL
REQUIREMENTS AND APPLICATION.
The individual identified above was observed exhibiting animals during the Eden Prairie, MN (Staring Lake) Outdoor
Center Open House event on 7 April 2018. This APHIS inspector observed 2 cougars, 1 wolf and 1 fox in primary
enclosures set up behind a barrier made of caution tape and orange cones allowing the public attending the open
house event to view the animals.

This individual does not hold a valid USDA Class "C" exhibitor license. This individual had been licensed with USDA
as a Class “C” exhibitor however the license (41-C-0122) was revoked in 2012 and the individual was issued a
cease and desist order.

Per this Section of the Regulations, any person operating or intending to operate as an exhibitor must have a valid
license with the USDA. The possession of a valid USDA license demonstrates that the premises, vehicles,
equipment, and the care of the animals comply with the Regulations and Standards as set forth in the AWA to
ensure the health and well-being of the animals.

USDA regulated activities may not be conducted without a USDA license.

Inspector spoke with the individual but did not conduct a formal exit interview.

End of report.
