2.40(a)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
A female Shiba Inu identified by tattoo number 40 and a female Shiba Inu identified by tattoo number 22 were both
shot and killed by the facility representative December 22, 2015. The facility's program of veterinary care states
euthanasia will be carried out by the attending veterinarian using Beuthanasia.
Gunshot is not an approved humane method of routine euthanasia for dogs according to the AVMA Guidelines on
Euthanasia. Inappropriate methods of euthanasia can result in distress and or pain to the animal and can be a
safety hazard. The licensee must follow the attending veterinarian's instructions in the program of veterinary care
regarding euthanasia and must ensure all methods of euthanasia are appropriate and consistent with the provision
of adequate veterinary care. To be corrected: February 8, 2016.
2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
An adult blonde female Shiba Inu identified by tattoo number #79 has a problem with its right eye. The eye is
enlarged and has a cloudy blue appearance. The facility representative believes the problem occurred within the
last six months and stated the dog has not been evaluated by a veterinarian.
Eye disease can be caused by a variety of problems including infection, trauma, etc and can be painful. The
licensee must have the dog examined by a licensed veterinarian and appropriate treatment administrated.
Documentation should be available on all medical problems. The diagnosis and any recommended treatment must
be part of the documentation. The licensee must ensure that all animals receive adequate veterinary care at all
times.
To be corrected by: February 12, 2016.
Exit briefing conducted with the licensee and facility representative.
