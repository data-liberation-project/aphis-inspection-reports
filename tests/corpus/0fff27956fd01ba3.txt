3.84(a)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
There was excessive build-up of fecal material on the floor of the enclosure housing two adult spider monkeys.
Routine cleaning and sanitation must be done daily to protect the health and well-being of the two spider monkeys.
Fecal material must be removed daily to prevent nonhuman primates from becoming soiled and to reduce disease
hazards. To be corrected by: June 14, 2018.
3.84(c)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
In the enclosure with two adult spider monkeys, there is an excessive build-up of hair covering the electrical light
fixtures. The accumulation of hair hanging from the electrical fixtures prevents adequate cleaning and sanitation.
Buildings for non-human primates must be kept clean and in good repair in order to protect the health and
well-being of all animals housed inside this facility. To be corrected by: July 1, 2018.
3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
In the outdoor enclosure housing 40 goats, there was coiled cable which appeared to be partially buried in the soil.
This cable is a potential entanglement hazard for the domestic goats housed in this enclosure. This cable must be
removed to protect the animals from injury. To be corrected by: July 1, 2018.
3.127(b)
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
Shelter from inclement weather. One adult female camel (Jasmine) and one adult male camel (Aladdin) are housed
together in one outdoor enclosure. The single shelter in this enclosure is not tall enough or wide enough to
comfortably house both of the camels during inclement weather. An additional taller shelter must be added to this
outdoor enclosure to provide adequate shelter for both camels. To be corrected by August 1, 2018.This inspection and exit briefing were conducted with facility representatives.
