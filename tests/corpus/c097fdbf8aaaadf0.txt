3.11(d)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
At the time of inspection, there were excessive roaches found within the night enclosure for the two wolf/dog
hybrids. Roaches can transmit disease and cause harm to the animals. The accumulation of roaches is an indicator
of an ineffective pest control program. The facility must implement an effective program for the control of insects
and mammalian pests.
Correct by: October 23, 2015
3.84(c)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
At the time of inspection, there was no source of natural or artificial light in the brown capuchin night enclosure. On
an overcast afternoon, the enclosure was completely dark upon entrance. Housing facilities must be lighted well
enough to permit routine inspection and cleaning, and observation of the non human primates. Lighting must be
provided to assist in husbandry practices for the animals in the enclosure.
Correct by: January 23, 2016
3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
At the time of the inspection, the resting platforms in the night quarters for the two tigers were in need of repair
and/or replacement.
At the time of inspection, the shelter in the cavy enclosure had planks that were worn and chewed down and in
need of replacement and/or repair. Both indoor and outdoor housing facilities must be structurally sound and be
maintained in good repair to protect the animals. Correct by: October 23, 2015
3.131(c)
SANITATION.
At the time of inspection, there was no source of natural or artificial light in the deep freezer, even with the door
opened. Facilities must be lighted well enough to facilitate good husbandry practices. Lighting must be provided to
assist in the husbandry practice and be kept in good repair.
Correct by: January 23, 2016
3.131(d)
SANITATION.
At the time of inspection, there were evidence of pests (rodent droppings and ripped bags of grain) found in the feed
room. Rodents can harbor and spread disease to the animals. The facility must establish and maintain a program
for the control of insects and mammalian pests.
Correct by: October 23, 2015
