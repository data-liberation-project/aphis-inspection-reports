3.55 DIRECT REPEAT
WATERING.
Two enclosures, containing one rabbit each, did not have water at the time of this inspection. Water was provided
during the inspection and both rabbits drank from the full water bottles indicating they were thirsty and had not had
adequate water supplied for them. Water is essential for life and must be provided daily or as often as needed to
provide for the animals water needs. A regular schedule of watering of all animals must be in place and maintained.
Corrected at time of inspection.
3.56(a) REPEAT
SANITATION.
Two enclosures, each housing one rabbit, had an excess of excrete and soiled bedding in them. All animal
enclosures need to be cleaned daily or frequently enough to maintain a clean healthy environment for the animals
contained in them. A schedule of regular cleaning and sanitizing needs to be in place and maintained.
Correct by: February,13,2017
3.130 DIRECT REPEAT
WATERING.
One degu, one ferret, and six gerbils had no water at the time of this inspection. Water was supplied during
inspection and all these animals went immediately to the water bottles and drank. It was apparent from this action
that they had not had water supplied has needed for good health. Water is essential for life and must be supplied at
all times or as often as necessary for the health and comfort of the animal. A regular schedule of watering all
animals must be in place and maintained.
