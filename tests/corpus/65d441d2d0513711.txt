3.31(b)

Sanitation.
Housekeeping. In the food storage area adjacent to the animal room, there was scattered hay, bedding, and rodent
droppings on the floor and behind stored bird cages. There were accumulations of spilled feed fines on the pallet with
stacked bags of guinea pig feed. Ineffective cleaning can expose the animals to disease hazards and health problems.
Correct by ensuring the premises are kept clean, including: routinely sweeping and cleaning all surfaces on the inside of
facility and maintaining a level of general cleanliness to support good animal husbandry and health. Correct by August 27,
2022.
3.31(c)

Sanitation.
Pest control. In the food storage area adjacent to the animal room, there was a live wild mouse climbing along the wall
behind stored vacant bird cages. There was scattered hay, bedding, and rodent droppings on the floor and behind the
stored bird cages. There were accumulations of spilled feed fines on the pallet with stacked bags of stored guinea pig
feed. No pest control measures were noted aside from one empty rodent bait wrapper. Wild rodents and pests can
expose the other rodents in the facility to disease. The licensee must establish and maintain an effective pest control
program to control wild mice. Correct by August 27, 2022.
