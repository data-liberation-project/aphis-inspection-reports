2.33(b)(5)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE.
Item Veterinary Care 2.33 b 5
At the time of inspection it was noticed that a swine had been left unattended in his cage following anesthesia. This
was brought to the attentation of the attending vet and he assigned a vet tech to watch the animal until recovered.
Facility needs to insure that post operative procedures for monitoring are reviewed to insure the safety and well
being of the animal.
Correct from this day 4/16/14
3.6(a)(1)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
Item Primary Enclosures 3.6 a 1
At the time of inspection the facility had erected temporary cages for dogs, the sides of the cages were not aligned
properly resulting in wide spaces and gaps in which a dog could get himself caught. In addition because of the
height of the dogs in relation to the height of the cages the facility had placed tops on the cages but were not
completely secured and could be moved if the dog jumped or stood on its hind legs. Such an incident occurred a
few weeks ago when a dog was able to protrude through the spaces.
Facility needs to insure that all primary enclosures are safe and structurally sound to protect the animals.
Correct from this day 4/16/14
3.11(a)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
Item Cleaning 3.11 a
At the time of inspection canine kennels were reviewed and it was noticed a buildup of dirt and grime along the side
panel of the cages and along the top of the cages indicating the cages had not been properly cleaned.
Facility needs to insure husbandry staff properly clean all surfaces of cages to prevent a build up of excessive
waste. Correct from this day 4/16/14
3.28(a)(1)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
