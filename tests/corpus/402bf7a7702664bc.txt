3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The following areas are in need of repair.
1) One outdoor wooden shelter housing has an estimated ten inch gap located where the back wall and left side
wall join. Five goats are in contact with the affected area located in outdoor housing. The potentially injury exists
causing stress or discomfort if any goat gets caught in the affected area.
2) The metal galvanized fencing has become detached from the galvanized metal pipe frame on two panels.
There is a one to two inch gap between the fencing and metal pipe frame. Seventy five young goats are in contact
with the affected panels located in shelter housing. .This affects the ability of the enclosure panel to contain the
goats securely and could lead to potential injury.
3) Three "4 by 8" sheets of plywood are broken with jagged, sharp edges. Thirty goats are in contacted with the
affected plywood sheets located in outdoor housing. Sharp, jagged edges could potential cause injury.
4) The wiring fencing has become detached along the bottom causing the fence to turn up creating an estimated
two to six inch gap between the ground and bottom of the fence. Twenty four goats are in contact with the affected
area located in outdoor housing. The potentially injury exists causing stress or discomfort if any goat gets caught in
the affected area.
The licensee must ensure all areas are kept in good repair and protect the animals from injury at all times. Correct
by 3/31/14.
3.129(b)
FEEDING.
* One plastic feed trough used by 24 goats has been destroyed. An estimated 90 percent of the trough is missingand is unable to hold any feed being dispensed to the goats. The remaining plastic is chewed and has a
roughened, jagged edge. The receptacle used by 24 goats is located in outdoor housing. A chewed, roughened,
jagged surface cannot be easily cleaned and sanitized and could potentially cause injury. In addition, it could
harbor bacteria and other disease causing agents that can affect the health and wellbeing of the animals. The
licensee must ensure that all receptacles can be properly cleaned and sanitized at all times. Correct by 3/31/14.
* The facility inspection done on 3/10/14 and the exit briefing done on 3/13/14 were conducted with the facility
director.
