3.28(a)(1)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
There are 11 enclosures housing a total 87 adult guinea pigs and their offspring with holes on the front side. The
holes range in size from 2 inches by 2 inches to as large as 3 inches by 7 inches. In addition, there are sharp wire
ends and jagged wire around the holes. Primary enclosures must be structurally sound and maintained in good
repair to protect the guinea pigs from injury. Failure to do so can lead to injury from falls to the ground, getting
trapped, or caught on sharp/jagged wire. Licensee must ensure that primary enclosure are structurally sound and
kept in good repair to prevent injury to the animals. Correct by March 31, 2020.
This inspection was conducted with the licensee and exit interview was conducted with the facility representative.
