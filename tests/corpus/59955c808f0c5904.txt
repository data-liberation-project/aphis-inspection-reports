2.40(b)(3) Repeat

Attending veterinarian and adequate veterinary care (dealers and exhibitors).
The muted calico colored guinea pig in the 9th bin of the top row on the left side of the guinea pig and rabbit barn had a
blue-gray tint to its' right eye. The licensee had not observed this condition prior to inspection.

Each dealer shall establish and maintain programs of adequate veterinary care that include daily observation of all
animals to assess their health and well-being provided that a mechanism of direct and frequent communication is required
so that timely and accurate information on problems of animal health, behavior, and well-being is conveyed to the
attending veterinarian.

Daily observations must be conducted to assess health and well-being of the animals to ensure a proper diagnosis and
treatment plan can be established in a timely manner by the attending veterinarian.
3.31(a)(1) Repeat

Sanitation.
The majority of the guinea pig primary enclosures had wet and soiled pine shavings with excessive fecal material beneath
a layer of fresh hay.


Primary enclosures shall be cleaned and sanitized often enough to prevent an accumulation of excreta or debris.

The licensee must ensure primary enclosures are cleaned and sanitized often enough to reduce amount of urine, feces,
and possible disease hazards.

This inspection and exit interview were conducted with a facility representative.
