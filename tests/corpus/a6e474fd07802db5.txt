2.75(b)(1)

Records: Dealers and exhibitors.
A current record of guinea pigs on hand was not available for review. A total of 759 guinea pigs including adults,
weanlings, and newborns were counted during this inspection. There were no inventory records present indicating the
number of adults, weanlings, and newborns held, or the number of guinea pigs that died in the last few months. Failure to
keep inventory records including births and deaths makes it difficult to track changes in production, losses due to death,
and to assess the animal care program. Licensee must ensure that records are kept and maintained on guinea pigs held,
owned, born, died, and sold to help keep track of animals, document changes in ownership, and to help assess the
animal care program. Correct by November 5, 2022.
3.31(b)

Sanitation.
Housekeeping. There were shavings, hay, and debris covering the floor in the animal room and the storage area. Animal
facilities must be kept clean to prevent pest problems, to ensure good quality of the air, and to prevent disease risks.
Ineffective cleaning and housekeeping can expose the guinea pigs to disease hazards, pest problems, and health risks,.
Correct by ensuring the premises are kept clean, including: removing shavings, hay, and debris from the floor, and
maintaining a level of general cleanliness to support good animal husbandry and health. Correct by November 5, 2022.
