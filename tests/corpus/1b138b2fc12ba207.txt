3.1(c)(1)(i)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The lower galvanized metal bars that support the inner primary enclosures for two adult dogs are rusty. Primary
enclosures must be designed and constructed of suitable materials so that they are structurally sound. Be free of
excessive rust that prevents the required cleaning and sanitization, or that affects the structural strength of the
surface. The primary enclosures must be kept in good repair to allow for proper sanitation to prevent possible
diseases. The licensee must replace or repair all rusty galvanized metal bars that come contact with adult dogs.
Correct by 12-1-2017
3.31(b)
SANITATION.
The food storage area has loose dry dog food scattered all over and underneath wooden pallets that the food
barrels are on. The walls, enclosures and ceiling have cob webs that are covered in brown dust. Premises
(buildings and grounds) shall be kept clean and in good repair in order to protect the animals from injury and to
facilitate the prescribed husbandry practices set forth in this subpart. Premises shall remain free of accumulations of
trash, serious cob web infestation and loose dog food to protect the health of the animals. The licensee must sweep
up and discard the dog food and remove all brown covered cob webs.
