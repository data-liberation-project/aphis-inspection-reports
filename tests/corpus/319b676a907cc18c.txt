2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
*** 7 animals were found in need of veterinary care during the inspection. This included:
1. One female guinea pig was housed in pen #59. The right eye was being held partially
closed and had brown crusty debris on the upper and lower lid accumulating in the corner of the eye closest to the
nose.
2. One white and tan guinea pig was present in pen #31. The left eye was being held partially closed and had brown
crusty debris on the hair surrounding the entire eye.
3. One black and white guinea pig was in pen #32. This guinea pig had an area on the back end near the tail that
had blood on it. The hair was matted down with a pink - bloody discharge, and there was blood observed on some
of the shavings inside the pen.
4. One white and brown guinea pig was in pen #22. There was two golf ball sized lumps observed under and
extending out on both sides of its chin.
5. One white and tan guinea pig with a black face was housed in pen #16. This guinea pig's right and left eyes were
held partially closed and had brown crusty debris.
6. One brown guinea pig with a white face was housed in pen #17. There was a golf ball sized lump observed under
its chin.
7. One tank and black female guinea pig was housed in pen #10. There was a golf ball sized lump observed under
both sides of its chin.
The clinical signs of abnormal lumps/masses, wounds, and eye abnormalities are all indicators of ill-health or
disease. These conditions can result from multiple causes including traumatic injury an infectious diseases, or other
causes. Depending on the underlying cause, left untreated these conditions may progress leading to additional
unnecessary animal suffering. All licensees are required to implement appropriate methods for adequate veterinary
care including the use of methods to prevent, control, diagnose, and treat disease
for all animals. Appropriate diagnosis and treatment of these animals is necessary to ensure humane care.
Correct by having these animals examined by a licensed veterinarian for diagnosis and the development of an
appropriate treatment plan for animal each condition. Documentation of this examination, the treatment plan,
implementation of the treatments, and recommendations for prevention / control of disease spread to the remaininganimals shall be maintained by the licensee for examination by APHIS Officials upon request.
