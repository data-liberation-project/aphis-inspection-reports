2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
A male Yorkshire terrier, microchip # 476736231B, was observed to have a buildup of a brown colored substance
on his teeth and the inspector observed a reddish color along the gum line. Licensee explained that dental
cleanings were done every year and a half. Poor dental health can result in discomfort and an increase in disease
risk to the dog.
The licensee must have the dog evaluated by a veterinarian and establish a dental plan for all dogs to maintain
good dental health.
Affects at least 1 dog
Correct by 07/31/14
3.1(c)(1)(2)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
In the outdoor enclosures of the large dog building, the bottom of the metal sheets of the building have rusted and
left holes with jagged edges at the level of the dogs. One enclosure, housing labradors, has a section of the metal
sheet that has broken and twisted upward, resulting in a sharp edge at the level of the dogs.
Jagged edges and sharp points may injure the dogs and the licensee must remove, replace or repair the damaged
metal.
Affects 7 dogs
Correct by 07/31/14
3.1(c)(3)HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
In the building housing large dogs, there is a buildup of dirt and grime on the walls of the indoor enclosures at the
level of the dogs. The walls of the enclosure housing the basset hounds, is exceptionally dirty. The outdoor walls
around the access door of the basset hounds, also has a buildup of dirt. The buildup of dirt and grime can increase
the risk of disease hazards to the dogs.
The license must clean and sanitize all surfaces that come in contact with the dogs every two weeks.
