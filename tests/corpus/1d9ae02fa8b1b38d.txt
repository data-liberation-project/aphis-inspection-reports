2.40(b)(2) DIRECT REPEAT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
A female Bichon ("Maggie"; DOB 6/2009; Microchip #836-573-360) has a cloudy right eye with excessive discharge
present. The discharge is thick, yellow-green in color and covers the surface of the eye. Some discharge is present
on the eyelids. The eye itself is moderately cloudy and the white of the eye is moderately reddened. The eyelids are
also mildly reddened. Eye conditions such as these can be painful and/or distressful to the animal. The animal listed
above must be examined by a licensed veterinarian in order to ensure that an accurate diagnosis is obtained and an
appropriate treatment plan is developed and followed. This information, including the diagnosis, treatment and
resolution of the condition, must be documented and made available to the inspector upon request. The licensee
must ensure that all animals receive appropriate veterinary care at all times.
3.6(a)(2)(10) REPEAT
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
In the "Male Building" there are at least three enclosures, containing a total of four dogs, which have large
gaps/holes in the raised flooring. The affected areas of flooring are located in the outdoor portion of the enclosures
next to the gates and the gaps/holes are large enough for the foot and leg of a dog to pass through. Gaps and holes
large enough to allow the foot and leg to pass through can cause injury to the dog.
The flooring of primary enclosures must be constructed in a manner that protects the dogs' from injury and does not
allow the dogs' feet to pass through any openings. The licensee must alter the affected flooring in a manner that
does not allow the dogs' feet or legs to pass through. The licensee must ensure that all used primary enclosures
are safe for the dogs at all times.
3.11(b)(2)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
In the "Male Building" there are two enclosures, containing a total of four dogs, which have a thick buildup of dark
brown dirt and grime on the surface of the access doors. A buildup of dirt and grime can increase the risk ofdisease towards the dogs.
Used primary enclosures must be sanitized once every two weeks, or as often as necessary to prevent an
accumulation of dirt, debris, food waste, excreta and other disease hazards. The licensee must clean and sanitize
the access doors. The licensee must ensure that all surfaces of used primary enclosures remain free of dirt and
grime buildup in order to reduce the risk of disease.
