3.137(a)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES USED TO TRANSPORT LIVE ANIMALS.
The female Patagonian Cavy enclosure contained a can wired to the side to provide drinking water. The top edge
of the drinking water can is jagged sharp and rusty, which could injure the animal. The traveling enclosure must not
contain any protrusions which may cause harm to the animals within. All traveling enclosures accepted by the
auction must meet the requirements set forth in this section.
Correct by April 2, 2015.
3.137(d)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES USED TO TRANSPORT LIVE ANIMALS.
The enclosure with the coati-mundi did not have a pan, nor a solid bottom with absorbent bedding to contain
leakage. Enclosures must contain solid bottoms with adequate amounts of absorbent bedding to prevent leakage of
wastes. All enclosures accepted for consignment by the auction must meet the requirements in this section.
Correct by April 2, 2015.
