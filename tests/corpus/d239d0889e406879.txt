SANITATION.
Housekeeping. The floor contains accumulations of scattered hay, bedding, boxes, and debris. There is a shelf
below the first enclosure on the right by door that is filled with clutter, boxes, cobwebs, water bottles, dust, and
debris. Animal facilities must be kept clean and in good repair to prevent unnecessary disease risks. Ineffective
cleaning exposes the animals to disease hazards and health problems. Correct by ensuring that the premises are
kept clean, including: removing accumulations of scattered hay, bedding, boxes, dust and debris from the floor and
shelf; routinely dusting off and sanitizing all surfaces inside the facility, and maintaining a level of general
cleanliness to support good animal husbandry and health. Correct by September 3, 2019.
