2.32(a) Critical Repeat

Personnel qualifications.
In March 2020, a vole was found alive after CO2 euthanasia was conducted. The PI's staff member failed to verify death
and the animal recovered. Corrective action has included retraining the staff member in proper euthanasia procedures
and close monitoring .
In December 2020 , veterinary staff member discovered that pain medication had not been give to macaques in a timely
manner . The required dosing schedule was every 12 hours and the medication had not been administered 15 hours after
the last dose.. The vet staff medicated the animals and informed the PI of the issue. Corrective measures included
retraining PI staff in the correct timing of medication administration and continued monitoring of medication records.
In April 2021, one marmoset was found out of the home enclosure at an afternoon room check . The loose marmoset had
caused an injury to the finger of a marmoset in a nearby enclosure. The wound on that animal was minor but did require
treatment with an anti inflammatory medication . It was found that the escape occurred when a side door was not properly
secured. Corrective action has included retraining staff on cage door complexities to prevent further escapes.
It shall be the responsibility of the research facility to ensure that all scientists, research technicians, animal technicians,
and other personnel involved in animal care, treatment, and use are qualified to perform their duties. This responsibility


shall be fulfilled in part through the provision of training and instruction to those personnel.
