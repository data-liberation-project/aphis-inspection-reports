2.40(b)(2) DIRECT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
Sec. 2.40 Attending veterinarian and adequate veterinary care (b)(2)
There were 5 adult dogs at the facility with health concerns.
*A female Beagle (Tattoo # 1402) named "Apple" had a hard, golf ball size mass on her back. The licensee was not
aware of the mass and was unsure how long it had been present.
** A female Beagle (Mc # 46594A2E31) named "Betsy" had a large mass on the right side of her stomach. This
mass was identified by the attending veterinarian during a hands on examination as a " hernia right side" 11 months
prior, on April 24, 2014.
*** A female Beagle (Mc # 445PO5266B) named "Blonde" had a large mass on her stomach. This mass was
identified by the attending veterinarian during a hands on examination as a " large hernia " 11 months prior, on April
24, 2014.
**** A female Yorkshire Terrier (Mc# 49663A2A61) named "Reen" had a small mass in her left mammary area. This
mass was identified by the attending veterinarian during a hands on examination as a " small breast cancer " 11
months prior, on April 24, 2014.
***** A female Yorkshire Terrier (Mc# 482E062346) named "Lucy" had a large mass on the right side of her
stomach. This mass was identified by the attending veterinarian during a hands on examination as a " large hernia "
11 months prior, on April 24, 2014.
These dogs were also marked unsound for breeding. However, these females are still housed with males. No
treatment plan was recorded.
Abnormal tissue masses such as these are the indication of a serious medical condition that may cause the dog
discomfort or be painful. The licensee shall provide adequate veterinary care by obtaining a written diagnosis and
treatment plan for each dog as well as providing care as prescribed.

3.11(b)(2)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
Sec. 3.11 Cleaning, sanitization, housekeeping, and pest control.(b)(2)
There were 5 water buckets in the outdoor housing area that were dirty and had green algae covering their inside
surfaces. Dirty water receptacles are a disease risk. The licensee shall ensure water receptacles are sanitized at
least once every 2 weeks using one of the methods prescribed in paragraph (b)(3) of this section, and more often if
necessary to prevent an accumulation of dirt, debris, food waste, excreta, and other disease hazards.
