3.84(d)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
There are excessive numbers of flies on the fruit and in the enclosure of the macaques. Flies can carry disease,
contaminate food, and are a nuisance to the animals. The licensee must maintain a safe and effective program for
the control of insects and other pests. Corrected at time of the inspection.
3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
There is a twisted wire protruding out from the lip of the water receptacle in the goat enclosure. The wire is about 1
to 2 inches in length with the points extending forward. Sharp points can cause injury to the animals. The licensee
must ensure that the facilities are maintained in good repair to protect the animals from injury. Corrected at the time
of inspection.
3.130
WATERING.
There is green material collecting on the inside of the water receptacle in the goat enclosure. There were also
mosquito larvae seen in the water of this receptacle. Water receptacles that are not clean and sanitary can lead to
disease transmission as well as decreased water consumption. The licensee must keep all water receptacles clean
and sanitary. To be corrected by 4 September 2014.
3.131(d)
SANITATION.
There are excessive numbers of flies on the food and in the enclosure of the ground hog. Flies can carry disease,
contaminate food, and are a nuisance to the animals. The licensee must maintain a safe and effective program for
the control of insects and other pests. Corrected at the time of inspection.
