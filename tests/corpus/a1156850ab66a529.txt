2.40(b)(2) DIRECT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
A single housed neutered male pig was found to have skin lesions on the ears, jowl and flank. On the middle of the
outer surface of each ear there was a red superficial ulceration of the skin with brown crusty debris. The tip of the
ears also had a brown crusty lesion. On the right jowl there was an approximately 1cm deep circular ulcerative
lesion on the skin. The right lower flank had multiple red ulcerative dermal lesions with a brown crusty debris
around the edges of the lesions. All lesions were attracting flies.
Correct by 8 November 2016.

3.125(a) REPEAT
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The indoor and outdoor housing facilities shall be structurally sound and maintained in good repair to protect theanimals from injury and to contain the animals.
The pasture still has potential hazards that could cause injury to the animals that have access to this area. Primarily
this includes broken glass that has been exposed due to bulldozer activity, but also loose wire and discarded baling
twine that could cause entanglement.
The hazards must be removed from the pasture or the animals precluded from entering the hazardous area.
Correct by 9 December 2016.
3.131(d)
SANITATION.
A safe and effective program for the control of insects, ectoparasites, avian and mammalian pests shall be
established and maintained. The food storage shed has a rodent problem as evidenced by large amounts of rodent
feces found throughout the shed. The feed needs to be maintained off the floor. Feed stored in bins need to be
secured with a lid. Pest control measures need to put in place.
Correction to start immediately and be ongoing.
