3.31(a)(1)
SANITATION.
There is a buildup of brown scale, stains, fly dirt, and debris on the sides and floor of plastic guinea pig enclosures.
The regular cleaning process (method and frequency) utilized is not effective in removing this accumulation of scale,
stains, dirt, and debris. Failure to effectively clean and sanitize enclosures exposes the animals to potential disease
hazards such as bacterial, fungal, and viral diseases. Primary enclosures must be cleaned often enough to prevent
an accumulation of scale, stains, dirt, and debris and must be sanitized at least once every two weeks. Correct by
ensuring that all primary enclosures are kept clean to prevent an accumulation of scale, stains, dirt, and debris and
the that enclosures are sanitized at least once every two weeks. Correct by March 19, 2016.
