2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
In the red building there is a female Maltese (ear tattoo CSM) that has an approximately one inch long open wound
located along the underside of her right flank at the top of the right rear leg. The area around the wound is slightly
reddened and the inside edge of the wound is somewhat swollen. The licensee has been self-treating the wound
with Vetericyn but this dog has not been evaluated by a licensed veterinarian. Open wounds can cause pain and
discomfort to the dog and could lead to further health issues. This dog must be evaluated by a licensed veterinarian
for proper diagnosis and treatment plan. The diagnosis and treatment plan must be documented and available to
the inspector upon request.
Correction date: June 12, 2015
The female Maltese (ear tattoo CSM) that has an open wound was treated with Vetericyn during the inspection.
Closer examination of the bottle of Vetericyn revealed that it had expired in October of 2013. The use of expired
medication is not considered to be acceptable veterinary care and may not be effective. The expired medication
should be properly disposed of or appropriately labeled as expired and moved to a physically separate location from
the non-expired medications. Licensee must ensure that all medications used on the dogs is current and has not
expired.
Correction date: Immediately
