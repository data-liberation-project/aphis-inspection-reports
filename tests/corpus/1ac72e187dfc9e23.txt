3.8

Exercise for dogs.
At the time of inspection, there was no written program of exercise for dogs made available. The program of exercise
must be developed in accordance with the attending veterinarian recommendations to ensure the dogs are receiving
adequate exercise. Exercise is necessary to benefit the health, comfort, and well-being of the dogs. The facility must
develop, document and follow an appropriate plan of exercise and the plan must be approved by the attending
veterinarian.

3.13(a)

Veterinary care for dogs.
At the time of inspection, there is no written Program of Veterinary Care (PVC) for dogs. A written Program of Veterinary
Care (PVC) for dogs is required to ensure that there is an expectation about the care provided to any dogs at the facility.
Failure to establish and maintain a written Program of Veterinary Care (PVC) for dogs can decrease the ability to ensure
optimal care of the dog. A written Program of Veterinary Care (PVC) for dogs must be established and maintained so that
the facility has a plan for providing adequate preventative care and treatments as necessary. The plan must include all of


the required components as listed in the Animal Welfare Act and Animal Welfare Regulations, Part 3, Subpart A, Section
3.13.

Date to be Corrected: May 13, 2022
