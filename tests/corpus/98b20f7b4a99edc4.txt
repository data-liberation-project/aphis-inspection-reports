RECORDS: DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS.

3.75(a)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
Three pieces of wire with sharp tips were observed in the squirrel monkey enclosure.
Sharp objects may cause injuries to the animals. Animal enclosures should be constructed and maintained in a
manner that will protect the animals from injury.
Correct by November 4, 2015.
3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
A piece of frayed hardware cloth was attached to the side of a chinchilla enclosure. The edges of the hardware cloth
were sharp and some of the points were accessible to the animals.
Sharp objects may cause injuries to the animals. Animal enclosures should be constructed and maintained in a

3.131(a)
SANITATION.
Two chinchilla enclosures contained an excessive amount of fecal material. There were no clean areas in the
enclosures where the animals could avoid exposure to fecal material.
Excessive accumulations of excrement in animal enclosures may adversely affect animal health and wellbeing.
Excreta should be removed from enclosures as often as necessary to prevent contamination of the animals and to
minimize disease hazards and reduce odors.
Correct by November 4, 2015.
An exit briefing was conducted with a facility representative.
