3.13(a)(3)

Veterinary care for dogs.
The program of veterinary care states the following regarding fecal testing, “at least 5% of adult breeding stock will be
tested at random on a yearly basis.” Fecal testing was not performed at the frequency described in the program of
veterinary care. Fecal testing is important for ensuring animals do not have any intestinal parasites. Each dealer much
follow an appropriate program of veterinary care for dogs that includes the sampling and treating for pests (including
worms, coccidia, and giardia) in accordance with a schedule approved by the attending. The applicant must have a fecal
test performed.

This is a 1st Re-License Inspection for a Class “A” license. The inspection and exit briefing were conducted with the
applicant.

All items must be in compliance within 2 more inspections or by 18-MARCH-2023 or the applicant will forfeit the license
fee and must wait 6 months to reapply. Please contact your inspector to schedule your next Re-License Inspection.

Regulated activity can continue under your existing USDA license until it expires.
