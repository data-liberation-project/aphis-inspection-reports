3.26(b)
FACILITIES, INDOOR.
Ventilation. There was a strong ammonia odor throughout the indoor guinea pig room. Three windows were open on
two opposing walls, but the widows were not sufficient to provide adequate ventilation. According to the licensee
enclosures were last cleaned on March 14, 2020. Failure to provide fresh air can expose the guinea pigs to potential
disease hazards, and not cleaning soiled bedding often enough can increase ammonia levels. The indoor housing
facilities must be ventilated to minimize odors and ensure the health of the guinea pigs. Licensee must ensure that
housing areas are adequately ventilated to provide the health and comfort of the animals. Correct by March 28,
2020.

SANITATION.
There is a buildup of dirt, dust, grime, feces, soiled bedding, cobwebs, and debris on walls and floors of enclosures.
According to the licensee the enclosures were last cleaned on March 14, 2020. The accumulation of dirt, dust,
grime, feces, soiled bedding and cobwebs can exposes the guinea pigs to potential infections with bacteria, fungi,
and parasites. Too much dirt, grime, feces, and soiled bedding is accumulating between the cleaning interval.
Correct by ensuring that all primary enclosures are cleaned and sanitized often enough to prevent an accumulation
of feces, dirt, grime, soiled bedding, and debris.
