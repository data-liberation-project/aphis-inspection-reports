2.40(b)(2) DIRECT REPEAT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
In building D a male Pomeranian, "Sparky", microchip #010813009, was observed by the inspectors to be
intermittently scratching both sides of his body. Closer examination showed thinning hair on both sides of the body.
The skin in those areas was moderately reddened with some areas turning slightly dark gray and thickened. The
reddened areas are blotchy and vary in size from approximately one centimeter to four centimeters.
The same dog was observed to have a thick, grey/tan material encasing all of the upper and lower cheek teeth. A
milky grey/tan substance is also present along the gum line of all of the cheek teeth.
Dental and skin diseases can be painful and/or distressful to the animals and can lead to further health
complications.
The animal listed above must be examined by a licensed veterinarian in order to ensure that an accurate diagnosis
is obtained and an appropriate treatment plan is developed and followed. This information, including the diagnosis,
treatment and resolution of the condition, must be documented and made available to the inspector upon request.
The licensee made contact with the veterinarian during the inspection to set up an appointment to see the animal.
A complete inspection was conducted on June 22, 2015. This is a focused inspection report limited to the Direct
non-compliant items identified.
Exit interview conducted with facility representative.
