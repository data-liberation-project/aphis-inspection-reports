3.50(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
Structural strength. There are holes in the walls and floor of the rabbit shed from wild rodents. Housing facilities for
rabbits must be structurally sound and maintained in good repair to protect the animals and to restrict the entrance
of other animals. The holes in the walls and floor of the rabbit shed must be repaired to prevent the entrance of wild
rodents. Correct by March 30, 2019.
3.56(c)
SANITATION.
Housekeeping. There are accumulations of shavings, straw, spilled feed, excreta, and debris on the floor of the
rabbit shed. Buildings and grounds must be kept clean and in good repair to protect the animals and to facilitate
good husbandry practices. Correct by ensuring the rabbit shed and grounds are kept clean, including: removing
accumulations of shavings, straw, spilled feed, excreta, and debris from the floor of the rabbit shed, and maintaining
a level of cleanliness to support good animal husbandry and health. Correct by March 20, 2019.
3.56(d)
SANITATION.
Pest Control. There are large holes in the wall and floor of the rabbit shed by the doggie door. Rat droppings and
debris were present by holes on the floor. An effective program must be established and maintained to control wild
rodents. In addition to establishing a rodent control program, improved housekeeping, covering holes, and removing
waste from the rabbit shed are necessary to maintain an effective pest program. Correct by March 30, 2019.
