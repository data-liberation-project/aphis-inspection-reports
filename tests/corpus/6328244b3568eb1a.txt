2.40(a)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
The Program of Veterinary Care is not being followed. The program states all animals must be tested for
Brucellosis prior to breeding, annual heartworm testing and preventative must be done and a monthly treatment for
ticks and fleas must be used. The licensee is not following these veterinary recommendations.
The licensee is removing dewclaws without written approval by the attending veterinarian.
Each dealer shall assure that the attending veterinarian has appropriate authority to ensure the provisions of
adequate veterinary care and oversee all other aspects of animal care.
The program of veterinary care has to be followed as written by the attending veterinarian to ensure the health of
the animals. Removal of dewclaws has to be completed by the attending veterinarian, or written consent from the
attending veterinarian for the licensee to remove dewclaws has to be available.
The Program of Veterinary Care must be followed from this day forward.
Dewclaw removal cannot be performed by the licensee until the attending veterinarian approves the method in
writing.
2.40(b)(2) DIRECT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
There are at least 6 large breed, Basset Hounds and Golden Retrievers, that have overgrown toenails.
Prolat/Lintox HD, a cow and pig insecticidal, was used on the small breed dogs for flea treatment. This product
was not in the Program of Veterinary Care. Two Yorkies were nursing four puppies. The licensee stated that
these four puppies died from the use of this medication because they forgot to wash it off.
Each dealer shall maintain a program of veterinary care as instructed by the veterinarian to include the use of
appropriate methods to prevent, control, diagnose and treat disease and injuries.
Overgrown toenails must be clipped to prevent injury to the dog. Flea treatment must be approved by the attendingveterinarian to ensure that it is an appropriate product for use in dogs and does not cause death.
Toenail trims to be corrected by August 5, 2016.
All medications must be approved by the veterinarian from this day forward.
2.40(b)(3) DIRECT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
The licensee stated that a Standard Poodle was lethargic and not acting right. The licensee dewormed the dog, but
it died. The licensee did not consult the attending veterinarian concerning this dog. At least two of the Siberian

Daily observations must be made of all animals to asses their health and well-being. If these observations are
