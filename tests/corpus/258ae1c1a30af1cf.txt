2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
Multiple expired medical products were found in the cabinets in the small dog whelping room. The following
products were found to be expired: Povidone Iodine Swabsticks expired 11/2008, Lactated Ringer’s Injection
expired 08/01/2016, Anti-diarrheal medication expired 09/2001, small bottle of Iodine expired 1998, Tums expired
04/2011, and Nitrofurazone expired 11/26/2015. Expired medical products may lose their effectiveness and the
health of the animal could suffer.
The use of expired medical materials on regulated animals is not considered to be acceptable veterinary practice
and does not constitute adequate veterinary care. The licensee must either dispose of the expired products or
clearly label them as expired and they are not to be used on the dogs. The licensee must ensure that all medical
products used for the regulated dogs is current in order to promote the health and well-being of the dogs.
Correction date: The licensee removed the expired products at the time of the inspection.
Inspection and exit interview conducted with Kelly Maxwell, ACI and the facility representative.
