2.33(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE.
Review of medical treatment records for two dogs currently housed at the facility show that medications have not
been administered by the students as prescribed by the clinical veterinarian. The missed treatments and
documentation were not identified by staff prior to the review of the records during this inspection. The affected dogs
include:
Dog # 869 (white with black and tan tricolored adult female) was not administered Otomax on the evening of
1/28/18 and was not administered Ketoconazole and Simplicef on the evening of 1/30/18.
Dog # 870 (light brown adult male) was not administered Carprofen and Otomax on the evening of 1/28/18 and was
not administered Carprofen on the evening of 1/30/18.
The improper administration/missed dosages of the prescribed medications may delay healing from infection and
inflammation and may lead to ineffective pain control. All medications shall be administered as prescribed to treat
diseases and injuries appropriately. Correct by 2/1/18.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with undersigned Attending Veterinarian and other facility
personnel.
