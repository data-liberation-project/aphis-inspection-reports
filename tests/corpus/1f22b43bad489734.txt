2.40(b)(2) DIRECT REPEAT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
Two animals were identified as in need of veterinary attention during this inspection:
*One young bactrian camel has an open draining wound on its right back lower back leg. The owner reports that he
has been treating it topically and that it was healing but now it has reopened. The wound is irregular in shape and
about 2 in long and 1 to 1 1/2 in wide. The skin edges appear ragged. The tissue appears red and swollen. The
camel is bearing weight and walking on the leg.
*One deer has a blue-ish discoloration to the lower half of her right eye. The owner reports that the color change
has been there for a while. He stated he thinks the deer may be getting a cataract.
A veterinarian needs to examine these two animals in order to determine a diagnosis and treatment plan. All
medical diagnoses and treatments shall be documented in writing.
3.27(b)
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
Four guinea pigs are currently being housed in an outdoor enclosure. Guinea pigs housed outdoors may be
vulnerable to predators and adversely affected by the elements (temperatures, humidity, rain, etc). The guinea pigs
shall not be housed outdoors unless located in an appropriate climate and approval for such outdoor housing is
obtained from the Regional Office. Correct by housing the guinea pigs indoors or obtaining written approval. Correct
by 9/9/17.
