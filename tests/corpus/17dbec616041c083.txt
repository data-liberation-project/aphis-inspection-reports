3.9(b) DIRECT REPEAT
FEEDING.
***Direct - Inside of the main food holding compartment and some of the trough areas of the self-feeders in 8
enclosures, affecting 16 dogs, there is a significant to an exessive amount of rodent feces (fresh to dried
appearance) mixed throughout the food being offered to the dogs. Rodent feces and rodents could contaminate the
feed and increase the risk of disease to the dogs.

The licensee must remove the rodent droppings from the self-feeders, clean and sanitize the feeders. The licensee
must ensure that all food receptacles are readily accessible to the dogs, and located in such a way as to minimize
contamination by excreta and pests.
This is a focused inspection to specifically address the direct non-compliant items (2.40 b. 2. - Veterinary Care
issues and 3.9 b. Contaminated feed) identified during the inspection conducted on 9 Feb 15.
