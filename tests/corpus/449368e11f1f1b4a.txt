3.125(c) REPEAT
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
***In the alley way between the barns containing animals, there were multiple boxes of produce stacked on the
ground and on top of each other. The licensee stated that the produce had arrived last Monday (August 13, 2018) in
a semi trailer and that the staff had not had time to go through it all yet. The licensee also stated that all of the bad
produce is being thrown away as they go through it. One box of eggplant was rotted and had mold growing on it.
Flies were on and around the produce. The temperatures for the past week have ranged from 73 degrees F to 101
degrees F according to the National Weather Service. Feed/food that is stored inappropriately can be contaminated
by pests and inclement weather and if used, cause illness in the animals. The licensee must assure that supplies of
perishable food are refrigerated adequately protect them from deterioration, molding or contamination by vermin as
required.
3.131(d) REPEAT
SANITATION.
***There were two enclosures that contained sixteen Zebu, seven cattle, two Yak and two goats that had a
numerous amount of flies around the feed receptacles and on the animals. There was also a multitude of flies on
the produce that was stacked in the alley way. At the time of inspection, the licensee stated that some pest control
was being used. Pests carry diseases which increase the risks of contamination and disease to the animals. The
licensee must take measures to control pests in the facility to include, but not limited to proper food storage and
assure that an effective program for pest control is established as required.
This inspection began at 1045.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the licensee.
End of report.
