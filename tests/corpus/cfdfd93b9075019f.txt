3.1(c)(1)(i)

Housing facilities, general.
The two platforms serving as entry and exit points from the primary enclosures into the exercise yard were rusted to the
point that they could no longer be readily cleaned and sanitized.

Excessively rusted metal surfaces can not be properly cleaned and disinfected leading to potential disease hazards for
the animals using these platforms.

The licensee must ensure that all surfaces that come in contact with dogs have the ability to be be appropriately cleaned
and sanitized.

To be corrected by close of business on Tuesday, October 5, 2021.
3.1(c)(2)

Housing facilities, general.
The rug in the exercise yard was worn, frayed, and in need of replacement.



Worn and soiled rugs can serve as potential areas of disease hazards. Also a dog could ingest these materials causing
injury.

The licensee must ensure all areas that cannot be readily cleaned and sanitized should be replaced when worn or soiled.

To be corrected by close of business on Friday, September 24, 2021.
3.1(c)(3)

Housing facilities, general.
There was an excessive accumulation of dog excreta in the area under the primary enclosures and on the ground of the
exercise yard.

Excessive dog excreta and other waste can serve as potential sources for disease hazards for the dogs.

The licensee must ensure all hard areas that dogs come in contact with be cleaned and sanitized accordingly.

To be corrected by close of business on Friday, September 24, 2021.
