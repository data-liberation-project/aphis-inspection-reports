Records: Dealers and exhibitors.
The facility has not documented the acquisition information for the 28 adult dogs. Failure to document who the dogs were

2.75(a)(1)(v)

Records: Dealers and exhibitors.
The facility has not documented the date that each dog was acquired. Failure to document the date of acquisition
interferes with APHIS officials ability to trace the movement of dogs.

Each dealer shall make, keep, and maintain records or forms which include the date of acquisition for each dog. The
facility must document the date of acquisition for each of the dogs.
2.75(a)(1)(vi)

Records: Dealers and exhibitors.

The facility uses microchips to identify each dog, but has not documented the official identification number for each dog
along with the acquisition information. The official identification number is necessary for APHIS officials to trace the
movements of dogs, as well as to complete required documents such APHIS form 7005 and medical records.

Each dealer shall make, keep, and maintain records or forms which include the official identification number for each dog.
The facility must add the microchip number to the acquisition information for each of the dogs.

Records: Dealers and exhibitors.
The facility is not using APHIS form 7005 to document the required acquisition information for the dogs. The facility is

Each dealer shall use APHIS form 7005 (Acquisition of Dogs and Cats on Hand) to make, keep and maintain the required

3.1(e)

Housing facilities, general.
One open bag of dog food is sitting on the floor of the dog kennel. Open bags of food could become contaminated by
pests such as flies and also could also allow humidity and liquids to come into contact with the food potentially resulting in
molding or caking of the feed.

Supplies of food and bedding must be stored in a manner that protects the supplies from spoilage, contamination, and
vermin infestation. All open supplies of food and bedding must be kept in leakproof containers with tightly fitting lids to

prevent contamination and spoilage. The facility must have the open bag of food placed in a leak-proof container with a
tightly fitted lid in order to protect it from contamination, molding or caking.
3.1(f)

Housing facilities, general.
One trash can located inside the kennel does not have a lid. Trash containers without lids could attract pests such as
mice or flies which could have a negative impact on the health and well-being of the dogs.

Trash containers in housing facilities must be leak-proof containers and have tightly fitted lids. The facility must have a
tightly fitted lid applied to the trash container or remove the trash container from the kennel in order to protect the health
and well-being of the dogs.
3.13(a)

Veterinary care for dogs.
The facility has acquired a new attending veterinarian, but lacks his signature on the Program of Veterinary Care
indicating that he has reviewed the information contained in it. It is necessary for the attending veterinarian to review and
sign the Program of Veterinary Care in order to ensure the animals are receiving adequate veterinary care.

Each dealer must follow an appropriate program of veterinary care for dogs that is developed, documented in writing, and
signed by the attending veterinarian. The facility must have the new attending veterinarian review and sign the Program
of Veterinary Care.
3.13(a)(2)

Veterinary care for dogs.

The Program of Veterinary Care is incomplete. How often the physical examinations of the dogs is to be conducted by
the attending veterinarian is missing. The physical examination is necessary in order to ensure that the dogs are
receiving adequate veterinary care.

Each dealer must follow an appropriate program of veterinary care for dogs that is developed, documented in writing, and
signed by the attending veterinarian and includes information concerning the physical examination of each dog by the
attending veterinarian which is conducted no less than once every 12 months. The facility must add to the Program of
Veterinary Care how often each animal is to be examined by the attending veterinarian every 12 months.
3.13(a)(3)

Veterinary care for dogs.
The Program of Veterinary Care is incomplete. The schedule for vaccinating the dogs for rabies is missing. How often
the dogs are to receive the rabies vaccine must be documented in order to ensure the animals are receiving adequate
veterinary care.

Each dealer must follow an appropriate program of veterinary care for dogs that is developed, documented in writing, and
signed by the attending veterinarian which includes the schedule for vaccinating against disease. The facility must add
the schedule for vaccinating the dogs for rabies to the Program of Veterinary Care.
3.13(a)(4)

Veterinary care for dogs.
The Program of Veterinary Care is incomplete. The plans to maintain healthy and unmatted hair coats, properly trimmed
nails, and clean and healthy eyes, ears, skin, and teeth is missing. The plans are necessary to ensure the dogs are
receiving adequate veterinary care and any problems are identified quickly and corrected appropriately.


Each dealer must follow an appropriate program of veterinary care for dogs that is developed, documented in writing, and

clean and healthy eyes, ears, skin, and teeth. The facility must add these plans to the Program of Veterinary Care in
order to ensure each dog is receiving adequate veterinary care.
