3.81

Environment enhancement to promote psychological well-being.
At the time of inspection, the documented program of environmental enhancement for non-human primates to was
inadequate. It did not address the specific needs of each individual species or special considerations for the singly
housed, common squirrel monkey. An adequate environmental enhancement program is essential in promoting the
psychological well-being of non-human primates. Dealers. Exhibitors, and research facilities must develop, document, and
follow an appropriate plan for environment enhancement adequate to promote the psychological well-being of non-human
primates. To be corrected before the next re-license inspection.
3.130

Watering.
The water receptacle for the adult wolf enclosure housing three adult timber wolves was in need of cleaning. The sides
and bottom surfaces are coated with a green-brown slimy material and mosquito larva could be seen swimming in the
water. Drinking contaminated water can lead to disease and illness or may cause the animals to stop drinking water
leading to dehydration. All water receptacles shall be kept clean and sanitary. Corrected by the end of the inspection.
