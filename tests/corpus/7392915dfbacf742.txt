3.11(a)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
There is an accumulation feces in at least three of the outdoor enclosures. There was an odor of feces in one of
these enclosures. When feces are allowed to accumulate they attract insects/pests, contribute to odors, soil the
animals, and can facilitate the spread of diseases. The applicant must remove excreta and food waste from primary
enclosures daily. Correct by 11 May 2018.
3.11(b)(2)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
Numerous food receptacles had debris/dirt caked on the interior surfaces in contact with the food. Dirty food
receptacles can lead to contamination of the food.
Also, at least 9 water receptacles had green algae growing on the sides and had dirt residue in the bottoms. Water
receptacles that are not kept clean can contaminate the water.
All food and water receptacles must be sanitized at least once every 2 weeks, or more often if necessary to prevent
the accumulation of dirt, debris, food waste, excreta, and other disease hazards.
