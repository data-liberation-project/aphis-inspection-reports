3.129(a)
FEEDING.
There is a moderate amount of food debris and uncovered food items on the shelves of the refrigerator.Food stored
in this manner may be less palatable, become spoiled and/ or contaminated which can negatively affect the health
and well-being of the animals.
The food shall be wholesome, palatable, and free from contamination and of...nutritive value to maintain all animals
in good health. A system should be in place to ensure refrigerated food is stored in a manner which minimizes
contamination and the refrigerator is cleaned out as often as necessary as required by the Act.
To be corrected by August 18, 2017
3.131(a)
SANITATION.
Two outdoor animal enclosures have a small to moderate about of fecal material and debris collecting around the
inside edges of the primary enclosures. This may increase disease hazards to animal housing areas.
Excreta shall be removed from primary enclosures as often as necessary to prevent contamination of the animals
contained therein and to minimize disease hazards and to reduce odors. A system should be in place to ensure
primary housing enclosures are cleaned as required by the Act.
To be corrected by August 18, 2017
