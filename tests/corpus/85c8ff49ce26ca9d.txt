2.75(a)(1)

Records: Dealers and exhibitors.
There were no records available at time of inspection concerning dogs on hand, and acquisition and disposition
documents.

Records are necessary to be able to accurately track animals being used in regulated activities to ensure their legal
acquisition, proper care, and humane transportation.
Available records are required at time of inspection to ascertain compliance with the animal welfare regulations.

Each dealer, other than operators of auction sales and brokers to whom animals are consigned, and each exhibitor shall
make, keep, and maintain records or forms which fully and correctly disclose the following information concerning each
dog or cat purchased or otherwise acquired, owned, held, or otherwise in his or her possession or under his or her
control, or which is transported, euthanized, sold, or otherwise disposed of by that dealer or exhibitor. The records shall
include any offspring born of any animal while in his or her possession or under his or her control.

Correct from this day forward.


3.8

Exercise for dogs.
There was no written exercise plan available at time of inspection.

Exercise is important for the health and well-being of dogs and an exercise plan approved by the attending veterinarian
must be available to USDA officials at time of inspection.

Dealers, exhibitors, and research facilities must develop, document, and follow an appropriate plan to provide dogs with
the opportunity for exercise. In addition, the plan must be approved by the attending veterinarian. The plan must include
written standard procedures to be followed in providing the opportunity for exercise. The plan must be made available to
APHIS upon request.

Correct by November 30, 2021.
3.13(a)

Veterinary care for dogs.
There was no written program of veterinary care readily available at time of inspection.

A written program of veterinary care, along with regularly scheduled visits to the premises ensures adequate veterinary
care and that all dogs are thoroughly examined annually and the exam findings are documented in readily retrievable
records for inspection.

Each dealer, exhibitor, and research facility must follow an appropriate program of veterinary care for dogs that is


developed, documented in writing, and signed by the attending veterinarian. Dealers, exhibitors, and research facilities
must keep and maintain the written program and make it available for APHIS inspection. The written program of
veterinary care must address the requirements for adequate veterinary care for every dealer and exhibitor in § 2.40 of this
subchapter --see pages 125-126 of AWA & AWA Regulations.

Correct by Nov. 30, 2021
