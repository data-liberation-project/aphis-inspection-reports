2.40(b)(2) REPEAT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
A female Boxer #097-284-302 had a rasied, reddened, swelling in betwen her toes on her front left paw. The tissue
in between the toes was swollen and enlarged to about the length of the outer toes and about 1/2" wide. It was
displacing the normal configuration of the toes. Rasied, abnormal appearing areas and sores on the feet could be
the result of injury, infection or a variety of veterinary medical conditions which may be painful. The licensee must
consult with a licensed veterinarian in order to ensure that accurate diagnoses are obtained and appropriate
treatment plans are developed and followed. The licensee must ensure that all animals receive adequate veterinary
care at all times.

3.6(a)(2)(10) REPEAT
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
(a) (2) Primary enclosures (x) Flooring
*** Inside the whelping building, there was one enclosure housing 4 small breed puppies and dam. The flooring was
1" x 1" coated wire and the puppies' feet and legs were observed falling through the openings in the flooring. The
animals' feet and legs falling through the openings in the enclosure floor could cause injury. The licensee must
ensure that appropriate flooring is used to prevent the animals' feet from passing through the openings in the floor.
Inspection and exit interview conducted with the licensee.
