3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The pen currently housing the two bulls is in disrepair. There are two boards that have detached from the front of
the stalls on one end and are falling to the ground. There are smaller pieces of wood laying on the ground
containing screws with the pointed ends sticking up. Loose boards and sharp points may injure the animals. The
pen shall be repaired to protect the bulls from injury. Correct by June 20, 2016.
The plastic pool in the prairie dog enclosure is in disrepair. The plastic has been cracked and broken leaving sharp
edges that may cause injury. The pool shall be repaired or replaced. Correct by June 20, 2016.
3.125(c)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
Storage. The containers used for storing the dry feed in the garage do not seal completely. There are several
insects (spiders, flies and spider webs) inside of the containers). Insects cause contamination and deterioration of
food. The food shall be stored in a manner that will protect it from deterioration and contamination. Correct by June
20, 2016.
3.130
WATERING.
There is a build up of debris in the bottom and on the inner walls of the water bowl in the enclosure housing the two
goats. The build up of debris and sediment can increase disease hazards (bacterial growth, fungus, algae, etc.)
and cause contamination. The water bowls shall be cleaned and sanitized to protect the water from contamination
and provide for the health and well being of the animals. Correct by June 17, 2016
3.131(b)
SANITATION.
The flooring and wire base of the sides of the cage housing the ferret have rust and corrosion. The rust feels grimy
in some areas where there is a build up of waste material. Rusty surfaces can not be adequately be cleaned andsanitized. Improper cleaning increases the risk of bacterial growth and other disease hazard which may cause
illness. The surfaces shall be prepared in a manner that will permit appropriate husbandry and the cage cleaned
and sanitized to protect the ferret from illness. Correct by June 20, 2016.
3.131(c)
SANITATION.
Housekeeping. The yellow building housing the small mammals has dirt and debris on the floors and on the racks
holding the animal cages. There are several small insects flying around and fly strike on the walls and cage racks.
Insects can act as a vector to spread disease as well as cause discomfort. The building shall be cleaned and
maintained in a manner that will help to minimize the attraction of insects and thereby facilitate the health and
well-being of the animals. Correct by June 20, 2016.
