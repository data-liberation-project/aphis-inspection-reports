3.125(a)

Facilities, general.
An electric cord was unprotected and accessible to two young coatimundi in the indoor area. Electrical cords pose a
significant health risk to animals that may chew or pull on them. The facilities shall be maintained in good repair to protect
the animals from injury. This electrical cord was removed at time of inspection.
3.84(d)

Cleaning, sanitization, housekeeping, and pest control.
An excessive number of houseflies were present in the indoor housing area at 3 enclosures containing (1) the colobus
monkey, (2) 2 spider monkeys, and (3) 1 pig-tailed macaque and 1 rhesus macaque. Flies can harbor and distribute
disease among animals housed in the same area. An effective program for the control of insects must be maintained so
as to promote the health and well-being of the animals and reduce contamination by pests in animal areas. Correct by
February 10, 2021.

This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the facility owner.
