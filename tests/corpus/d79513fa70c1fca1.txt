3.128
SPACE REQUIREMENTS.
The sugar glider enclosure located next to the office containing three sugar gliders and the sugar glider enclosure
next to the register containing three sugar gliders. The cages are too small and do not provide adequate space for
each animal to make normal postural and social adjustments. The sugar gliders need to have a larger enclosure to
make normal postural movement and social adjustments with adequate freedom for their health and well-being.
To be corrected by September 4, 2014.
3.129(b)
FEEDING.
The sugar glider enclosure located next to the office containing three sugar gliders. The food receptacle has a
brownish material, lining the edge of the bowl and the food is caked at the bottom of the bowl. Inadequate cleaning
and sanitizing of receptacles can lead to contamination and potential for disease. Food receptacles shall be kept
clean and sanitary at all times. Adequate measures shall be taken to prevent caking of food.
To be corrected from this day forward.
3.130
WATERING.
The sugar glider enclosure located next to the office containing three sugar gliders. The watering bowl had no water
available to the animals inside the enclosure. An employee of the facility filled the watering bowl with water and the
sugar gliders drank continuously until the water was gone. Watering must be provided as often as necessary for the
health and comfort of the animals.
To be corrected from this day forward.3.131(a)
SANITATION.
Cleaning of enclosures.
The sugar glider enclosure located next to the office containing three sugar gliders. The enclosure has excessive
feces and caked food on the floor of the enclosure. The edge of the flooring has excessive smeared brownish
material along the borders of the enclosure floor. Excreta shall be removed to prevent contamination of animals
contained and minimize disease hazards and reduce odors.
To be corrected by Aug 30, 2014.
