2.126(a)(2) REPEAT
ACCESS AND INSPECTION OF RECORDS AND PROPERTY; SUBMISSION OF ITINERARIES.
The facility representative stated that the facility changed attending veterinarians on August 01, 2016 and the new
attending veterinarian still has APHIS form 7002, Program of Veterinary Care in his office and the form was not
available at the time of the inspection.
The information contained on APHIS form 7002 is required to facilitate a complete inspection of the animals and the
routine veterinary treatments practiced by the facility. The facility must ensure that all records required by the
Animal Welfare Act are properly completed and available to the inspectors at the time of the inspection.
3.125(a) REPEAT
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The enclosure containing the feed bunks for the deer has sharp points from broken wires attached to a wooden
corner post and they are located at the level of the animals. There are three deer that have access to this enclosure.
Sharp points can cause injury to the animals. The licensee must remove all sharp points from the animal enclosures
in order to protect them from injury.
The enclosures containing five elk and three deer are constructed of wooden fence posts and woven wire. There
are multiple wooden fence posts located along the fence line behind the office building that are leaning and are
broken at the base and wobble and move freely when pushed by the inspector. The facility representative stated
that the broken fence posts located next to the round bales of hay that were cited on inspection report dated June
15, 2016 have not been repaired or replaced.
Broken fencing is not structurally sound and could cause injury to the animals or allow the animals to escape. The
facility must be constructed of such material and of such strength as appropriate for the animals housed within. The
facility must be structurally sound and kept in good repair to protect the animals from injury. The licensee must
repair or replace the broken wooden fence posts to ensure the animals are safe from injury and cannot escape. 3.131(c) REPEAT
SANITATION.
The enclosure containing five elk and three deer has a large pond at its center. Behind the pond dam there are at
least five discarded rubber vehicle tires lying on the ground. The tires are partially covered by dirt and leaves.
Contact with the rubber tires could cause injury to the animals.
The premises shall be kept clean in order to protect the animals from injury and all accumulations of discarded
items must be placed in designated areas. The licensee must remove the tires from the animal enclosure and
ensure that all discarded material and trash is placed in a designated area away from the animals.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with Kelly Maxwell, ACI and the facility representative.
