3.56(a)
SANITATION.
The wire enclosures were generally covered with cobwebs and had accumulations of hair, some also had
accumulations of feces that had not fallen thru the wire floors. Dust and organisms can be collected in these
accumulations and lead to disease in the animals. Correct by assuring primary enclosures are kept reasonably free
of excreta, hair, cobwebs and other debris by periodic cleaning. To be corrected by: 13 November 2015
3.57
EMPLOYEES.
It was evident from the condition of the facility that not enough man hours were being used to clean and maintain
the facility. The owner stated that he had been unable to do the work as normal recently. He stated that he had an
individual to help intermittently but that this individual did not do an adequate job of cleaning. If the current number
of employees is unable to provide the required man hours to facilitate the care, cleaning and maintenance then
more employees are needed. Failure to provide for sufficient operation of the facility can lead to disease in the
animals. Correct by providing a sufficient number of employees (man-hours) to maintain the prescribed level of
husbandry practices set forth in 9 CFR. To be corrected by: 13 November 2015
An exit briefing was conducted with the Owner.
