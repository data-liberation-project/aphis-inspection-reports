3.30
WATERING.
There were accumulations of dirt and debris by the automatic water valves and inside PVC drainpipes for four racks
of wire enclosures. These accumulations by the automatic water valves and inside the drainpipes exposes the
guinea pigs to potential infections with bacteria, fungi, and parasites. Inadequate cleaning and sanitation of the
automatic water valves and drainpipes can result in contamination of the guinea pig colony. Correct by keeping the
water lines and surrounding areas clean to ensure the health and well-being of the guinea pigs. Correct by July 16,
2016.
3.31(a)(1)
SANITATION.
The guinea pig enclosures had clean bedding, but there is a buildup of brown and roughened scale on the sides
and floor of the plastic tubs. Ineffective cleaning and/or infrequent sanitation exposes the animals to disease
hazards. Correct by ensuring that all primary enclosures are cleaned and sanitized often enough to prevent an
accumulation of excreta and debris. Additionally, ensure that the primary enclosures are sanitized at least once
every two weeks. Correct by July 16, 2016.
3.31(b)
SANITATION.
Housekeeping. The floor contains accumulations of spilled feed, shavings and debris. There are accumulations of
dust, dirt and cobwebs on the ceiling and walls of the animal building. Animal facilities must be kept clean to ensure
good quality of air for the animals and to prevent unnecessary disease risks. Ineffective cleaning exposes the
animals to disease hazards and health problems. Correct by ensuring the premises are kept clean, including:
removing accumulations of dust, cobwebs, spilled feed, shavings, and debris; routinely dusting off and cleaning all
surfaces on the inside of the facility; and maintaining a level of general cleanliness to support good animal
husbandry and health. Correct by July 20, 2016.
