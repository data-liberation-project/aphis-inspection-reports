2.134(a)

Contingency planning.
A contingency plan was not available at time of this inspection. If an emergency or weather incident occurred animal
health could be impacted if the facility was not prepared with a plan. Licensee needs to develop a contingency plan in the
event of an emergency that could affect the health and well-being of the guinea pigs. Correct by January 28, 2023.
3.28(a)(1)

Primary enclosures.
There are sharp ends of wire where the self-feeders are attached to the front wall of guinea pig enclosures. The sharp
ends are above the troughs which could cause injury to the guinea pigs while eating. Primary enclosures must be
constructed and maintained in good repair to protect the guinea pigs from injury. Licensee must ensure that protruding
wire ends are repaired or removed to prevent possible injury to the guinea pigs. Correct by January 28, 2023.

This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the facility representative.
