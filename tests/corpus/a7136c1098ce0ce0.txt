2.31(c)(1) REPEAT
INSTITUTIONAL ANIMAL CARE AND USE COMMITTEE (IACUC).
The most recent IACUC meeting and program review were conducted May 16, 2014 (eight months ago). According
to the IACUC Chair, a meeting and program review were supposed to be conducted December 2014, but were not
due to scheduling conflicts. At time of inspection, the next meeting and program review was tentatively scheduled
for February 2015.It is important for the IACUC to conduct regular reviews of the program to ensure that it is in
compliance with the Animal Welfare Act. The registrant must ensure that the IACUC reviews, at least once every
six months, the program for humane care and use of animals.
2.31(c)(2) REPEAT
INSTITUTIONAL ANIMAL CARE AND USE COMMITTEE (IACUC).
The most recent IACUC meeting and facility inspection were conducted May 16, 2014 (eight months ago).
According to the IACUC Chair, a meeting and facility inspection were supposed to be conducted December 2014,
but were not due to scheduling conflicts. At time of inspection, the next meeting and facility inspection was
tentatively scheduled for February 2015. It is important for the IACUC to conduct regular inspections of the facility to
ensure the health and welfare of the animals. The registrant must ensure that the IACUC inspects, at least once
every six months, all the animal facilities.
3.131(a)
SANITATION.
There was an accumulation of dried fecal material and fecal staining on the wooden shelter blocks within all the
primary enclosures of the sugar gliders and flying squirrel. There were many house flies near and around their
enclosures. According to the animal care checklist, none of the primary enclosures have been cleaned at all during
the month of January. The last recorded enclosure cleaning date was on December 30, 2014. Lack of cleaning can
lead to unsanitary conditions that could become hazardous to the health of the animals. The registrant must ensure
that excreta is removed from enclosures as often as necessary to prevent disease hazards.

SANITATION.
