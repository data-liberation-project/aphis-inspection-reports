3.31(b)
SANITATION.
Housekeeping. There is a buildup of dust, dirt, and debris on walls, shelves, window ledge, and old guinea pig
enclosures. There are accumulations of dirt, feces, and bedding on the floor, corners, along walls, below and on old
guinea pig enclosures. Animal facilities must be kept clean to ensure good quality of air for the animals and to
prevent unnecessary disease risks. Ineffective cleaning and housekeeping exposes the animals to disease hazards,
pest problems, and health problems. Correct by ensuring the premises are kept clean, including: removing dust, dirt,
feces, old enclosures, cleaning the floor, widow ledge, and shelves, and maintaining a level of general cleanliness
to support, good animal husbandry and health. Correct by March 7, 2020.
