3.125(a) REPEAT
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The following areas are in need of repair.
1) One outdoor wooden shelter structure has an estimated ten inch gap located where the back wall and left side
wall join. In addition, two exposed nails were observed at the bottom. Two goats are in contact with the affected
area located in outdoor housing. Sharp points and jagged edges could cause injury to the animals.
2) Galvanized metal fencing has become detached from the galvanized metal pipe frame on two panels. There is
a one to two inch gap between the fencing and metal pipe frame. Nine young goats are in contact with the affected
metal fencing located in shelter housing. .This affects the ability of the enclosure fencing to contain the goats
securely and could lead to injury or escape.
3) Two "4 by 8 " sheets of plywood on the west side of one outdoor shelter structure are broken with jagged, sharp
edges. Thirty goats are in contact with the affected plywood sheets. Sharp, jagged edges could cause injury.
4) One cattle panel is not secured to the chain link fencing. An estimated six inch gap exists between the cattle
panel and chain link fencing. Thirty goats are in contact with the affected area which is located in outdoor housing.
Sharp points and jagged edges could cause injury to the animals.
The licensee must ensure all areas are kept in good repair to protect the animals from injury and to contain the
animals at all times.
