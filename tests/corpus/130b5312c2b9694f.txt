2.134(a)

Contingency planning.
The facility has not yet developed and documented a contingency plan for the animals in the event of an emergency or
disaster. A contingency plan is necessary to provide for the humane handling, treatment, transportation, housing and care
of the animals in the event of an emergency or disaster. The plan should identify the situations triggering the plan, outline
the tasks to be carried out, identify a chain of command, and address how the response and recovery will be handled.
The facility shall develop and document a contingency plan to provide for the animals in the event of an emergency or
disaster. Correct by 11/30/22.

This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the facility representative.
