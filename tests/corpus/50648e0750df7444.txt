3.26(c)
FACILITIES, INDOOR.
Lighting. The lighting in the guinea pig room is not adequate to care and observe the animals properly. The room is
too dark to adequately observe the animals especially in the lower enclosures. Lighting in guinea pig facilities must
be
uniformly distributed and of sufficient light intensity to permit routine inspection and cleaning during the entire
working period. Additional lighting must be provided in the guinea pig room to allow proper observation of the
animals and adequate cleaning of facilities. Correct by September 1, 2015.
3.31(a)(1)
SANITATION.
Primary enclosures for guinea pigs are not being cleaned adequately or often enough to remove accumulations of
dust, dirt, cobwebs, and soiled bedding. All enclosures have accumulations of dust, dirt, cobwebs, excreta, and
soiled bedding. The wire sides and frames have accumulations of cobwebs and debris. The cleaning process
(method and frequency) utilized is not effective in removing this accumulation both inside and outside the
enclosures. The licensee stated that the enclosures are usually cleaned each Saturday. Ineffective cleaning and
sanitation exposes the animals to disease hazards and health problem. Primary enclosures must be cleaned often
enough to prevent an accumulation of dust, dirt, cobwebs and soiled bedding. Correct by ensuring that all
enclosures are kept clean and sanitized often enough to prevent an accumulation of dust, dirt, cobwebs, excreta,
and dirty bedding. Correct by August 12, 2015.
3.31(c)
SANITATION.
Pest control. Numerous flies were present in the guinea pig room. The presence of flies creates conditions which
promotes undue stress for the animals and creates conditions which promotes the spread of disease within the
facility.The fly strips in the animal room are not effective in reducing the current fly problems. Additional measures
such as screening the door and windows is needed to control or eliminate the fly problem. An effective pest program
must be established and maintained to control flies. Correct by August 19, 2015An exit briefing was conducted with the owner.
