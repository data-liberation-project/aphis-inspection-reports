2.75(b)(1)
RECORDS: DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS.
Disposition records were not present for 4 adult white-tailed deer and 2 fawns that were born in 2017. Records must
be kept and maintained on all animal births, deaths, acquisitions, and dispositions to help keep track of animals,
document changes in ownership, and to comply with the animal welfare regulations. The disposition records must
include the name and address of the person animals were sold, the date of sale, species, and number of animals in
the shipment. APHIS 7020 forms were left with facility to help maintain records. Correct by March 23, 2018.
2.131(b)(4)
HANDLING OF ANIMALS.
Around noon today and adult, female member of the public pulled into the parking lot and fed the goats, deer, and
pig from a white plastic grocery bag for approximately a half hour. In addition, this person was observed adding
water to the pig and goat water receptacles from a one gallon container. There were no signs or employees present
in this area to discourage this public feeding. If public feeding of animals is allowed, the food must be provided by
the animal facility and appropriate to the type of animal and its nutritional needs and diet restrictions. Facility must
establish a means such as signs or employees to prevent the public from bringing food not purchased or provided
by the animal facility. These measures are necessary to protect the health of the animals and prevent overfeeding.
Correct by April 12, 2018.
This inspection was conducted with the owner and the exit interview was conducted with the customer service
representative.
