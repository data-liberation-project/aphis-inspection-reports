3.56(a)(1)
SANITATION.
There was a heavy accumulation of excreta in a rabbit hutch. Food and water for the rabbits were placed next to the
waste material. Maintaining primary enclosures in such a manner can attract pests, contaminate feed, and increase
disease hazards to this area. A system should be in place to ensure primary enclosures are kept reasonably free of
excreta and other debris by periodic cleaning to maintain the health and well-being of the animals.
To be corrected by 11/21/2014
3.129(a) REPEAT
FEEDING.
In a pen housing two sheep, there was a food receptacle which contained dirt and debris in with the food pellets.
The food receptacle should be cleaned and maintained in a manner that minimizes contamination of the feed. A
system should be in place to ensure animal feed is wholesome, palatable, and free from contamination to maintain
all animals in good health.
Original Correction date 07/23/14
3.130 REPEAT
WATERING.
The water receptacle in the sheep pen had a heavy accumulation of brown organic material along its sides and
bottom. All water receptacles shall be kept clean and sanitary to ensure the health of the animals. A system should
be in place to ensure water receptacles are cleaned as often as needed to be in compliance with The Act.
