3.53(a)(1)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
The lower doors on two rabbit hutches containing 4 rabbits total were broken and tied together with poly twine. A
animal could injury itself or escape from a broken door.
Primary enclosure should be maintained in good repair to protect the rabbits from injury, to contain them and to
keep predators out.
To be corrected by 5/14/18.
3.127(c)
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
In the cow(3)/donkey pen, a large (~ 8' by 8') water puddle was standing on the north side of the feeder, forcing
animals to stand in several inches of water to eat. There was no drainage out of the enclosure for the water.
Standing water can be breeding environment for pests and having to stand in water for prolonged periods can
cause damage to an animals lower legs and feet. Disease, injury or pain could result.
Proper drainage should be provided to eliminate excess water from enclosures.
To be corrected by 4/25/18.
