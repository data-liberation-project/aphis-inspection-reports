2.40(b)(2) REPEAT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
***In the ready to use medication storage area, there were bottles of medication past their expiration dates and
medications without readable expiration dates. There was a bottle of "Ivermectin" that had a labeled expiration date
of 01/15 and a bottle of "Safe-guard" ("Fenbendazole") with a labeled expiration date of 04/2015. Also, there was a
bottle of "Ivermectin" and a bottle of "Wheat Germ Oil Blend" without legibly printed expiration dates. Medications
that are expired and given to the animals may not produce the desired result. The licensee must ensure that all
medications for use in dogs are appropriately labeled with expiration dates and that medications are not used past
their labeled expiration date. As part of the facility's program of veterinary care, the licensee must make certain that
all medications are within the expiration date.
The licensee must ensure that all animals receive adequate veterinary care at all times.
*Note - The male Yorkshire Terrier puppy (no ID) included under 2.40 (b)(2) on the March 9, 2015 inspection report
had been addressed.
The inspection and exit interview were conducted with Erika Leisner (VMO), Beverly Hicks (ACI) and the licensee.
