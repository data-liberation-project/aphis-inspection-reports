3.26(d)
FACILITIES, INDOOR.
Interior surfaces. The guinea pig room is currently being remodeled with new enclosures and wall surfaces. There is
bare wood on some supports, frames and widow/curtain sills. Interior building surfaces must be constructed and
maintained so that building surfaces are impervious to moisture so that they can be readily cleaned and sanitized.
The bare wood on supports, frames and window/curtain sills must be sealed or covered with an impervious surface
so that these surfaces can be repeatedly cleaned and sanitized, will not retain odors, and which fluids will not be
absorb into the surface material. Correct by June 9, 2018.
3.28(c)(1)(iii)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
Space. Insufficient space was being provided for weaned guinea pigs in approximately 40 enclosures. A total of 15
weaned guinea pigs were being housed per enclosure measuring 840 square inches of floor space (28 inches by
30 square inches).Weaned guinea pigs weighing up to 350 grams require at least 60 square inches of floor space
per animal. Inadequate space can cause stress, crowding, and aggression in animals. Licensee must ensure that
guinea pigs are provided with adequate space to prevent stress, aggression, and crowding. No more than 14
weaned guinea pigs can be housed in the new stainless steel enclosures. Correct by April 18, 2018.
This inspection was conducted with the owner and the exit interview was conducted with the owner's wife.
