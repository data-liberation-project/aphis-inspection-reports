3.28(a)(1)

Primary enclosures.
The wooden guinea pig enclosures are no longer impervious to moisture. The sides and floors have stains where
moisture is penetrating into the wood. Enclosures for guinea pigs must be constructed and maintained so that surface are
substantially impervious to liquids and moisture. Failure to to maintain enclosures that are impervious can increase the
risk of disease and health problems. The wooden enclosures must be resealed, replaced, or covered with an impervious
surface so that they can be repeatedly cleaned and sanitized. Correct by April 1, 2021.

This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the licensee and his wife.
