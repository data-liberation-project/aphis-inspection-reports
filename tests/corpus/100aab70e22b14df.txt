2.40(b)(2) REPEAT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
***According to the facility representative, three Tiger cubs were born between January 18, 2014 and January 20,
2014. The actual date of birth is unknown.The facility representative also stated that (cid:28)one of these cubs "got a little
cold and the mother may have sat on it."(cid:29) The cub was not taken to a licensed veterinarian for proper diagnosis and
treatment. Illness or injury to an animal can result in pain, stress or discomfort. The licensee must assure that all
animals with signs or symptoms of sickness or injury be adequately diagnosed by a licensed veterinarian for
appropriate treatment.
To be corrected from this day forward.
2.131(b)(1)
HANDLING OF ANIMALS.
*** According to the facility representative, a Tiger cub that was born in mid-January, was transported on January
31, 2014 to another transporter for a destination at another facility. This cub had health concerns of illness and
possible injury from the date of birth. The facility representative stated that there had been some improvement in the
health of the cub, yet the facility representative at the destination site stated "there was something wrong with that
cub." Conditions can worsen for animals with health concerns during transportion, causing stress, pain or
discomfort. The licensee must assure that all animals are handled in a manner not to cause undue stress, pain or
discomfort as required.
To be corrected from this day forward.
