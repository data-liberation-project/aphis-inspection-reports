2.40

Attending veterinarian and adequate veterinary care (dealers and exhibitors).
A current/updated written Program of Veterinary Care (PVC) was not available for inspection. In order to comply with this
section of the Animal Welfare Act (AWA) a written PVC must be in place and available for inspection at all times. Under
formal arrangements, as directed by the AWA, the establishment must have an updated PVC by the current Attending
Veterinarian (AV). Also, the establishment must ensure the AV conducts regularly scheduled visits to the premises where
regulated animals are kept. To ensure adequate veterinary medical care to regulated animals, a current/updated PVC
must be readily available for inspection, and at a minimum, the AV must visit the establishment's premises once per year.
2.75(b)(2)

Records: Dealers and exhibitors.
Records for animals at hand containing required information on acquisition, disposition, or transportation were not
available for review at the time of the inspection. There were no required records available on the recent acquisition of a
juvenile common zebra. The establishment shall make, keep, and maintain records or forms which fully and correctly
disclose information on animals purchased or otherwise acquired, owned, held, or leased. Also, records are required
when animals are transported, sold, euthanized, or otherwise disposed. The records shall include any offspring born of
any animal at the facility's premises. The Record of Animals on Hand (APHIS Form 7019) and Record of Acquisition,


Disposition, or Transport of Animals (APHIS Form 7020) are forms which may be used by the facility to keep and maintain
the information required by federal regulations. The establishment needs to obtain/document the required information on
the recently acquired common zebra, and made it available for inspection at all times.
3.81

Environment enhancement to promote psychological well-being.
The plan for environment enhancement adequate to promote the psychological well-being of the ring-tailed lemur was not
available for inspection. Exhibitor facilities must develop, document, and follow an appropriate plan for environment
enhancement adequate to promote the psychological well-being of nonhuman primates. The plan must be in accordance
with the currently accepted professional standards as cited in appropriate professional journals or reference guides, and
as directed by the attending veterinarian. The plan, at a minimum, must address each of the following: social grouping,
environmental enrichment, special considerations, and restraint devices. The plan must be made available to APHIS upon
request during inspections.
