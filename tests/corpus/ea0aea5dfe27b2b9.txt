2.40(a)(1)

Attending veterinarian and adequate veterinary care (dealers and exhibitors).
A written Program of Veterinary Care (PVC) was not available for inspection. In order to comply with this section of the
Animal Welfare Act (AWA), a written PVC by the Attending Veterinarian (AV) must be in place and available for inspection
at all times. Also, the PVC must include provisions for the AV to conduct regularly scheduled visits to the premises where
regulated animals are kept. To ensure adequate veterinary medical care to regulated animals, the facility's PVC must
always be available for USDA inspections.
3.125(c)

Facilities, general.
At the food storage room’s side door, a gap was identified at its floor junction. The gap was large enough for vermin to go
through and unrestricted under the door. Supplies of food and/or bedding shall be stored in facilities which adequately
protect such supplies against contamination by vermin. The door’s gap should be eliminated/closed/repaired to eliminate
possible contamination by vermin at the food storage room.
