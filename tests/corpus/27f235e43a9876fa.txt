2.75(b)(1)
RECORDS: DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS.
While reviewing acquisition and disposition records, the owner had records accounting for 14 adult Striped Skunks.
She had a note that an older female skunk had died but had no date as to when this occurred, leaving her with an
inventory of 13 adult skunks. However, only 11 adults were observed during inspection plus 30 infant/juvenile
skunks. The owner had no records of sale, donation, or death of the other two adult skunks.
Additionally, while further reviewing acquisition and disposition records, the owner had records accounting for six
domestic ferrets but only five were observed during inspection. The owner stated that one of them died but had no
record of this occurring. The owner also had an acquisition record for a goat on 8/2/2018 but there was no
information regarding where the animal came from.
Acquisition and disposition records must include name, address, date of acquisition/disposition, species of animal,
and number of animals in the shipment. Acquisition and disposition records must also include USDA license or
registration number (if applicable), or vehicle license number and drivers's license number and state if not licensed
or registered with the USDA. These records must also include offspring born and animals who died/were
euthanized while still with the owner.
Failure to maintain these records can inhibit disease traceback investigations, accounting for animals for health
purposes, or establishing proof of ownership. Correct by including all required information on acquisition and
disposition records, including births and deaths. Correct by June 30, 2019.
3.58(a)
CLASSIFICATION AND SEPARATION.
A Flemish Giant rabbit was in the same primary enclosure as a juvenile Patagonian Cavy. Rabbits are not to be
housed in the same primary enclosure with any other species unless required for scientific reasons. Housing
rabbits with other species may have a negative impact on health of either animal due to potential spread of disease
or bacterial contamination. Correct by housing the rabbit either singly or with other rabbits. Correct by June 19,
2019.This inspection and exit interview were conducted with facility owner.
