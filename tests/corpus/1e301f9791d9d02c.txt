3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL. The animal enclosures were not structurally sound, and contained hazards that could injure the animals:
- Cattle - Corrugated metal sections in fence-line, and on the ground, that had sharp edges. Welded wire fencing
panels fastened together, or to posts, with twine. Openings between fencing and posts, with pointy ends, large
enough for animals to entangle head or limbs. Laundry area with bottles of detergent and bleach accessible to
cattle.
- Goats - Corrugated metal sections in fence-line, on the ground, and dangling overhead that had sharp edges.
Welded wire fencing panels fastened together, or to posts, with twine.
- Sheep - Corrugated metal sections in fence-line that had sharp edges. Welded wire fencing panels fastened
together, or to posts, with twine. Loose rods that have some unwelded from welded wire that pose a risk of
entanglement.
- Llama - Welded wire fencing panels fastened together, or to posts, with twine, with gaps that could result in
entanglement.
Enclosures should be constructed of materials, and of sufficient strength so as to be appropriate for the species.
They should be maintained in good repair. They should adequately contain the animals, and protect them from
injury.
The current state of these enclosures puts the animals at risk of escape and or injury. Steps should be taken to
repair and maintain the enclosures.
To be corrected by 10/23/15.
