2.40(a)(1)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
The attending veterinarian has signed the front page of the program of veterinary care form and the dogs have been
seen by the veterinarian within the last year , however there is no written program of veterinary care present, A
written vet care program which describes treatments, vaccinations and provision for emergency care must be
developed with the attending veterinarian , signed by the veterinarian and followed by the licensee to ensure
adequate vet care is being provided. This must be made available to the USDA inspector upon request.

3.8
EXERCISE FOR DOGS.
Dealers, exhibitors, and research facilities must develop, document, and follow an appropriate plan to provide dogs
with the opportunity for exercise. In addition, the plan must be approved by the attending veterinarian. The plan
must include written standard procedures to be followed in providing the opportunity for exercise. There is no
exercise plan present at time of the inspection. The exercise plan must be approved by the veterinarian and made
available to USDA upon request.
