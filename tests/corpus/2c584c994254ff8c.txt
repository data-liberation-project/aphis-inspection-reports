2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
Sec. 2.40 (b)(2)
*** A bottle of Metronidazole 250 mg is missing dosage, frequency, route, which dog it is for, expiration date and lot
number. Mislabeled medication can lead to improper dosing of the dogs, wrong dog being given the medicine and
not knowing when the medication expires. The medication used in the facility is to be label with required information.
Vet Clinic information, date, Dog name, drug name, concentration, dosage, frequency, route, expiration date and lot
number.
*** Two full multi use syringes of Doc Roys GI Synbiotics have expiration dates of 02/2017, they are sitting next to
two used ones with the same expiration dates.
*** A jar of Boric Acid Powder has an expiration date of 08/2012 was found on the top shelf in the kennel.
*** A container of Calcium Aspartate Anhydrous has expiration date of 01/2017 on the label. Licensee states it is an
old bottle that he reuses with new product. Explained that the new expiration date and lot number must be on the
bottle.
Expired medication used in the facility are not as effective to treat, prevent or control diseases or injuries as
expected. If containers are being reused with new product, then the label should reflect the new expiration date and
lot number after being cleaned and sanitized before reusing. All expired medication must be removed from the
facility to prevent them from being used on the dogs.
To be corrected: Licensee threw away expired medication as soon as it as discovered.
3.6(a)(2)(i)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
Section 3.6 (a)(2)(1)
*** Nineteen enclosures have a metal solid surface on the outside, these metal squares are secured to the hard
plastic flooring with metal self tapping screws that the head of the screw stick up approximately 1/8 inch. The dogs
can injure themselves if they step on the head of the screw. *** The metal squares that are being used as solid resting surfaces on the outside part of the enclosures, the dogs
are chewing the edges and pulling them up. The edges are jagged and sharp edges can injure the dogs. The metal
squares are exposed to the sun and might be the reason why the dogs do not use them.
All enclosures must be designed and maintained to protect the dogs from injury, escaping and allowing other
animals from entering the pen.
