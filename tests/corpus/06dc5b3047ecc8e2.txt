3.50(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
** During the inspection it was noticed that the white and black rabbits primary enclosure door was open. The rabbit
could have jumped out of the enclosure and injured itself. Outdoor housing facilities for rabbits shall be maintained
to protect the animals from injury, to contain the animals, and to restrict the entrance of other animals. It was
corrected immediately.

PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.

3.125(a) REPEAT
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
** The fencing containing 2 llamas has two big holes on the south side of the enclosure. The fence needs to be
repaired and/or replace to properly contain the animals and prevent injury.
** The primary enclosure for the goats has several areas with pointy ends of wire protruding from the fence on the
east side. There is also a strand of barbed wire on the north side of the fence trailing the ground that the animals
can come in contact with and injure themselves. The barbed wire and protruding ends of the fencing need to be
removed to ensure the safety of the animals.
**In the primary enclosure containing the goats there were several strands of hay bale twine. The twine should be
removed to prevent the animals from possible ingestion or becoming entangled in it.
** On the east side of the fencing there were two down planks of wood and one had a nail head protruding from the
end of it. This should be removed to prevent injury of the animals,3.130
