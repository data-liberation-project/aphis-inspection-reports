(a)
DEMONSTRATION OF COMPLIANCE WITH STANDARDS AND REGULATIONS.
***** At time of inspection the manager of the exhibition states that the animals are housed at a site in Kaufman, TX
and will return there following the conclusion of the exhibit. This is not an approved site. This licensee did notify
USDA of a new site in Kaufman, TX, however the facility was not in compliance on the new site inspection
conducted 7/26/12. The inspection report from 7/26/12 states: No regulated activities may be conducted until this
site is in compliance with the Animal Welfare Act. Tigers and farm animals housed at this site have been exhibited
since this date. The facility owner must demonstrate that his or her premises and any animals, facilities or other
premises used or intended for use in the business comply with the regulations and standards. The site housing the
tigers, farm animals, and any other regulated animal must be in compliance with the Animal Welfare Act prior to
conducting activities requiring a USDA license.

ACCESS AND INSPECTION OF RECORDS AND PROPERTY; SUBMISSION OF ITINERARIES

received an itinerary from the licensee prior to overnight travel. The lack of a required itinerary was also noted on
the inspection reports dated 8/20/14 and 11/3/14. The licensee must submit an itinerary to the Regional Director no
fewer than 2 days in advance of travel whenever the animals will be exhibited at a location other than the approved
site(s) and whenever travel extends overnight. The itinerary shall contain complete and accurate information
concerning the whereabouts of all animals intended for exhibition at any location other than the approved site.This inspection and exit briefing was conducted with the exhibit manager at the Canton, Texas Balloon fest 2015
held in Canton, TX on 5/15-16/2015.
