3.28(a)(1)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
A majority of the front sides of the guinea pig enclosures in use are rusty and pitted.The severity of the rust present
indicates these enclosures are not in good repair. Rusted and pitted wire creates sharp edges which can injure
animals. Additionally, rust makes cleaning and sanitization more difficult. Improper sanitization can harbor disease.
Primary enclosures shall be constructed of smooth materials, be structurally sound, and maintained in good repair.
Correct by replacing or repairing rusty and pitted wire on enclosures. Correct by July 1, 2016.
3.29(d)
FEEDING.
The galvanized self-feeders in use are rusted and pitted. Rusted and pitted feeders creates sharp edges which can
injure animals. Additionally, rust makes cleaning and sanitization more difficult. Improper sanitization can harbor
disease. Food receptacles must be kept clean and shall be sanitized at least once every 2 weeks. The rusted and
pitted self-feeders must be replaced or repaired. Licensee must ensure that self-feeders are cleaned and sanitized
as required. Correct by June 14, 2016.
