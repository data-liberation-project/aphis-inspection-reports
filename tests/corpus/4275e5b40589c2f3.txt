2.131(c)(1)
HANDLING OF ANIMALS.
The 2 cavy exhibits and the capybara exhibit do not have public barriers to prevent contact between the public and
the animals . The feeder for the capybara is hanging on a section of fence line where the public stands. The public
could reach the animals thru the fence. Sufficient distance or barriers must be present in front of all exhibits with
covered species so there is minimal risk of harm to the animals and to the public.
CORRECT BY : 9/1/16
Facility stated they will provide an attendant to monitor these exhibits until the construction of barrier fences to
prevent public contact.
3.127(b)
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
The male camel has access to natural shade and to an enclosure for shelter from inclement weather ,however the
shelter is no longer tall enough for this animal . The hump on the camels back is taller than the doorway to the
shelter.
A shelter that is large enough to comfortable house the camel from inclement weather must be provided to prevent
discomfort to the animal.
