3.50(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
(a) Structural strength.
-A hole about five inches wide is noted in one of the wire cages. The hole is large enough for the entire head and
neck of a rabbit to pass through and may cause injury should the rabbit attempt to escape. Facility shall ensure that
housing facility is structurally sound and maintained in good repair.
CORRECT BY AUGUST 1, 2014.
3.50(c)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
3.50 (c) Storage
(c) Feeds stored in utility feed cart do not have cover to adequately protect against infestation or contamination of
vermin. Facility shall ensure that feeds are stored in a manner that will protect these from infestation or
contamination.
CORRECT BY JULY 31, 2014.
3.52(e)
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
3.52 (e) Drainage.
-Large pools of blackish water mixed with fecal materials under rabbit cages are noted in the barn. Barn do not have
adequate drainage to channel excess water from the barn. Standing water may provide growth for harmful
organisms. Facility shall ensure that a suitable method be provided to rapidly eliminate excess water from the barn.
CORRECT BY AUGUST 6, 2014.
3.56(c)
SANITATION.
(c) Housekeeping.
-About 50 % of wire cages are rusting and if not corrected in a timely manner may start flaking. These flakes may beingested by the rabbits and may cause serious health problems. Facility shall ensure that cages are maintained in
good condition to protect animals from injury and to facilitate good husbandry practices.
