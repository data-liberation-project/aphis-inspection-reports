2.40(b)(2)

Attending veterinarian and adequate veterinary care (dealers and exhibitors).
There were expired K9 Distemper vaccines (expired 11/2020) in the fridge. The licensee stated that he had used the

Each dealer shall establish and maintain programs of adequate veterinary care that include the use of appropriate

2.75(a)(1)

Records: Dealers and exhibitors.
The licensee does not have any acquisition records for a black and white female mini poodle named "Mim."

Each dealer shall make, keep, and maintain records or forms which fully and correctly disclose the following information

concerning each dog purchased or otherwise acquired, owned, held, or otherwise in his or her possession or under his or
her control, or which is transported, euthanized, sold, or otherwise disposed of by that dealer. The records shall include
any offspring born of any animal while in his or her possession or under his or her control.

A licensee must make, keep, and maintain records to allow animals to be traced and tracked in case of disease outbreak
and to ensure that appropriate dealers and brokers are licensed. The licensee must ensure records are made, kept, and
maintained for all animals on premises.

To be corrected by close of business today, 8/18/21.
3.1(a)

Housing facilities, general.
The outdoor of the sheltered kennel has orange plastic snow fence coming up through the gravel. There was evidence of
chew marks.

Housing facilities for dogs must be designed and constructed so that they are structurally sound. They must be kept in
good repair, and they must protect the animals from injury, contain the animals securely, and restrict other animals from
entering.

The exposed plastic may pose a risk of injury to and possible ingestion by the animals. The licensee must ensure to keep
the housing facilities in good repair.

To be corrected by close of business Friday, 8/20/21.

3.11(d)

Cleaning, sanitization, housekeeping, and pest control.
There were cobwebs and spiders present in all of the kennels and there were two wasp nests on the facility (one at the
corner of the back door of the kennel area and one at the corner of the garage door at the front of the kennel).

An effective program for the control of insects must be established and maintained so as to promote the health and well-
being of the animals and reduce contamination by pests in animal areas.

The cobwebs can pose a fire risk and the spiders and wasps can cause potential injury to the animals. The licensee must
ensure an effective pest control program is in place.

To be corrected by end of business on Friday, 8/20/21.
