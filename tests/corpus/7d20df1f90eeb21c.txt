This was a Focused inspection to address the non-compliant items listed on the January 14, 2016 Inspection
Report.
All of the animals listed under the NCI 2.40 (b)(3) have been seen by the Veterinarian and are currently under
treatment, except the black and white pig that was limping, and the white pig with hair loss. Those were both
euthanized by the Veterinarian.
The 3.52 (c) NCI concerning the bedding in the rabbit hutches. Rabbit housing had an extra amount of wood chips
added to suffice for the colder weather.
The 3.125 (a) NCI concerning:
The enclosure where the two goats were sticking their heads through the opening in the wire, have been removed
from that enclosure and it is no longer being used until the wiring can be repaired.
The secondary wire within the bobcat (2) enclosure that was old and rusted has been removed and replaced.
The back panels in the hospital area containing goats and sheep, that was not secured, and had gaps to possess a
possible injury hazard, have been repaired, and replaced.
The gap under the fencing of the of the pig pen, containing two pigs, has been repaired.
The NCI 3.127 (b) concerning:
The deer enclosure has had three sides added to its open housing, allowing the deer more protection from the wind
and rain.
The holding areas within the hospital, at the time of last inspection holding 4 adult goats, 2 sheep, and two newborn
goats, with inadequate shelter, has had new housing areas provided.The larger goat enclosure housing the 17 goats, has had new shelters added to adequately house the goats during
bad weather.
Extra bedding of straw and/or wood chips has been added to all the shelters housing animals at the park.

The NCI 3.127 (c), drainage in the area containing two pigs has had a concrete slab poured in a higher area of the
enclosure to ensure the animals have a safe, dry, secure area.
Concerning the NCI 3.131 (a). The raised platform for the bears has been cleaned and sanitized.
This inspection and exit interview was conducted by Brenda Pietrosante, ACI, Michelle Williams, VMO, with the
facility Manager.
