3.6(a)(2)(ix)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
At least six resting platforms in the indoor facility are excessively chewed on the corners, and at least two access
doors in the sheltered facility are excessively chewed on the bottom edge.
Chewed and roughened surfaces are difficult to properly clean and sanitize, causing possible disease hazards.
These resting platforms and access doors must be repaired in a manner that ensures proper sanitization. The
licensee must develop and follow a regular maintenance program that ensures all surfaces in contact with the dogs
are able to be readily cleaned and sanitized in accordance with 3.11 (b) of this subpart, or be replaced when worn
or soiled.
To be corrected by 23 September 2016.
3.11(c)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
Around the sheltered facility, there is an overgrowth of weeds and grass.
Overgrown weeds and grass create a breeding ground and living area for pests and vermin, causing possible
disease hazards.
These weeds and grass must be controlled to reduce or eliminate breeding and living areas for rodents, pests, and
other vermin. The licensee must develop and follow a regular method of premises housekeeping and maintenance
to ensure the protection of the health and well-being of the animals.
To be corrected by 23 September 2016.This inspection and exit briefing were conducted by Lynn Clarke, ACI, with the licensee and a facility representative.
