3.125(a) REPEAT
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
*** The wood shelter box for the woodchuck is in disrepair and needs to be replaced. There are excessively chewed
areas that are affecting the structural integrity of the shelter box.
*** The wood enclosure frame in the raccoon enclosure is chewed around the door area and along the walls. The
chewed wood has exposed some metal screws which can be a hazard to the animals. These sections of wood are
in disrepair and should be replaced for the safety and containment of the animals.
*** The wood frame for the far skunk enclosure is separating from the side support beams. This section of the
enclosure need to be addressed to keep the enclosure in good repair for the safety of the skunk.
*** Several old metal feeders and metal coffee cans have been left in the enclosures for several years. Both items
are rusted which is staring to rust other areas of wire mesh the items are in contact with. If allowed to sit, rust will
start to affect the structural integrity of the enclosures.
3.131(a)
SANITATION.
There was an accumulation of feces in the raccoon enclosure, opossum enclosure and the woodchuck enclosure.
These enclosures need to have feces removed more often to prevent this accumulation, to reduce odor in the area
and for the health of the animals.
