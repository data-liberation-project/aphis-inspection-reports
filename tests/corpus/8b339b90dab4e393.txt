3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
A section of wire fencing in the pig enclosure had been repaired leaving a piece of heavy gauge sharp wire
protruding into the enclosure.
Animals may injure themselves on sharp objects. The fencing should be maintained in good repair in order to
protect and contain the animals.
Correct by December 30, 2013.
3.130
WATERING.
The water in the pig trough was dirty and contaminated with a heavy growth of algae.
Animals may be reluctant to drink contaminated water and decreased water consumption could lead to health
problems such as dehydration and intestinal obstruction. Contaminated water may harbor harmful microorganisms
that could cause illness in animals. All water receptacles should be kept clean and sanitary.
Correct by December 24, 2013.
3.131(c)SANITATION.
A cinder block, tin can, and some other, unidentifiable objects were present in the pig enclosure.
Debris and inappropriate objects in animal housing areas could harm the animals. Animal housing should be
inspected on a regular basis and any trash or debris should be promptly removed.
Correct by December 30, 2013.
An exit briefing was conducted with a facility representative.
