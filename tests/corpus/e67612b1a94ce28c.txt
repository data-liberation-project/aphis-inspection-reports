2.131(c)(1)
HANDLING OF ANIMALS.
The rope barriers outside two tiger enclosures (Rajah, Kaela) are too close to the primary enclosure fence. With
minimal effort, a person can reach to and through the primary enclosure fence and make contact with the animals
inside. Sufficient barriers between animals and the general viewing public must be in place to assure the safety of
animals and the public. Correct by or before January 24, 2017.
3.125(c)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The food preparation area, 2 walk-in freezers, 1 upright freezer, and 1 walk-in cooler have areas of build-up of dark
brown material, unclean racks and soiled floors. Unsanitary food storage can serve as a source of contamination
and disease for the animals. Supplies of food shall be stored in facilities. Correct by April 12, 2017.
3.129(b)
FEEDING.
The food receptacles (transport tubs and feed chutes) are coated with brown debris and dried blood. Unclean food
receptacles provide a breeding ground for pests and diseases, which can negatively impact the health of the
animals. Food receptacles shall be kept clean and sanitary at all times. Correct by January 24, 2017.
3.131(c)
SANITATION.
The facility has many areas (including but not limited to food preparation, workshop, veterinary care/treatment
center, pharmacy, quarantine building areas) that are cluttered with excessive amounts of debris and piles of items
not in use for the current care of the animals. Excessive amounts of disorganized items and debris throughout a
premises can serve as a breeding ground for pests and does not facilitate meeting the prescribed husbandry
practices set forth in this subpart. Correct by October 12, 2017.This inspection was conducted on 1-17-17 and the exit interview was conducted on 1/18/17 with the facility
representative.
