2.40(a)(1)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
A written Program of Veterinary Care (PVC) was not in place. In order to comply with this section of the Animal
Welfare Act (AWA) a written PVC must be in place. The PVC should identify an attending veterinarian, who under
formal arrangements should conduct regular scheduled visits to the premises where the animals are kept.
Correct by: January 4, 2015
2.75(b)(2)
RECORDS: DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS.
Acquisition and disposition records of animals on hand were not available for inspection. In order to comply with
this section of the Animal Welfare Act (AWA), the facility must keep and maintain the information required by
paragraph (b)(1) of this section of the AWA.
Correct by: January 4, 2015
3.75(c)(1)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The pine wood ladder located inside the capuchin monkey enclosure was worn and porous due to chewing by the
primate. The single capuchin monkey in the enclosure was observed chewing on the ladder, and wood chips were
also visualized in the enclosure's floor. Perches, shelves and other furniture-type fixtures or objects within the
enclosure must be removed or replaced when worn or soiled in order to provide safe activity and welfare of
nonhuman primates.
Correct by: January 4, 2015
3.81ENVIRONMENT ENHANCEMENT TO PROMOTE PSYCHOLOGICAL WELL-BEING.
An appropriate plan for environment enhancement adequate to promote the psychological well-being of nonhuman
primates was not available for inspection. The plan must be in accordance with the currently accepted professional
standards, and as directed by the attending veterinarian.
Correct by: January 4, 2015
