3.125

Facilities, general.
A section/ panel of the back fence of the camel enclosure does not appear to be secure as it is drooping downward and
the bottom portion is curling inward. There are 2 small areas of the enclosure where the fence is broken and sharp points
are present. Animal enclosures should be maintained in good repair and any areas of the fence that are not secured
should be fastened to prevent injury to the animal.
The outdoor housing facilities shall be structurally sound and shall be maintained in good repair to protect the animals
from injury and to contain the animals.
To be corrected by August 16, 2021
3.131(c)

Sanitation.
There is a mild to moderate amount of discarded materials and trash along the back fence of the camel enclosure. The
presence of trash can attract pests and flying insects and items such as paper, plastic and glass pieces can be harmful to
animals if ingested. Premises (buildings and grounds) shall be kept clean and in good repair in order to protect the
animals from injury and to facilitate the prescribed husbandry practices set forth in this subpart. Accumulations of trash


shall be placed in designated areas and cleared as necessary to protect the health of the animals.
To be corrected by August 1, 2021
