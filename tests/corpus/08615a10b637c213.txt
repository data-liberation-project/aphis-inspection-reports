3.127(b) CRITICAL
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
The young capybara was housed in an enclosure in the barn. The shelter had two heat lamps. The licensee
unplugged one lamp and the second lamp malfunctioned overnight. The capybara was found dead in the morning.
The licensee stated that he thought it died from the extreme cold.
Shelter appropriate to the local climatic conditions for the species concerned shall be provided for all animals kept
outdoors to afford them protection and to prevent death or discomfort.
Animals must be provided appropriate shelter from the elements to prevent death.
To be corrected by end of business today.
3.128 REPEAT
SPACE REQUIREMENTS.
The licensee has three wallaby's that are housed in vari-kennels. The wallaby's are allowed access to the house
twice daily.
Enclosures shall provide sufficient space to allow each animal to make normal postural and social adjustments with
adequate freedom of movement.
Build an enclosure or move the wallaby to an enclosure that allows the animal to make normal postural movements
and allows freedom of movement.
