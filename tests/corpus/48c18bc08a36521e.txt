3.1(b)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
(b) Condition and site.
The licensee is raising ducks and geese in enclosures of the 2nd sheltered facility. The 15 birds are sharing the
same air space and wash downs as the adult dogs housed in the facility. Additionally, the enclosures housing the
birds, the wash downs below them and all of the surfaces surrounding it are covered in bird waste. Birds are a
disease hazard as well as a cleanliness issue. The licensee shall remove the birds from the facility and keep all
other business separate from the dog facility. TO BE CORRECTED BY: 5/9/14
3.1(d)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
(d) Water and electric power. The water lines in the second sheltered housing facility are broken and the facility no longer has running water. The
licensee stated that the water pipes froze and broke in mid March 2014. The licensee is currently transporting water
into the facility to water and clean. The licensee shall ensure the housing facility can provide adequate running
potable water for the dogs' drinking needs, for cleaning, and for carrying out other husbandry requirements. TO BE
CORRECTED BY: 5/14/14
3.1(f)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
(f) Drainage and waste disposal.
There was a dead puppy in the 1st sheltered facility. The licensee stated that the puppy had passed away the
previous day and was still in an enclosure during the inspection at 2pm the following day. Dead animals are a
disease hazard for the animals in the facility.The licensee shall promptly remove dead animals in a manner that
minimizes contamination and disease risks. TO BE CORRECTED BY:5/8/14
3.4(b) DIRECT
OUTDOOR HOUSING FACILITIES.
(b) Shelter from the elements.
There were two adult, chocolate Labrador Retrievers in outdoor enclosures that did not have shelters or any source
of shade. The outdoor temperatures were 84 degrees at 2pm. Both of the dogs were panting heavily. Adequate
shelter is necessary to protect the animals from the elements. The licensee shall provide one or more shelter
structures that are accessible to each animal in each outdoor facility, and that are large enough to allow each
animal in the shelter structure to sit, stand, and lie in a normal manner, and to turn about freely. In addition to the
shelter structures, one or more separate outside areas of shade must be provided, large enough to contain all the
animals at one time and protect them from the direct rays of the sun. Shelters in outdoor facilities for dogs or cats
must contain a roof, four sides, and a floor.
* TO BE CORRECTED BY: 5/7/14 The licensee was directed to provide shade and shelters immediately following
the inspection( materials were on site.)
