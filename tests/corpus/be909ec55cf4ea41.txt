2.40(a)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
During the inspection it was identified that the attending veterinarian conducted the annual physical exams for each
the animals on 12 January 2016. During the veterinarians exam one dog was identified as having grade 3 dental
disease. The dog identified was an adult female Cava-Poo (microchip ending in #2729). The licensee cleaned this
dogs teeth in February and July of 2016. Upon examination of this dog, the teeth appeared to have very little
buildup and staining of the teeth. The gums were pink and firm and showed no sign of inflammation or bleeding
when touched. According to the program of veterinary care any dog identified as having grade 3 dental disease
shall be seen by a veterinarian for dental cleaning to appropriately remove the tartar buildup from the teeth. The
licensee did not contact the veterinarian for a dental cleaning, but rather cleaned the teeth himself. The Licensee
shall assure that the attending veterinarian has appropriate authority to ensure the provision of adequate veterinary
care.
Licensee must contact the veterinarian for the examination of the animal previously identified as having dental
disease. Documentation of the examination, diagnosis and treatment must be maintained and made available for
inspection.
Correct by close of business on November 21, 2016
Inspection and exit briefing conducted with the Licensee.
