2.33(a)(1)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE.
The licensee has an attending veterinarian and a written Program of Veterinary Care (PVC). However, the written
PVC has not be updated since April 29, 2008. The PVC states de-worming will occur spring and fall. No
medications were listed, no dosage, route of administration, frequency, or concentration listed on the PVC for this
treatment.
The licensee must consult with the attending veterinarian and develop a complete and accurate written program of
veterinary care that reflects the practices at this facility. The written program of veterinary care will ensure that there
is accurate communication between the applicant and the attending veterinarian and ensure that all routine
medications and vaccines provided to the animals by the applicant are done with the direction and knowledge of the
attending veterinarian.

To be corrected by: 15 February 2015
2.35(c)(1)
RECORDKEEPING REQUIREMENTS.
The licensee stated that 2 goats were taken out of production and there are no records ( APHIS official 7020 forms )
to verify the location of the goats. Specifically goat 14-G53 was taken out of production on 10-1-2014 and was no
longer on the premise. Also, goat 14-G51 was taken out of production on 10-1-2014 and was not on the premise.
Complete and accurate transaction records ( 7020's ) provide a source of information to facilitate the ability to trace
and verify the location of all animals after the transaction is completed. The licensee must maintain records ( 7020's
) which fully disclose all information concerning the transaction of all animals in their possession. To be corrected
from this date forward. Complete and accurate records must be kept in order to ensure that each animal's identity
can be known with certainty and to facilitate tracking/tracing of animals.
To be corrected by 1 February 2015.
3.129(a)FEEDING.
There was an open bag of medicated goat fed on a shelf in the barn. This bag of feed was stored on the shelf along
with wasp and hornet killer spray and another bottle of chemicals. These materials are toxic and may potentially
contaminate the medicated food for these animals, posing a risk to their health. Substances that are toxic to the
animals must not be stored in food storage and preparation areas, but must be stored in cabinets in the animal
areas. Also, open bags of feed must be stored in a sealed container.
To be corrected by : 1 February 2015
