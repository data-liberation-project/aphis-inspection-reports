3.1(a)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
* Protruding wires resulting in sharp points were observed in three enclosures. Six dogs are in contact with the
affected wires located in outdoor housing. Sharp points could potentially injure the animals. The licensee must
ensure that all enclosures are maintained in good repair at all times to protect the animals from injury. Correct by
11/4/14.
*********************************
* The top half of one plastic shelter structure is broken and jagged with a sharp edge. Two dogs are in contact with
the affected shelter structure located in outdoor housing. A sharp, jagged edge could potentially cause injury. The
licensee must ensure that all surfaces are kept in good repair and protect the animals from injury. Correct by
11/4/14.
*********************************
* Enclosure fencing was bowing along the bottom creating a two to three inch gap between the fencing and
cement flooring. Three dogs are in contact with the affected fencing located in outdoor housing. The potential for
injury, stress or discomfort exists if any dog gets caught in the gap area. The licensee must ensure all enclosures
protect the animals from injury and contain the dogs securely at all times. Correct by 11/4/14.
3.1(c)(2)
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
* The bottom edge of one metal outdoor shelter used by two dogs is heavily rusted and has flaked off and created
a jagged, sharp edge along the bottom of the shelter. Jagged edges and sharp points could potentially cause injury
to the dogs. The licensee must ensure that all surfaces that come in contact with the dogs be free of jagged, sharp
edges. Correct by 11/4/14.
3.1(f)HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
* Mixture of water, excreta and urine is collecting next to the cement runs in two different areas. Six dogs are in
contact with the affected area located in outdoor housing. Potential disease or health risks exist when water,
excreta and urine is not removed in a timely manner. In addition, unsanitary conditions exists when water, excreta
and urine continues to collect next to the cement runs. The licensee must provide for the regular and frequent
removal of water, other fluids and waste in a manner that minimizes contamination and disease risks. Correct by
11/4/14.
3.9(b)
FEEDING.
* Bird droppings, dust and cobwebs were observed on top of one wood food receptacle. The receptacle used by
two dogs are located in outdoor housing. Potential food contamination exists affecting the health and well-being of
the dogs. In addition, the surface area cannot be readily cleaned and sanitized. The licensee must ensure that all
food receptacles can be properly cleaned and sanitized at all times. Correct by 11/4/14.
3.11(b)(2)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
* The cement runs in two outdoor enclosures have an estimated 1/4 to 1/2 inch cracks in the cement floor. Six
dogs are in contact with the affected cement flooring. Gaps or cracks in cement flooring can allow accumulations
of dirt, debris, food waste, excreta, and other disease hazards. In addition, the surface area can not be adequately
cleaned and sanitized. The licensee must fill in all gaps with surface material that will not allow the accumulation of
any dirt, debris, food waste, excreta or any other disease hazards in the gap area. Correct by 11/4/14
**************************
* Three metal pipe enclosure gates have a mixture of excreta, grime and dried fecal residue on 15 to 20 percent
of the metal gate. There are three dogs in contact with the enclosure gates located in outdoor housing. Sanitization
of surfaces is necessary to prevent an accumulation of excreta and other disease hazards. It indicates an
unsanitary living condition exists that could lead to potential disease or health risks. The licensee must ensure that
all surfaces are cleaned and sanitized once every two weeks or more often if needed. Correct by 11/4/14.* The facility inspection done on 10/28/14 and exit briefing done on 10/30/14 were conducted with the licensee.
