2.40(a)(1)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
At the time of inspection, there was no written program of veterinary care available. A written program of veterinary
care ensures that animals at the facility are receiving adequate veterinary care when utilizing a part-time
veterinarian. A written program of veterinary care shall be maintained with the part-time veterinarian.
TO BE CORRECTED BY SEPTEMBER 17, 2019.
2.75(b)(1)
RECORDS: DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS.
At the time of inspection, there were no records of acquisition or disposition available at the facility. Records
containing the required information for animals acquired or disposed of are necessary in order to accurately track
animals being used in regulated activities to ensure their humane care and use. Make, keep, and maintain records
of acquisition and disposition of all animals that fully and correctly discloses the required information.
TO BE CORRECTED BY SEPTEMBER 17, 2019.
3.127(a)
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
There were no areas within the three pens housing the seven cattle, two camels, one bison, and seven pigs that
provided shade from direct sunlight. The bison appeared to be breathing heavily. Lack of shade can lead to
overheating and discomfort. Sufficient shade shall be be provided to allow all animals kept outdoors to protect
themselves from direct sunlight.
TO BE CORRECTED BY OCTOBER 15, 2019.
3.127(b)
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR. There were no shelters in the four pens containing the seven pigs, two camels, one bison, seven cattle, and
fourteen goats. Shelter from the elements is necessary to provide protection and comfort for the animal. The facility
shall provide natural or artificial shelter from inclement weather.
TO BE CORRECTED BY OCTOBER 15, 2019.
3.127(d)
FACILITIES, OUTDOOR.
The perimeter fence along the backside of the camel enclosure had a large hole at the base of the fence. The hole
is large enough for an unauthorized person or animal to enter the facility. The perimeter fence is necessary to
protect the animals by restricting other animals and unauthorized persons from going through it or under it. The
perimeter fence must be constructed and maintained so that it protects all animals within the facility.
TO BE CORRECTED BY SEPTEMBER 17, 2019.
