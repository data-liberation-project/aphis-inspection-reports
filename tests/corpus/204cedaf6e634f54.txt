3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The ferret enclosure showed signs of excessive rust especially in the lower right hand corner. The rust appeared
pitted and paint was flaking off. The bars of the enclosure were mobile and integrity of the enclosure was
compromised. The primary enclosures of the facility must be kept in good repair to prevent injury to the animals and
ensure containment. The facility must take appropriate measures to repair the enclosure.
Correct by Feburary 8th 2018.
3.131(d)
SANITATION.
There is a pest control issue in the lion, rhinoceros and wallaby enclosures. Excessive amounts of mouse droppings
were noted in the corners of the lion back holding area. Mice can carry a variety of diseases and parasites that can
effect the health of the lions. The rhinoceros and wallaby enclosures had a large number of avian pests and avian
excreta. Avian pests, including house sparrows and pigeons, can carry a variety of diseases and parasites;
measures must be taken to minimize their entry into the enclosures. The facility must create a safe and effective
pest control program to ensure optimal animal health.
Correct by February 15th, 2018.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the facility representative.
