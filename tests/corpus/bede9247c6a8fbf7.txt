3.56(a)(3)

Sanitation.
The worm beds over the cages for the pregnant does, some of which have litters, are full to the top with fecal matter.
Some fecal matter has also spilled into the aisle. A greyish film is on top of the fecal matter in some areas of the bins.

The worm bins have not been maintained at a frequency to prevent excessive build-up of material. Worm beds should be
maintained to be as sanitary as possible. The facility worm bins must kept under sanitary conditions to prevent disease
and to ensure a healthy environment for the rabbits. Correct by February 6, 2022

This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the licensee.
