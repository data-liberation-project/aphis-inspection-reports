2.50(a)
TIME AND METHOD OF IDENTIFICATION.
In the whelping building there are raised wire enclosures which contained puppies. During the inspection one of
the enclosures contained two weaned puppies. The weaned puppies did not have any form of identification. All
class ``A'' dealers (breeders) shall identify all live dogs on the premises with an approved form of identification at all
times to insure traceability of all animals.
Affects two puppies. Correct by July 30, 2015
3.2(d)
INDOOR HOUSING FACILITIES.
The dealer has added a indoor whelping building to the kennel facility. The ceiling of the building is constructed
from unpainted / unsealed pressed wood panels. The ceiling needs to be treated in a manner to insure it is
impervious to moisture. Interior surfaces including all floors, walls and ceiling of indoor housing facilities, and any
other surfaces in contact with the animals, must be impervious to moisture.
Affects six adults and twenty-six puppies. Correct by August 28, 2015
3.4(c) REPEAT
OUTDOOR HOUSING FACILITIES.
The kennel facility consists of two outdoor housing areas. In the outdoor structure there are a total of twelve
primary enclosures. The interior wooden surfaces of three enclosures are chewed and worn. The chewed / worn
wooden surfaces need to be repaired or replaced and maintained on a regular basis. Surfaces of outdoor housing
facilities--including houses, dens, etc.--that cannot be readily cleaned and sanitized, must be replaced when worn or
soiled to facilitate husbandry pratices, prevent the spread of disease and protect the animals from injury.
Affects seven adults.3.6(a)(2)(1)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
In the outdoor housing structure there are six primary enclosures which have protruding nails with sharp edges that
need to be removed to protect the contained animals from injury. Primary enclosures must be constructed and
maintained so that they have no sharp points or edges that could injure the dogs.
Affects fourteen adults. Correct by July 30, 2015
3.11(c)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
The dealer has added a indoor whelping building to the kennel facility. The interior surfaces of the whelping building
need to be cleaned to remove the accumulation of old waste material under the raised wire waste catch structures.
The surfaces through-out the room need to be cleaned to remove waste material, old chew bones, hair, dirt, dead
bugs, spider webs and bags of trash. Premises where housing facilities are located, including buildings and
surrounding grounds, must be kept clean and in good repair to protect the animals from injury, to facilitate the
husbandry practices and to reduce or eliminate breeding and living areas for rodents and other pests and vermin.
Affects six adults and twenty-six puppies. Correct by July 31, 2015
