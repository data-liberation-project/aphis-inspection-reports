3.56(b)(1)
SANITATION.
While inspecting the rabbit enclosures, the owner stated that the rabbit feeders were sanitized once every two
weeks but the primary enclosures themselves were sanitized quarterly.
Primary enclosures for rabbits need to be sanitized at least once every thirty days. Failure to do so can lead to
increased spread of disease. Correct by sanitizing the enclosures at least once every thirty days by using one of
the following methods:
- Washing with hot water (180 degrees) AND soap or detergent
- Washing all soiled surfaces with a detergent solution followed by a safe and effective disinfectant
- Cleaning all soiled surfaces with live steam or flame
Correct by October 31, 2019.
3.56(c)
SANITATION.
On the windows and rafters above the enclosures of the four rabbits, there is an excessive accumulation of dust and

Premises, including building and grounds, need to be kept clean and in good repair. Failure to do so can negatively
impact the rabbits by potentially contaminating their feed or inhibiting ventilation. Correct by cleaning and
maintaining the areas surrounding the rabbit hutches. Correct by October 9, 2019.
