2.40(b)(1)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
The facility’s veterinary clinic contained two “go kits” for emergency/field use located in the pharmacy. Both of these
boxes contained several expired medications to include: 9 bottles of midazolam, 4 bottles of ketamine, 4 bottles of
diazepam, 3 bottles of yohimbine, 3 bottles of detomidine, 2 bottles of acepromazine, 2 bottles of xylazine, 2 bottles
of flumazenil, 1 bottle of tolazoline, 1 bottle of atipamezole, 1 bottle of butorphanol and 1 bottle of Euthanasia
Solution. Expired medication may not be effective after their expiration date and may not be used on regulated
animals. This facility must ensure that all medications are within date, especially those that will be utilized in an
emergency situation. In addition, all expired medications must be properly disposed of on or before their expiration
date.
Correct by: 8/19/2016
During this inspection, inspectors followed up on the citation 3.125 (a) that was written on the report dated
6/27/2016. The facility has made corrective measures to the juvenile black bear enclosure. They have added a
portion of non-climbable fence and two additional hot wire strands to the enclosure’s primary fence. The enclosure
contained two juvenile bears at the time of this inspection, and according to one of the bear keepers, the bears have
shown no interest in attempting to climb the fence.
