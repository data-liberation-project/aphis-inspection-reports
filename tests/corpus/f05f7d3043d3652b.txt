2.40(b)(2) DIRECT REPEAT
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
(b)(2)
There were two dogs at the facility that required veterinary care.
*An adult male Papillion ID # 97 called "Prince" had a protruding right eye with a pink center. The area surrounding
the eye was wet and the tissue on the bottom of his eye lid was swollen with an abnormal section of cream
tissue.The licensee stated that she had first seen the issue the previous week and applied ointment to the dog's
eye. The licensee also stated that the condition had worsened since the initial treatment.
** An adult, female Yorkshire Terrier ID # 35 called "Floats" had complete hair loss in sections across her neck,
shoulders, and back. Her skin was dry and flaky. The licensee stated that it had been an ongoing condition for a
year and that she had treated the dog with "Perimite" dip in August. The licensee stated that the dog's condition
improved after treatment but had recently reoccurred.
Abnormal conditions such as those listed above indicate health concerns which may be painful for the animals.
The licensee shall provide adequate and timely veterinary care for the animals at the facility. The licensee shall
obtain a written diagnosis and treatment plan for these animals and provide care as prescribed. Treatments should
be recorded and all records should be available during the next inspection. TO BE CORRECTED BY THE CLOSE
OF BUSINESS 9/19/2014
3.1(c)(3) REPEAT
HOUSING FACILITIES, GENERAL.
(c) Surfaces(3)
*The indoor portion of the sheltered building housing Bulldogs had a buildup of grime, hair and trash. One of the
enclosures where an adult female English Bulldog had been housed up until that morning (moved due whelping)had sections of soiled paper towel scattered across the floor. The enclosures had thick brown grime and hair along
their edges and corners.
** There were multiple dog doors throughout all areas of the facility that had a build-up of brown grime on their
surface.
Adequate cleaning is an important aspect of animal husbandry and helps to prevent disease, odor, pests. Hard
surfaces with which the dogs come in contact must be spot-cleaned daily and sanitized in accordance with Sec.
3.11(b) of this subpart to prevent accumulation of excreta and reduce disease hazards.
3.9(b)
FEEDING.
There were multiple self-feeders through all areas of the facility that were dirty and had molded, caked feed in them.
Molded feed could make the dogs ill or may make the food unpalatable. The licensee shall ensure that feeders are
kept clean and are sanitized in accordance with Sec. 3.11(b) of this subpart. Measures must be taken to ensure that
there is no molding, deterioration, and caking of feed. TO BE CORRECTED BY: 9/22/2014
3.11(a) REPEAT
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
(a) Cleaning of primary enclosures.
The outdoor portion of the sheltered building housing adult dogs had an accumulation of feces in the animal areas.
Some of the feces had dried. When ask, the licensee stated that area had not been cleaned for two days. Adequate
cleaning is a crucial aspect of animal husbandry and helps to reduce disease hazards, odors and pests. The
licensee shall remove excreta from the primary enclosures daily and from under primary enclosures as often as
necessary.
3.11(d)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
There was evidence of rodents in the" Bull Dog" building. Rodents can spread disease. The licensee shall
incorporate an effective pest control method to remove rodents from the facility. TO BE CORRECTED BY:
