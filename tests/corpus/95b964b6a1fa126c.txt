2.40(b)(2)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
In the outdoor portion of the kennel facility there is one enclosures which contains two animals. One of animals is a
male chocolate merle Cocker Spaniel born June 27, 2015, chip # 7E10030124, who needs to be evaluated by the
attending veterinarian. During the inspection the animal had green matter surrounding the right eye. The dealers
stated they were not aware of the animal's condition. The animal must be evaluated by the attending veterinarian.
The dealer must maintain written documentation of the evaluation and all treatment.
Affects one adult. Correct by October 30, 2015
3.4(c) REPEAT
OUTDOOR HOUSING FACILITIES.
On the eastern exterior side of the outdoor kennel structure the enclosures have concrete floor surfaces. Three of
the outdoor enclosure portions have concrete floor surfaces which are broken leaving holes in the floor surfaces.
During the inspection the holes contained fluids. The floor surfaces need to be repaired or replaced to insure the all
surfaces can be properly cleaned and sanitized and do not cause injury to the contained animals.
Affects five adults.
3.6(a)(2)(1) REPEAT
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
During this inspection there were three primary enclosures in the outdoor housing structure which have protruding
nails with sharp edges that need to be removed to protect the contained animals from injury. Primary enclosures
must be constructed and maintained so that they do not have sharp points or edges that could injure the dogs.
Affects nine adults.
