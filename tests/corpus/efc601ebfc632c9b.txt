3.2(b)

Indoor housing facilities.
At the time of inspection, two guinea pigs were being exhibited outdoors. The temperature inside their enclosure was
recorded with a kestrel between 93.9 and 94.0 degrees Fahrenheit. Guinea pigs do not tolerate temperature extremes
well and are susceptible to heat stroke in high temperatures. Housing of guinea pigs outdoors is restricted under the
Animal Welfare Act unless facilities are located in an appropriate climate for outdoor housing and prior approval has been
obtained from the Deputy Administrator.
Corrected at the time of inspection.
3.52(a)

Facilities, outdoor.
At the time of inspection, the temperature inside the outdoor rabbit enclosure was recorded by kestrel to be between 93.9
and 94.0 degrees Fahrenheit. Rabbits are sensitive to excessive heat and prone to heat stress. Heat stress is detrimental
to the health and welfare of animals. Licensees shall provide artificial cooling when atmospheric temperature exceeds 90
degrees Fahrenheit.
Corrected at the time of inspection.
