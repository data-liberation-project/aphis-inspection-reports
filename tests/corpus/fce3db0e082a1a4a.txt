2.40(b)
ATTENDING VETERINARIAN AND ADEQUATE VETERINARY CARE (DEALERS AND EXHIBITORS).
The dealer recently aquired a seven month old female Poodle, chip # 0A0217001. The animal needs to be
evaluated by the attending veterinarian. The animal was observed keeping its back legs tucked under and not fully
extending them. The dealer stated the animal had this conditiion when it was purchased and had not been
evaluated by the attending veterinarian. The animal must be evaluated and the dealer must maintain written
documentation of the evaluation and all treatment.

3.2
INDOOR HOUSING FACILITIES.
During the inspection the whelping room of the kennel facility was dark upon entry. The room had multiple over
head lights but they were not operational. In the house portions of the enclosures a heat type light had to be moved
from enclosure to enclosure in order to see the puppies.The dealer must provide additional lighting for the animals.
Indoor housing facilities for dogs must be lighted well enough to permit routine inspections and cleaning of the
facility, and observation of the dogs to protect their health and welfare.
Affects twenty-six adults and forty-two puppies. Correct by August 23, 2014
3.6(a)(2)(1)
PRIMARY ENCLOSURES.
The kennel facility consists of one indoor housing building and two sheltered buildings. On the exterior portion of
the sheltered building enclosures there are metal doors which the animals enter and exit the building within their
enclosures. On the western exterior side of building E there are eight metal doors which have worn / chewed /sharp
edges which could injure the animals. Primary enclosures must be constructed and maintained so that they do not
have sharp points or edges that could injure the dogs. The dealer must repair or replace the doors to protect the
animals from injury.Affects twelve adults. Correct by August 6, 2014
3.11(a)
CLEANING, SANITIZATION, HOUSEKEEPING, AND PEST CONTROL.
The kennel facility consists of one indoor housing area and two sheltered buildings. On the exterior western side of
building D there are a total of sixteen outdoor enclosure portions. The floor surface for the exterior enclosure
portions is concrete. Most of the enclosures contained multiple dogs. During the inspection there was more than
one days accumulation of waste material in the enclosures. During the inspection the animals were observed trying
to avoid contact with the excrete. Excreta must be removed from primary enclosures daily or as often as necessary
to prevent an excessive accumulation of feces, to prevent soiling of the dogs contained in the primary enclosures,
and to reduce disease hazards, insects, pests and odors.
Affects thirty-six adults Correct by July 24, 2014
The inspection and exit briefing was conducted with the dealers.
