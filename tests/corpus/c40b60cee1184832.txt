Date: 28-APR-2022

3.125(a)

Facilities, general.
In several places, electrical power cords were exposed and unprotected within animal enclosures. In the small clawed
otter (3) indoor pool, a piece of hose placed over the cord for protection had cracked, exposing about a foot of cord. An
exposed cord was also present in the Asian Leopard (1) enclosure.
In several enclosures, chewed wood exposed screws or nails which could injure the animals. Katia, an Asian Leopard
and Tar, the tiger had screws or nails exposed within their enclosures.
Animals could chew on exposed cords and receive an electrical shock and exposed screws/nails could injure or harm the
animals.
All housing facilities must be maintained in good repair to protect the animals from injury.
To be corrected by May 15, 2022.

This inspection and exit interview were conducted with facility representative.
