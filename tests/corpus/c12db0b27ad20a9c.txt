3.31(b)
SANITATION.
Housekeeping. There is a buildup of dust, dirt, cobwebs, clutter, and debris on window ledges, walls, ceiling,
heaters, and the floor of the animal room. Animal facilities must be kept clean to ensure good quality of the air for
the animals and to prevent unnecessary disease risks. Ineffective cleaning can expose the animals to disease
hazards and health problems. Correct by ensuring the premises are kept clean, including: removing the buildup of
dust, dirt, cobwebs, clutter, and debris; routine dusting off and cleaning surfaces; and maintaining a level of general
cleanliness to support good animal husbandry and health. Correct by March 30, 2020.
This inspection and exit interview were conducted with the owner.
