3.125(a)
FACILITIES, GENERAL.
The fencing for the sheep and goat outdoor enclosure on the north side of the barn is loose and broken in places.
There is barbed wire along the top of a portion of the fence which is very loose and not firmly attached on each end.
Lining another portion of the fence is chicken wire which has become bent and broken with holes and protruding
wires.
The stall for the sheep and goats has a board near one of the water buckets which has become loose at one end.
Several long nail ends are exposed to the animals. There are a few other exposed nail and screw ends at various
spots in the stall, randomly located.
Loose wire and sharp points pose an injury risk for the animals. Enclosures shall be structurally sound and shall be
maintained in good repair to protect the animals from injury.
